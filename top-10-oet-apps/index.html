Why Nurses Need Specialised OET Apps

The Occupational English Test (OET) is the preferred English language test for healthcare professionals seeking registration in the UK, Ireland, Australia, and New Zealand. Unlike IELTS, OET uses real healthcare scenarios, making specialised preparation essential. The Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) accepts OET with a Grade B (350+) in all four sub-tests. A dedicated OET app with nursing-specific content can be the difference between passing first time and costly resits.

OET Sub-Test Breakdown for Nurses

Understanding what each sub-test requires helps you choose the right app.

Listening (45 min)

Part A: Consultation extracts (note-completion). Part B: Short workplace extracts (multiple choice). Part C: Presentation extracts (multiple choice). Nurses must understand various healthcare accents.

Reading (60 min)

Part A: Expeditious reading (matching, short answer). Part B: Careful reading (multiple choice, matching). Part C: Careful reading (multiple choice, gapped text). Hospital policies and nursing guidelines feature heavily.

Writing (45 min)

One task: a referral letter (180-200 words) based on patient case notes. Nurses must extract relevant information, organise it logically, and write with appropriate language. This is where most nurses struggle.

Speaking (20 min)

Two role-plays: you are the nurse, the interlocutor plays a patient or relative. Scenarios include explaining conditions, reassuring anxious patients, and obtaining consent. Warm-up conversation precedes the role-plays.

Frequently Asked Questions

What OET grade do nurses need for UK NMC registration?

Nurses need at least Grade B (350 or above) in all four OET sub-tests, or Grade B in Reading, Listening, and Speaking with Grade C+ in Writing. However, Grade B in all four is strongly recommended for a smoother registration process.

Is OET easier than IELTS for nurses?

Most nurses find OET easier because the content is healthcare-related and uses familiar professional vocabulary. The writing task (a referral letter) is more practical than IELTS essays. However, OET still requires strong English skills and dedicated preparation.

How long does OET preparation take with an app?

Most nurses need 4-8 weeks of daily practice (30-60 minutes) to reach Grade B. Nurses with intermediate English may need 10-12 weeks. Apps with AI feedback like Anglotec can reduce preparation time by identifying weaknesses faster.

Can I use IELTS apps to prepare for OET?

IELTS apps can help with general English improvement but are not sufficient for OET preparation. OET requires profession-specific vocabulary, referral letter writing skills, and healthcare role-play practice. Use a dedicated OET app for best results.

How much do OET apps cost?

OET apps range from free (official samples) to 50-100 GBP/month for premium platforms. Anglotec offers a substantial free tier with premium features at competitive pricing. Consider the cost versus the 370 GBP OET retest fee.

Does the NMC prefer IELTS or OET?

The NMC accepts both equally. Many nurses prefer OET because the medical context feels more natural. The choice depends on your strengths: OET suits nurses comfortable with healthcare scenarios; IELTS may suit those stronger in general academic English.
