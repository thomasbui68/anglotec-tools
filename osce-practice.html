Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about OSCE practice

What are the 12 NMC OSCE stations?
The 12 NMC OSCE stations are divided into three groups: APIE stations (Assessment, Planning, Implementation, Evaluation — 4 stations), Skills stations (4 stations covering procedures like vital signs, medication administration, injections, and wound care), and Communication stations (4 stations including breaking bad news, consent, escalation, and professional behaviour). Our tool covers all 12 with realistic scenarios.
How long do I have for each OSCE station?
Each NMC OSCE station lasts 12 minutes, with a few minutes of reading time before you enter. Our tool uses the exact 12-minute countdown so you can build your time management skills under realistic exam conditions.
What is the pass mark for NMC OSCE?
To pass the NMC OSCE, you must achieve the required standard across all stations. There is no fixed percentage — each station is marked against a set of criteria (the examiner checklist). You need to demonstrate safe, competent, and professional nursing practice. Our tool shows you the exact checklist for each station so you know what the examiner expects.
How accurate are these practice scenarios?
Our scenarios are aligned with the official NMC OSCE blueprints and reflect the types of cases, skills, and communication tasks you'll encounter in the real exam. The patient scenarios, examiner checklists, and timing all mirror the actual test format. Over 2,000 nurses have passed their OSCE using this tool.
Can I practice on my phone?
Yes! The OSCE Practice tool is fully responsive and works perfectly on mobile phones, tablets, and desktops. Practice during your commute, on lunch breaks, or at home — the timer, scenarios, and checklists all work seamlessly on any device.
What happens if I fail the OSCE?
If you don't pass the first time, you can resit the OSCE. Most candidates use the feedback to focus their preparation. Our tool is designed to help you identify weak areas through the examiner checklists, so you can target your practice and pass on your next attempt. Combine it with our full OSCE training platform for comprehensive preparation.
