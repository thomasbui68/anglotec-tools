Dentistry in the United Kingdom

The UK offers excellent opportunities for international dentists, with high demand across both NHS and private sectors. The dental profession is regulated by the General Dental Council (GDC), and overseas-qualified dentists must pass the Overseas Registration Exam (ORE) to practise independently. The ORE is challenging but achievable with proper preparation. Once registered, dentists enjoy competitive salaries, the option to work in NHS practice, private practice, or both, and clear pathways to specialist training and consultancy.

The ORE Registration Pathway

1

GDC Registration Requirements

Overseas-qualified dentists must register with the General Dental Council (GDC) to practise in the UK. The primary route for dentists from outside the EEA is the Overseas Registration Examination (ORE).

Eligibility for ORE

  • Dental degree recognised by the GDC (from an approved university)
  • English language: IELTS Band 7.0 overall (minimum 6.5 in each) OR OET Grade B
  • Certificate of Good Standing from your home dental regulator
  • Health and character declarations
  • Indemnity insurance (after registration)

Important

Graduates from certain countries with reciprocal agreements may have alternative registration pathways. Check the GDC website for the most current list.

2

ORE Part 1: Written Examination

ORE Part 1 is a written exam consisting of two papers, testing your theoretical knowledge across all aspects of dentistry. It is held at the King's College London examination centre.

Paper A: General Dental Sciences (3 hours)

120 multiple-choice questions covering: applied dental sciences (anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, pathology, microbiology), human disease (medical conditions relevant to dentistry), and oral biology.

Paper B: Clinical Dentistry (3 hours)

120 multiple-choice questions covering: restorative dentistry, periodontology, endodontics, prosthodontics, paediatric dentistry, orthodontics, oral surgery, oral medicine, and dental public health.

Key Facts

  • Total: 240 MCQs over two papers
  • Pass mark: approximately 50-55% (varies by exam)
  • Cost: approximately GBP 1,500
  • Held multiple times per year
  • Pass rate: approximately 40-50%

Preparation: Allow 4-6 months of intensive study. Focus on UK-specific guidelines, BNF dental prescribing, and GDC standards. Join ORE study groups and consider preparatory courses.

3

ORE Part 2: Clinical Examination

ORE Part 2 is a practical clinical examination held at the King's College London Dental Institute. It tests your operative dental skills and clinical judgment on simulated patients, manikins, and models.

Part 2 Components

Operative Dentistry on Manikins: Preparations, restorations, endodontic access on Phantom Head. OSCE Stations: Clinical scenario discussions, radiograph interpretation, treatment planning. Practical Exercises: Crown preparations, cavity preparations, rubber dam application. Medical Emergencies: CPR and basic life support assessment.

Key Facts

  • 2-day practical examination
  • Cost: approximately GBP 3,000
  • Held at King's College London
  • Pass rate: approximately 50-60%
  • Limited attempts allowed (check current GDC rules)

Preparation Investment

Most candidates spend GBP 5,000-10,000 on ORE preparation including courses, materials, travel, and exam fees. Budget accordingly and prepare thoroughly to avoid costly resits.

4

Foundation Training (DFT) for Overseas Dentists

After passing the ORE, you can apply for Dental Foundation Training (DFT), a mandatory 1-year training programme for all new dentists entering NHS practice in the UK. It provides structured support and supervision.

DFT Programme Overview

  • 1-year salaried training post
  • Rotations through general dental practice
  • Educational supervisor support
  • Weekly study days
  • ePortfolio and assessments
  • Salary: approximately GBP 35,000-40,000

Note: Competition for DFT places is high for overseas dentists. Some dentists enter as performers (salaried associates) instead, though DFT is strongly recommended for adapting to UK practice.

5

NHS vs Private Practice in the UK

UK dentists can work in NHS practice, private practice, or a combination (mixed practice). Understanding the differences is crucial for your career planning.

NHS Dentistry

  • UDAs (Units of Dental Activity) payment system
  • Fixed NHS fee bands for patients
  • Guaranteed patient base through NHS lists
  • NHS pension and employment benefits
  • Regulated by NHS England / equivalents
  • Income typically 50,000-85,000 GBP

Private Dentistry

  • Fee-for-service or subscription models
  • Higher fees for complex treatments
  • Cosmetic dentistry opportunities
  • Greater clinical autonomy
  • Marketing and business development needed
  • Income varies: 60,000-150,000+ GBP
6

Specialist Training and Career Progression

After gaining experience in general practice, you can pursue specialist training in one of the 13 GDC-recognised dental specialties. Specialist dentists command higher fees and prestige.

GDC-Recognised Specialties

  • Orthodontics (3-year training)
  • Endodontics (3-year training)
  • Periodontics (3-year training)
  • Prosthodontics (3-year training)
  • Oral Surgery (5-year training)
  • Restorative Dentistry (5-year training)
  • Paediatric Dentistry (3-year training)
  • Oral Medicine (3-year training)
  • Dental Public Health (3-year training)
  • Special Care Dentistry (3-year training)
  • Oral Microbiology, Oral Pathology, Dental and Maxillofacial Radiology

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to become a registered dentist in the UK?

From starting your GDC application to full registration typically takes 2-3 years. This includes English test preparation, ORE Part 1 and Part 2 preparation and exams, and processing times.

How much does the ORE cost in total?

The total cost for ORE registration, both exam parts, preparation courses, materials, and travel to London typically ranges from GBP 8,000 to 15,000. Proper preparation to pass first time saves significant money.

Can I work as a dental nurse while preparing for the ORE?

Yes, many overseas dentists work as dental nurses or dental technicians while preparing for the ORE. This provides income, improves your English, and gives you valuable UK dental practice exposure.

Do I need IELTS if I have OET?

The GDC accepts both IELTS (Band 7.0 overall, minimum 6.5 each) and OET (Grade B in all sub-tests). Choose the test that suits you best.

Can I practise privately without NHS registration?

No, you must be GDC-registered to practise any dentistry in the UK, whether NHS or private. GDC registration is mandatory for all dental practice.
