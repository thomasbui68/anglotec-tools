Overview of NMC Registration Changes 2026

The Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) has introduced several important changes to its registration process for international nurses in 2026. These updates aim to streamline the pathway for overseas nurses seeking to work in the UK, while maintaining the high standards of patient safety and professional competence that underpin UK nursing practice.

The UK continues to rely heavily on internationally educated nurses to staff its National Health Service and private healthcare sector. With an estimated 40,000 nursing vacancies across the UK, international recruitment remains a priority. The NMC's 2026 changes reflect this need by reducing bureaucratic barriers, improving processing times, and providing clearer guidance for applicants.

Key changes for 2026 include updated English language requirements with more flexible evidence options, a redesigned online application portal, changes to the Objective Structured Clinical Examination (OSCE) for nurses, and updated qualification assessment procedures. This guide covers each change in detail, helping you navigate the registration process successfully.

English Language Requirements Update 2026

The NMC's English language requirements have been updated for 2026 to provide more flexibility while ensuring all registered nurses can communicate effectively in UK healthcare settings.

Accepted English Tests

The NMC continues to accept IELTS (Academic) and OET for nursing in 2026. The required scores remain: IELTS Academic overall 7.0 with at least 7.0 in Listening, Reading, and Speaking, and 6.5 in Writing; or OET with at least Grade C+ in Listening, Reading, and Speaking, and Grade C+ in Writing. Both tests must have been taken within the last two years at the time of application.

New: Combined English Test Evidence

A significant 2026 change is the introduction of a combined test evidence option. Candidates can now combine their two most recent test sittings to meet the requirements, provided both tests are within the validity period and the candidate achieved the required scores across the two sittings. This change provides flexibility for candidates who may narrowly miss one component in a single sitting.

English Majority-Speaking Country Exemption

Nurses who completed their nursing education in an English majority-speaking country (defined by the NMC as the UK, Ireland, USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, or Jamaica) may be exempt from English language testing. In 2026, the NMC has clarified that evidence of both nursing education and registration in an exempt country can support an exemption application.

Additional English Evidence

For 2026, the NMC has expanded the types of additional English evidence that can be considered alongside test scores. This includes evidence of nursing practice in English-speaking healthcare settings, additional English language qualifications, and employer references confirming English language competence. This evidence can be submitted as supporting documentation but does not replace the requirement for an accepted English test score.

Streamlined NMC Application Process 2026

The NMC has completely redesigned its online application portal for 2026, creating a more intuitive and efficient experience for international applicants.

New Online Application Portal

The redesigned NMC portal features a step-by-step application wizard that guides candidates through each requirement. The system now provides real-time validation of uploaded documents, reducing delays caused by incomplete submissions. Applicants can track their application status in real-time and receive automated notifications at each processing stage.

Document Upload Improvements

Document submission has been simplified with an improved upload system that accepts a wider range of file formats and provides instant feedback on document quality. The system includes built-in image enhancement for scanned documents and automatic translation verification for non-English documents. These improvements significantly reduce the common issue of documents being rejected for technical reasons.

Processing Time Updates

The NMC has committed to faster processing times for 2026, with a target of 30 working days for qualification assessment once all documents are received. Priority processing is available for applicants with confirmed job offers from NHS trusts, with a target of 15 working days. These processing time commitments represent a significant improvement from previous years.

Candidate Support Enhancements

The NMC has expanded its candidate support services, introducing a dedicated international applicant helpline with multilingual support. A new live chat function on the NMC website provides instant answers to common questions. Monthly webinars guide applicants through the registration process, with recordings available on demand.

OSCE Changes for NMC Registration 2026

The Objective Structured Clinical Examination (OSCE) is the practical assessment that all internationally educated nurses must pass to join the NMC register. Several updates have been introduced for 2026.

Updated OSCE Scenarios

The OSCE scenario bank has been refreshed for 2026 to reflect current UK nursing practice priorities. New scenarios address topics including medication administration safety, safeguarding vulnerable adults, mental health crisis assessment, and wound care management. The scenarios test not only clinical skills but also communication, professionalism, and decision-making abilities.

OSCE Station Restructure

The OSCE structure has been slightly restructured for 2026. The exam continues to assess skills across APIE (Assessment, Planning, Implementation, Evaluation) stations, but the distribution of marks has been adjusted to place greater emphasis on patient safety and communication. There are now 10 stations in total, with two linked stations that assess the candidate's ability to manage a patient scenario over time.

New Test Centres

Two new OSCE test centres have opened in 2026, increasing capacity and reducing waiting times. The new centres are located in Manchester and Glasgow, joining existing centres in London, Oxford, and Northampton. This expanded capacity means candidates can typically book their OSCE within 4-6 weeks of receiving their eligibility letter.

OSCE Preparation Resources

The NMC has published updated OSCE preparation guidance for 2026, including detailed station descriptors, marking criteria, and sample scenarios. NHS employers are required to provide structured OSCE preparation programmes for their international nurse recruits, with a minimum of two weeks of supervised practice recommended before sitting the exam.

Qualification Assessment Updates 2026

The NMC's assessment of international nursing qualifications has been updated for 2026, with changes designed to streamline the process for nurses from recognised education systems.

Streamlined Assessment for Priority Countries

Nurses trained in countries with nursing education systems closely aligned to UK standards now benefit from streamlined qualification assessment. The list of priority countries has been expanded for 2026 to include India, the Philippines, Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, and South Africa, alongside Australia, Canada, New Zealand, and the USA. Nurses from priority countries typically receive their qualification assessment within 15 working days.

Transitional Programme Requirements

For nurses whose qualifications require additional preparation, the NMC has updated its guidance on transitional programmes. These programmes, typically provided by UK universities, help nurses adapt their knowledge and skills to UK practice standards. The NMC has approved five new transitional programmes for 2026, increasing options across the UK.

Experience Requirements for NMC 2026

The NMC has updated its experience requirements for international applicants in 2026.

Minimum Practice Experience

The NMC requires evidence of at least 12 months of post-registration nursing practice. For 2026, this requirement has been clarified to specify that the experience must be in a registered nursing role with direct patient care responsibilities. Experience in administrative, educational, or research roles (without concurrent clinical practice) does not meet this requirement.

Recent Practice Requirement

In addition to total experience, the NMC requires evidence of recent nursing practice. For 2026, applicants must demonstrate at least 450 hours of nursing practice in the last three years. This requirement ensures that registered nurses are current in their clinical skills and knowledge.

NMC Registration Fees 2026

The NMC registration fees have been updated for 2026. International applicants should budget for the following costs:

  • Application fee: £153 (non-refundable, payable on submission)
  • Registration fee: £120 (payable after successful application)
  • OSCE fee: £794 (payable when booking the OSCE)
  • OSCE resit fee: £397 (for each resit attempt)

Some NHS employers cover all or part of these costs as part of their international recruitment packages. Candidates should clarify fee arrangements with their employer before beginning the application process.

Changes for EEA/EU Nurses in 2026

Following Brexit, EEA and EU nurses are now subject to the same registration requirements as other international nurses. The 2026 updates include specific provisions for this group.

Automated Recognition Ended

The automatic recognition of EEA nursing qualifications ended with Brexit. EU nurses must now have their qualifications individually assessed by the NMC, the same process followed by nurses from other countries. However, nurses who began but had not completed the registration process under the old system have been granted transitional arrangements.

Support for EU Applicants

The NMC has introduced dedicated support for EU applicants, including translated guidance documents in major European languages and a dedicated EU applicant support team. These resources help EU nurses navigate the new registration requirements effectively.

NMC Revalidation Requirements

Once registered, all NMC registrants must complete revalidation every three years. International nurses should understand these requirements as part of their long-term career planning in the UK.

Revalidation requirements include 450 practice hours, 35 hours of continuing professional development (CPD), 5 pieces of practice-related feedback, 5 written reflective accounts, reflective discussion, professional indemnity evidence, and confirmation of good health and character. NHS employers typically provide structured support for revalidation.

Common NMC Application Issues & Solutions

Many international nurses encounter similar challenges during the NMC registration process. Understanding these common issues helps you avoid delays.

Document Issues

Incomplete Documentation

The most common cause of delays is incomplete or unclear documentation. Ensure all documents are certified copies, translated by approved translators if necessary, and clearly legible. Use the NMC document checklist to verify completeness before submission.

English Test

English Score Shortfalls

Many candidates narrowly miss the required scores in one sub-test. With the 2026 combined evidence option, you may be able to combine two sittings. Alternatively, consider switching from IELTS to OET or vice versa based on your strengths.

Verification

Verification Delays

The NMC must verify your registration and qualifications with your home country regulator. These verifications can take several weeks. Contact your home regulator in advance to ensure they respond promptly to NMC verification requests.

OSCE

OSCE Preparation

Many candidates underestimate the OSCE. Allow adequate preparation time, practice with colleagues or study groups, and familiarise yourself with UK nursing protocols. Your employer should provide structured preparation support.

How to Prepare for NMC Registration

Successful NMC registration requires careful planning and preparation. Follow these steps to navigate the process efficiently.

  1. Verify your eligibility: Check that you meet the basic requirements (nursing qualification, registration in home country, 12 months experience) before beginning your application.
  2. Take your English test: Schedule your IELTS or OET early, allowing time for retakes if needed. Remember that scores are valid for two years.
  3. Gather documentation: Collect all required documents including qualifications, registration certificates, transcripts, and references. Ensure all documents are certified and translated if necessary.
  4. Submit your application: Use the new NMC online portal to submit your application with all supporting documents. Pay the application fee.
  5. Complete qualification assessment: Wait for the NMC to assess your qualifications. Respond promptly to any requests for additional information.
  6. Book your OSCE: Once you receive your eligibility letter, book your OSCE at your preferred test centre. Begin intensive preparation.
  7. Pass your OSCE: Attend your OSCE and await results. If unsuccessful, you can resit up to three times.
  8. Complete registration: Pay the registration fee and receive your NMC PIN, allowing you to practise as a registered nurse in the UK.
