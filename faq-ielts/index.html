IELTS stands for the International English Language Testing System. It is the world’s most popular English language proficiency test for study, work, and migration.
The two types are IELTS Academic (for university study and professional registration) and IELTS General Training (for work experience, training programmes, and migration to English-speaking countries).
IELTS has four sections: Listening (30 minutes), Reading (60 minutes), Writing (60 minutes), and Speaking (11-14 minutes). The total test time is about 2 hours and 45 minutes.
IELTS uses a band score from 0 to 9 for each section. Your overall band score is the average of the four section scores, rounded to the nearest half band. Most universities require a band 6.5 or 7.0.
The IELTS Speaking test lasts 11 to 14 minutes. It consists of three parts: an introduction and interview, a long turn where you speak on a topic for two minutes, and a two-way discussion.
In IELTS Academic Reading, passages are taken from books, journals, and newspapers on academic topics. In General Training Reading, passages focus on everyday topics, workplace contexts, and general interest texts.
The IELTS exam fee varies by country, typically ranging from £170 to £220 in the UK, $245 to $255 in the USA, and similar amounts elsewhere. Check your local test centre for exact pricing.
You can register for IELTS online through the official British Council, IDP, or Cambridge websites. Select your test type, choose a test centre and date, upload your ID, and pay the fee.
IELTS results are released 13 calendar days after the paper-based test and 3 to 5 days after the computer-delivered test. You can view them online with your candidate number.
IELTS scores are valid for two years from the test date. After this period, institutions may not accept your results as a current measure of your English proficiency.
Yes, you can retake IELTS as many times as you wish. There is no waiting period between tests, but you must pay the full fee each time. Consider IELTS One Skill Retake if available.
IELTS One Skill Retake allows you to retake just one section (Listening, Reading, Writing, or Speaking) if you did not achieve your desired score. It is available at select test centres.
Most UK universities require an overall band score of 6.5 to 7.0, with no individual section below 6.0. Competitive programmes like Medicine or Law may require 7.0 or 7.5.
Yes, over 3,400 US institutions accept IELTS, including Ivy League universities. Most require a band score of 6.5 to 7.0 for undergraduate and postgraduate programmes.
Bring your valid passport or national ID (the same one used for registration), pencils, pens, an eraser, and a clear water bottle. No phones, watches, or electronic devices are permitted.
You can use a pen or pencil for the IELTS computer-delivered test. For the paper-based Writing test, you should use a pencil for the answer sheet and may use a pen or pencil for the writing booklet.
Use official IELTS practice materials, take timed mock tests, improve your vocabulary daily, listen to English podcasts, read academic articles, and practise speaking with a partner. Anglotec AI offers free IELTS preparation tools.
There is no pass or fail in IELTS. You receive a band score from 0 to 9 based on your performance. The score you need depends on the requirements of your university, employer, or immigration authority.
You can send your IELTS scores electronically through the Test Report Form (TRF) portal. Most test centres allow you to nominate up to five institutions to receive your scores free of charge within one month of your test date.
IELTS for UKVI is specifically approved for UK Visas and Immigration applications. It follows the same format but has stricter security protocols. If you need a student visa (formerly Tier 4), you must take IELTS for UKVI.
