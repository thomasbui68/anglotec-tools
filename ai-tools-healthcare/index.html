Other AI Tools for Exam Preparation

Beyond Anglotec's specialised tools, several other AI platforms can support your exam preparation journey:

ChatGPT (OpenAI)

While not healthcare-specific, ChatGPT is remarkably useful for exam preparation. You can use it to: generate IELTS Speaking cue card questions and model answers, practise OET Writing by providing case notes and asking for feedback, explain complex medical concepts in simpler terms, create personalised study schedules, and simulate professional conversations for OSCE practice. The free tier (GPT-3.5) is sufficient for most study purposes. Always verify medical information against authoritative sources.

Grammarly

Grammarly's AI writing assistant is invaluable for OET Writing and IELTS Writing preparation. The free version catches grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors. The premium version offers vocabulary enhancement suggestions and tone adjustments — useful for ensuring your referral letters maintain a professional register.

Oxford Medical Dictionary App

The Oxford Handbook series now includes AI-powered search that understands natural language queries. Instead of searching "DM type 2 complications," you can ask "What are the long-term complications of poorly controlled Type 2 diabetes?" This is invaluable for quick clinical reference during revision.

AI for Clinical Decision Support

For healthcare professionals already in practice, these AI tools provide clinical support:

New York and Oxford Medical

These clinical decision support tools use AI to help with differential diagnosis, treatment recommendations, and drug interactions. While primarily subscription-based, they offer free trials and limited free access that can be valuable during your adaptation period in the NHS.

VisualDx

An AI-powered diagnostic clinical decision support system that combines a comprehensive medical image library with artificial intelligence. Particularly useful for dermatology cases and rashes. Free access is sometimes available through NHS Trusts and academic institutions.

NICE BNF & BNFc Apps

While not strictly AI, the British National Formulary apps are essential free tools for all UK healthcare professionals. They provide up-to-date prescribing information, drug interactions, and dosage guidelines. The NICE Evidence Search is also invaluable for finding clinical guidelines.

AI for Documentation & Efficiency

Documentation is one of the most time-consuming aspects of healthcare work. These tools help:

Nuance DMO (Dragon Medical One)

Voice recognition software designed specifically for medical documentation. Many NHS trusts provide this to staff. It allows hands-free documentation and is trained on medical vocabulary. Check with your employer for availability.

Otter.ai

A free AI transcription tool that can record and transcribe lectures, meetings, and handover sessions. The free tier provides 300 minutes of transcription per month with real-time transcription and speaker identification. Excellent for recording OSCE study group sessions or clinical teaching.

Notion AI

Notion's AI features help you organise study materials, create revision notes, summarise long documents, and set up a personal knowledge base. The free personal plan includes limited AI credits. Many healthcare professionals use it to create structured revision databases.

AI for Continuous Professional Development

Lifelong learning is essential for healthcare professionals. These free AI tools support ongoing education:

PubMed with AI Search

PubMed now incorporates AI-powered search suggestions and article summarisation. The "Similar Articles" feature uses machine learning to find related research. Essential for evidence-based practice and keeping up with current research.

Explain Like I'm 5 (ELI5 Medical)

Several free browser extensions use AI to explain complex medical terms in plain language. These are particularly useful when reading UK clinical guidelines or NHS policies for the first time as an international professional.

LearningRadiology AI

A free educational resource that uses AI to help identify radiological findings. While primarily for doctors, nurses in A&E and acute settings may find it useful for understanding basic imaging.

AI Safety in Healthcare: What You Need to Know

While AI tools offer tremendous benefits, responsible use is essential:

  • AI is a support tool, not a replacement: Never rely solely on AI for clinical decisions. Always apply your professional judgment.
  • Verify information: AI can generate incorrect information. Cross-reference with clinical guidelines, BNF, and senior colleagues.
  • Patient confidentiality: Never input identifiable patient information into AI tools unless they are NHS-approved and GDPR-compliant.
  • Data security: Use tools that comply with UK data protection regulations. Anglotec AI Academy tools are fully GDPR-compliant.
  • Know the limitations: AI excels at pattern recognition and language processing but cannot replace clinical experience and human empathy.

NHS Guidelines on AI Use

The NHS has published guidance on the responsible use of AI in healthcare. Key principles include transparency, fairness, accountability, and ensuring AI augments rather than replaces clinical expertise. All AI tools used in NHS settings must meet strict safety and data governance standards.

Is AI safe to use in healthcare?
AI tools designed for healthcare are generally safe when used as decision support rather than replacement for clinical judgment.
