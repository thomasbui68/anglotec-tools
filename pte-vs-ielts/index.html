Which Test Is Easier? Breaking It Down

The answer depends on your strengths, preferences, and goals

🎤

PTE May Be Easier If...

You're comfortable speaking to a computer rather than a person. PTE's speaking section eliminates examiner anxiety. Many test-takers find PTE easier if they have clear pronunciation and can speak at a natural pace without hesitation.

🗣️

IELTS May Be Easier If...

You prefer natural conversation and want the ability to clarify, ask questions, or read body language. The live Speaking test with an examiner feels more human and less robotic to many candidates.

⚡

PTE May Be Easier If...

You want fast results. PTE results arrive in 48 hours vs 3-13 days for IELTS. If you have tight university or visa deadlines, this alone can make PTE the easier choice.

📝

IELTS May Be Easier If...

You prefer traditional question formats. IELTS Reading and Listening follow predictable patterns that are well-documented in prep materials. PTE's integrated skills tasks can be confusing at first.

🎯

PTE May Be Easier If...

You test well under pressure with time limits. PTE's single 2-hour sitting with no breaks suits focused test-takers. No separate Speaking appointment to schedule.

📚

IELTS May Be Easier If...

You want extensive prep resources. IELTS has decades of past papers, countless prep books, and well-established courses. PTE's resource library, while growing, is more limited.

Universities & Countries: Which Test Do They Prefer?

🇦🇺

Australia

Both accepted equally. PTE is extremely popular due to fast results for visa applications. Department of Home Affairs accepts both for all visa subclasses. Many Australian test-takers prefer PTE.

🇬🇧

United Kingdom

IELTS has wider acceptance (11,000+ institutions). PTE is growing rapidly and now accepted by most major universities. For UKVI purposes, both offer approved versions. IELTS remains the default choice.

🇨🇦

Canada

IELTS is more widely recognised by universities and for Express Entry immigration. PTE Core is now accepted for some Canadian immigration pathways, but IELTS remains the safer bet.

🇳🇿

New Zealand

Both tests accepted for visas and education. Immigration New Zealand recognises both PTE Academic and IELTS for Skilled Migrant and work visa categories.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is PTE really easier than IELTS? +
"Easier" is subjective. PTE eliminates face-to-face speaking anxiety and gives faster results, which many find easier. However, PTE's integrated skills format (where one task tests multiple skills simultaneously) can be challenging. IELTS has more predictable formats and vastly more practice materials. Your personal strengths matter more than objective difficulty.
Do universities prefer IELTS over PTE? +
Most universities now accept both equally. IELTS is accepted by 11,000+ institutions globally, while PTE is accepted by 3,500+. If applying to competitive programmes or lesser-known institutions, verify PTE acceptance directly with the admissions office before booking.
How quickly do I get PTE results vs IELTS? +
PTE Academic results are typically available within 48 hours. IELTS computer-delivered results arrive in 3-5 days, while paper-based results take 13 days. If you have tight deadlines for university applications or visa submissions, PTE's speed is a significant advantage.
Can I use PTE for UK nursing registration (NMC)? +
Yes. The NMC started accepting PTE Academic from August 2024. The required scores are: Overall 76 with minimum 66 in each communicative skill (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking). Always check the latest NMC requirements before booking.
Is PTE speaking easier because there's no human examiner? +
For many test-takers, yes. Speaking to a computer eliminates nervousness about being judged by a person. However, PTE's AI is strict about fluency, pronunciation, and pacing. You can't ask for clarification or use body language. Some candidates perform better with the conversational nature of IELTS Speaking.
Which test has more preparation resources? +
IELTS has significantly more preparation materials: decades of official past papers, hundreds of prep books, established courses, and a massive online community. PTE's official practice materials, while high-quality, are more limited. Anglotec AI provides comprehensive prep for both tests with AI-powered practice.
Can I switch from IELTS to PTE (or vice versa) while preparing? +
Yes, but significant format differences mean you'll need to adjust your preparation. Many skills (vocabulary, grammar, listening comprehension) transfer directly. However, you'll need to practise the specific question types and timing of your chosen test. Allow at least 2-3 weeks to adapt to the new format.
