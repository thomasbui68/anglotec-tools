Understanding Speaking Part 1

IELTS Speaking Part 1 lasts 4-5 minutes and consists of introduction and interview. The examiner introduces themselves, confirms your identity, and asks general questions on 2-3 familiar topics. This section is designed to help you relax and demonstrate your ability to communicate opinions and information on everyday subjects.

Part 1 accounts for approximately 25% of your speaking score. While it is the easiest section, a strong performance sets a positive tone for the rest of the test. Examiners assess your fluency, vocabulary range, grammatical accuracy, and pronunciation from the very first question.

Part 1 at a Glance

  • Duration: 4-5 minutes
  • Topics: 2-3 familiar topic areas
  • Questions: Approximately 10-12 questions total
  • Score weight: ~25% of speaking score
  • Focus: Personal experiences, opinions, habits

Your speaking is evaluated against four criteria, each worth 25%:

Hometown & Accommodation

Q: Tell me about your hometown.

"I come from [City], which is a coastal city in the south of [Country]. It's renowned for its stunning beaches and vibrant cultural scene. What I particularly love about it is the relaxed pace of life compared to the capital. However, it has grown significantly over the past decade, with numerous modern developments springing up."

Q: What do you like most about your hometown?

"Without a doubt, it's the sense of community. People are genuinely friendly and welcoming, which creates a warm atmosphere. There are also plenty of outdoor activities available, from hiking in the nearby hills to watersports along the coast."

Q: Do you live in a house or an apartment?

"I currently live in a spacious apartment on the third floor of a modern residential building. One of the major advantages is the proximity to public transport and local amenities. That said, I do occasionally miss having a garden, which is something I'd love to have in the future."

Q: Would you like to move to a different place in the future?

"Actually, yes. I've been contemplating moving to a quieter neighbourhood on the outskirts of the city. While I appreciate being centrally located, the noise and congestion can be rather overwhelming at times. A more tranquil setting would definitely improve my quality of life."

Work & Study

Q: Are you a student or do you work?

"I'm currently working as a software developer at a tech startup. I've been in this role for approximately two years now, and it's been an incredibly rewarding experience. The company has a really innovative culture, which keeps me motivated."

Q: What do you enjoy most about your studies/work?

"The aspect I find most fulfilling is the problem-solving nature of the work. Every day presents a new challenge, which means I'm constantly learning and developing my skills. Additionally, collaborating with a diverse team has really broadened my perspective."

Q: What would you like to do in the future?

"In the long term, I'm hoping to specialise in artificial intelligence and machine learning. I've always been fascinated by how technology can be harnessed to solve real-world problems. Eventually, I might even pursue a master's degree to deepen my expertise in this field."

Q: Do you prefer working alone or in a team?

"It really depends on the task at hand. For analytical work that requires deep concentration, I definitely prefer working alone. However, for brainstorming sessions and creative projects, I find that collaboration brings out the best ideas. A combination of both is ideal, in my opinion."

Hobbies & Interests

Q: What do you like to do in your free time?

"I'm quite passionate about photography. I tend to spend my weekends exploring different neighbourhoods and capturing street scenes. It's a wonderfully creative outlet that allows me to see my city from a completely different perspective."

Q: Have your hobbies changed since you were a child?

"Absolutely. As a child, I was quite obsessed with video games and would spend hours in front of the screen. These days, I'm far more interested in outdoor activities and physical exercise. I suppose my priorities have shifted as I've grown older."

Q: Do you prefer indoor or outdoor activities?

"On balance, I'd say outdoor activities. There's something incredibly refreshing about being in nature, whether it's going for a hike or simply cycling through the park. Having said that, during extreme weather, I'm more than happy to stay indoors and read a good book."

Q: Is there a hobby you'd like to take up?

"I've been meaning to learn how to play the guitar for years. Several of my friends play musical instruments, and I'm always envious when they jam together. I think learning an instrument would be a fantastic way to unwind after a stressful day."

Daily Routine & Food

Q: Tell me about your daily routine.

"I'm generally an early riser, so I typically wake up around 6:30. I start with a quick workout, followed by breakfast, and then commute to work. My evenings are usually quite relaxed; I cook dinner and perhaps watch a documentary or read before heading to bed around 10:30."

Q: What kind of food do you enjoy?

"I'm quite adventurous when it comes to food. I absolutely adore Thai cuisine because of the bold flavours and fresh ingredients. I'm also rather fond of Mediterranean food, particularly Greek salads and Italian pasta dishes. Having said that, nothing beats a home-cooked meal from time to time."

Q: Do you prefer eating at home or in restaurants?

"For everyday meals, I definitely prefer eating at home. I enjoy cooking, and it's considerably healthier and more economical. However, for special occasions or when meeting friends, restaurants are wonderful because of the ambience and the variety of dishes available."

Q: Has your diet changed over the years?

"Yes, significantly. I used to eat quite a lot of fast food during my university days, mainly because it was convenient and affordable. These days, I'm much more conscious about nutrition. I try to incorporate more vegetables and whole grains into my meals, and I've significantly reduced my sugar intake."

Travel & Transport

Q: Do you enjoy travelling?

"I'm absolutely passionate about travelling. There's nothing quite like immersing yourself in a completely different culture and experiencing new cuisines and customs. I've been fortunate enough to visit several countries in Southeast Asia, and each trip has been unforgettable."

Q: What form of transport do you use most often?

"I predominantly use the underground metro system. It's remarkably efficient and reliable, especially during rush hour when the roads are completely gridlocked. On weekends, however, I prefer cycling as it's more environmentally friendly and gives me some exercise."

Q: What type of holiday do you prefer?

"I tend to gravitate towards cultural holidays rather than beach vacations. I love exploring historical sites, visiting museums, and learning about the local heritage of a place. That said, after a busy period at work, a relaxing beach holiday can be absolutely rejuvenating."

Q: Is there a country you would love to visit?

"I've always dreamed of visiting Japan. I'm fascinated by the unique blend of ancient traditions and cutting-edge modernity. The food scene is also incredibly appealing, and I'd love to experience cherry blossom season in Kyoto. It's definitely at the top of my bucket list."

Technology & Internet

Q: How often do you use the internet?

"I use it constantly, both for work and personal purposes. It's an indispensable tool in my professional life for research and communication. Outside of work, I use streaming services, social media, and online shopping platforms on a daily basis."

Q: Do you think people spend too much time online?

"To some extent, yes. I think many people, particularly younger generations, have become overly dependent on their devices. Excessive screen time can negatively impact mental health and reduce face-to-face social interaction. However, the internet itself is not the problem; it's about finding a healthy balance."

Q: How has technology changed your life?

"Technology has transformed almost every aspect of my life. Working remotely would have been impossible without video conferencing tools and cloud-based collaboration platforms. On a personal level, I can stay connected with friends and family across the world instantaneously, which is remarkable."

Q: What technology could you not live without?

"That's a difficult question, but I'd have to say my smartphone. It functions as my navigation system, camera, music player, and communication device all in one. While I recognise I'm somewhat dependent on it, it genuinely makes my life significantly more convenient."

Answering Strategies for Band 7+

1. Expand Your Answers

Never give one-word answers. Aim for 2-4 sentences per response. Add a reason, example, or personal experience.

2. Use Natural Fillers

Phrases like "That's an interesting question," "I've never really thought about that," or "It depends on..." give you thinking time.

3. Show Range

Use a variety of tenses (present, past, present perfect, conditionals) to demonstrate grammatical range.

4. Be Yourself

Give honest answers. It's easier to speak fluently about your real opinions and experiences than memorised scripts.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should my answers be in Part 1?

Aim for 2-4 sentences per answer (approximately 15-30 seconds of speaking). This gives enough detail without rambling. The examiner needs to ask 10-12 questions in 4-5 minutes.

Can I ask the examiner to repeat a question?

Yes, you can politely ask. Say "Could you repeat that, please?" or "I'm sorry, I didn't catch that." This will not affect your score.

Should I memorise answers?

No. Memorised answers are easy for examiners to detect and will lower your score. Instead, practise speaking naturally about various topics and build your vocabulary.

What if I don't understand a question?

You can ask for clarification. Say "Do you mean...?" or "Are you asking about...?" It's better to clarify than to answer the wrong question.

Is it okay to say "I don't know"?

It's better to say something relevant. If you genuinely have no experience, say "I haven't had much experience with that, but I imagine..." and speculate.

Does accent affect my score?

No. Your accent is not assessed. What matters is whether your pronunciation is clear and easy to understand. Focus on clarity, word stress, and intonation.
