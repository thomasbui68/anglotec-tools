OET Speaking Tips

Start Strong

Always introduce yourself, confirm the patient's identity, and explain your role. This shows professionalism and builds rapport.

Use Open Questions

Avoid yes/no questions. Use "How", "What", "Can you tell me" to encourage the patient to share more information.

Show Empathy

Acknowledge the patient's feelings. Phrases like "I understand this must be difficult" demonstrate compassion.

Check Understanding

Always ask "Do you have any questions?" and summarise key points to ensure the patient has understood.

Manage Time

5 minutes passes quickly. Prioritise the most important key points and don't spend too long on any single topic.

Use Appropriate Language

Adapt your language to the patient's level. Avoid excessive jargon unless explaining it clearly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the OET Speaking sub-test?

The OET Speaking test consists of two 5-minute role-play scenarios. You take the role of a healthcare professional (usually a nurse) and interact with an examiner playing a patient, relative, or carer.

How is OET Speaking scored?

OET Speaking is assessed on nine criteria: overall communication, intelligibility, fluency, appropriateness of language, resources of grammar and expression, relationship-building, understanding and incorporating the patient's perspective, information gathering, and information giving.

Are these role plays similar to the real OET?

Yes, our scenarios mirror the structure and difficulty of actual OET Speaking role plays. They include realistic patient cases, appropriate tasks, and key points that reflect what you'll encounter in the exam.

How should I use this tool to prepare?

Generate a scenario, set the timer, and practise speaking aloud. Record yourself if possible. Review your performance against the key points. Repeat with different scenarios to build confidence and fluency.
