How to Choose the Right IELTS App

Consider these factors when selecting your IELTS preparation tool.

1. Your Target Band Score

For Band 8+, you need an app with advanced AI feedback. For Band 6-7, a basic practice app may suffice. Nurses typically need Band 7 in all sections.

2. Weak Areas

Identify which section needs the most work. Speaking and Writing are typically the hardest and benefit most from AI-powered feedback tools.

3. Budget

Free apps offer basic practice. Premium subscriptions (typically 10-30 GBP/month) unlock AI features. Consider it an investment versus retest fees.

4. Learning Style

Video learners prefer Magoosh. Interactive learners need AI apps like Anglotec. Self-directed learners may prefer practice-test focused apps.

5. Device Compatibility

Ensure the app works on your devices. Some apps are mobile-only, while others like Anglotec offer full-featured web versions for comfortable typing practice.

6. Healthcare Content

If you're a nurse or doctor, choose an app with healthcare-specific vocabulary modules. Anglotec includes medical terminology practice tailored for NMC registration.

How accurate are AI band score predictions?

Top-tier apps like Anglotec estimate band scores within 0.5 accuracy based on extensive calibration against real test results. However, no app can guarantee your actual score, which also depends on test-day performance and nerves.

Can I use multiple apps together?

Yes, many successful candidates use one comprehensive app (like Anglotec) as their primary tool and supplement with specialised apps for specific weaknesses. Just avoid app-hopping without deep practice in any single platform.

How long should I prepare with an IELTS app?

Most candidates need 6-12 weeks of consistent daily practice. Apps with AI can reduce this by identifying your weaknesses faster. Plan for at least 30-60 minutes of focused practice daily for optimal results.
