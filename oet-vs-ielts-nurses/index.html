The Big Question: OET or IELTS?

If you're an international nurse looking to register with the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) in the UK, the Nursing and Midwifery Board of Ireland (NMBI), or the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA), you need to prove your English proficiency. Both OET and IELTS are accepted - but which is better for you?

NMC Requirements (2026)

IELTS: 7.0 in Reading, Listening, Speaking | 6.5 in Writing (OR 7.0 all bands)
OET: Grade B (350+) in Listening, Reading, Speaking | Grade C+ in Writing

Why Nurses Choose OET

✔ Familiar vocabulary - Medical terms you use daily
✔ Realistic scenarios - Tasks mirror your actual job
✔ Better writing task - Referral letters vs academic essays
✔ Confidence in speaking - Professional role-plays
✔ Designed for healthcare - Every question is relevant

OET is Easier If...

You're comfortable with medical English but struggle with general academic topics. The writing task (referral letter) is very similar to what you already do at work.

Why Some Nurses Choose IELTS

✔ Cheaper - Often half the price of OET
✔ More test dates - Available almost every day
✔ Faster results - 3-5 days on computer
✔ Universal recognition - Accepted for visas, courses, jobs
✔ More preparation resources - Huge ecosystem of prep materials

IELTS is Better If...

You need English certification for multiple purposes (visa + registration + future study). It's also better if you already have strong general English skills.

FAQs

Does NMC prefer OET or IELTS?

NMC accepts both equally. There is no preference - choose the exam that plays to your strengths.

Can I combine OET and IELTS scores?

No. NMC requires all scores from a single exam type in one sitting (with some exceptions for combined scores within 6 months - check current NMC guidance).
