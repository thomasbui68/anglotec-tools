The 14-Day Emergency IELTS Plan

Two weeks is not ideal, but it's workable. This plan abandons breadth and goes deep on the highest-impact activities. We're not building English from scratch — we're optimizing your test performance.

Day 0 (Today, Before You Begin)

Take one full practice test immediately. You need to know your starting score. Do NOT skip this. Score every section ruthlessly. This 3-hour investment will save you 30 hours of wasted study time by showing exactly where to focus.

Week 1: Core Skills (Days 1-7)

Day 1

Listening Foundations

  • Complete 2 full Listening tests (Cambridge IELTS)
  • Analyse EVERY wrong answer — why did you miss it?
  • Practice spelling: names, numbers, postcodes
  • Learn to predict answer types before audio plays
  • Time: 4 hours minimum
Day 2

Reading Techniques

  • Master skimming (2-min gist) and scanning (find info)
  • Complete 3 full Reading passages under timed conditions
  • Focus on True/False/Not Given (hardest question type)
  • Learn keyword matching between questions and text
  • Time: 4 hours minimum
Day 3

Writing Task 1 Mastery

  • Learn the 4-sentence introduction structure
  • Practice all 4 chart types (line, bar, pie, table)
  • Write 2 Task 1 responses; get feedback if possible
  • Memorise 15 key describing phrases (rose, plummeted, etc.)
  • Always include an overview paragraph
Day 4

Writing Task 2 Mastery

  • Learn the 4-paragraph essay structure by heart
  • Practice opinion, discussion, and problem-solution essays
  • Write 2 full Task 2 essays today (250+ words each)
  • Memorise 20 academic phrases for introductions/conclusions
  • Use linking words: Furthermore, Consequently, Nevertheless
Day 5

Speaking Part 1 & 2

  • Record yourself answering 20 Part 1 questions
  • Practice 5 different Part 2 cue cards (full 2 minutes)
  • Use the 1-min prep time wisely: write 4-5 bullet points
  • Focus on fluency over perfect grammar
  • Listen to your recordings — identify hesitation patterns
Day 6

Speaking Part 3 & Mock Test

  • Practice 15 Part 3 discussion questions with full answers
  • Give opinions + reasons + examples + counter-arguments
  • Take a FULL mock test under strict exam conditions
  • Score yourself; compare to Day 0 baseline
  • Celebrate improvements; note remaining weak areas
Day 7

Review & Light Rest Day

  • Review all vocabulary learned this week (should be 300+ words)
  • Re-read your best Writing tasks as confidence boosters
  • Listen to English podcasts for passive learning
  • Review wrong answers from Days 1-6
  • Sleep 8 hours — your brain needs consolidation time

Week 2: Peak Performance (Days 8-14)

Day 8

Mock Test + Weakness Drill

  • Full mock test in the morning (3 hours, strict timing)
  • Afternoon: drill your weakest section only
  • If Writing is weak, write 3 essays
  • If Reading is weak, complete 4 passages
  • If Listening is weak, do 3 full listening tests
Day 9

Mock Test + Vocabulary Blitz

  • Another full mock test in the morning
  • Afternoon: vocabulary intensive — 100 new academic words
  • Focus on words that appear frequently in Reading
  • Learn word families (analyse, analysis, analytical, analyst)
  • Review all collocations (common word pairings)
Day 10

Speed & Accuracy Day

  • Complete Reading passages in 18 minutes (not 20)
  • Write Task 1 in 18 minutes, Task 2 in 35 minutes
  • Listening: practice with 1.2x speed audio
  • Speaking: practice giving answers 20% faster
  • This builds buffer time for the real exam
Day 11

Mock Test + Speaking Polish

  • Full mock test in morning
  • Afternoon: intensive speaking practice
  • Record 10 Part 2 answers and analyse them
  • Practice Part 3 with a partner or mirror
  • Focus on expanding answers with examples
Day 12

Final Full Mock Test

  • Your last complete mock test — treat it like the real exam
  • Wake at the same time as your exam day
  • Wear comfortable clothes
  • Use the same equipment (headphones, pen)
  • Score this test — it should predict your exam score
Day 13

Review & Light Practice

  • Review vocabulary lists one final time
  • Re-read your best writing samples for confidence
  • Do ONE section of Listening (keep ears sharp)
  • Read one English article aloud for 10 minutes
  • Prepare documents for exam day
  • Stop studying by 6 PM
Day 14 — EXAM DAY

You Are Ready

  • Wake up 2 hours before the exam
  • Eat a light, protein-rich breakfast
  • Arrive 30 minutes early at the test centre
  • Bring valid ID (passport/national ID)
  • Stay calm — trust your 2 weeks of preparation
  • You've done the work. Now execute.

12 Quick Wins That Boost Your Score Fast

When time is short, focus on changes that give the biggest score improvement with the least effort.

1. Learn 20 Linking Phrases

Furthermore, Moreover, Consequently, Nevertheless, In contrast. Using these correctly in Writing and Speaking instantly raises your coherence score.

2. Always Write in Paragraphs

Task 2 essays MUST have 4 paragraphs. Examiners penalise essays with fewer or no clear paragraphing.

3. Check Your Spelling

Common misspellings: government, environment, accommodation, definitely, necessary. Wrong spelling = lost marks.

4. Use ALL CAPS for Listening

Write all Listening answers in capital letters. This eliminates any capitalisation penalty and looks cleaner.

5. Answer Every Question

There's no negative marking in IELTS. Never leave a blank — guess if you have to. A guess has a chance; a blank has zero.

6. Task 2 Is Worth More

Task 2 counts for double Task 1 in Writing. Always spend 40 minutes on Task 2 and 20 minutes on Task 1. No exceptions.

7. Predict Before Listening

During the 30-second preview, predict the type of answer (number, name, date). This primes your brain to catch it.

8. Skim, Don't Read Everything

In Reading, 60% of the text doesn't contain answers. Learn to skim for gist and scan for specific information.

9. Expand Speaking Answers

Never give one-word answers. Aim for 3-4 sentences in Part 1, 2 full minutes in Part 2, and extended opinions in Part 3.

10. Learn the "Not Given" Trick

In Reading T/F/NG questions: if the text doesn't mention it at all, it's "Not Given" — not "False". This distinction costs many candidates a band.

11. Sleep Well Before the Exam

A well-rested brain performs 20-30% better. Study less, sleep more in the final 2 days. This is free score improvement.

12. Bring a Watch

Not all test centres have visible clocks. A simple watch lets you manage time across all sections without stress.

Emergency Strategies by Section

When you only have 2 weeks, these section-specific hacks deliver maximum score improvement.

Listening: The 3-Point Strategy

Point 1: Use the 30-second preview to read ALL questions and underline keywords. Know what you're listening for before the audio starts.

Point 2: The answer often comes AFTER the keyword. The speaker might say "The conference was originally scheduled for Monday, but it's been moved to... Wednesday." The answer is Wednesday, not Monday.

Point 3: Write your answers on the question paper first. Transfer them in the 10-minute transfer time at the end. Check spelling meticulously during transfer.

Reading: The Keyword Matching Method

Don't read the passage first — read the questions first. Underline 2-3 keywords in each question. Then scan the passage for those exact words or synonyms. Most IELTS Reading questions follow the order of the text, so work through sequentially. For True/False/Not Given: True = same meaning in text; False = opposite meaning in text; Not Given = not mentioned anywhere.

Writing: The Band 7 Formula

Task 1: Introduction (1 sentence) + Overview (2 sentences) + Detail 1 (3 sentences) + Detail 2 (3 sentences) = 150+ words. Task 2: Introduction (2 sentences) + Body 1 (topic + example + 5 sentences) + Body 2 (topic + example + 5 sentences) + Conclusion (2 sentences) = 250+ words. Use at least 5 linking words per paragraph. Include 2-3 complex sentences with subordinate clauses. Proofread for 3 minutes at the end.

Speaking: The STAR Method

For Part 2 cue cards, use STAR: Situation (set the scene), Task (what you needed to do), Action (what you did), Result (how it turned out). This gives you 2 minutes of structured, fluent speech without memorisation. For Part 3, always give a direct answer first, then explain with "This is because...", add an example "For instance...", and consider an alternative view "On the other hand...".

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I really prepare for IELTS in just 2 weeks?

Yes, if you already have intermediate-to-advanced English and can dedicate 6+ hours daily. This plan won't teach you English from scratch, but it will optimise your test performance significantly. Many students improve by 0.5-1 band in 2 weeks with focused preparation.

What's the minimum number of practice tests I should do?

In 2 weeks, aim for at least 6 full practice tests — one every other day minimum. Quality review of wrong answers is more important than quantity. One thoroughly analysed test beats three rushed ones.

Should I focus on my weakest or strongest section?

Focus 70% of your time on your weakest section — it has the most room for improvement. But don't completely neglect your strongest section; a small decline there can wipe out gains elsewhere.

Is it better to do paper or computer IELTS with 2 weeks prep?

Choose the format you're most comfortable with. Computer-delivered IELTS gives results in 3-5 days (vs 13 days for paper). The Speaking test is often on the same day. If you type faster than you write, go computer.

What if my English level is below B1 (intermediate)?

With below-intermediate English, 2 weeks is extremely challenging for most target scores. Consider postponing your test if possible. If you must take it, focus entirely on Listening and Reading (where you can pick up technique points) and aim for realistic Writing/Speaking scores.

How do I avoid panic on exam day?

Complete at least 4 full mock tests under exam conditions before the real thing. Familiarity breeds confidence. On exam day, arrive early, breathe deeply, and remind yourself: the examiner wants you to succeed. Trust your preparation.

Can I improve my Writing score in 2 weeks?

Yes — Writing is the section where technique matters most. Learning the correct essay structure, using linking words effectively, and including an overview in Task 1 can boost your score by 0.5-1 band even without significant vocabulary improvement. Focus on structure first, vocabulary second.
