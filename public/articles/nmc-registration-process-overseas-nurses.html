NMC Registration: The Complete Overview

[Image: Flowchart showing the complete NMC registration pathway from overseas nurse application through to receiving NMC PIN number]

Registering with the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) as an overseas-qualified nurse is a multi-stage process that requires careful planning, preparation, and patience. The NMC is the regulatory body for nurses and midwives in the UK, and registration is mandatory before you can practise as a nurse in the United Kingdom.

The process has been streamlined significantly in recent years, but it still involves several mandatory stages that must be completed in sequence. Understanding each stage, its requirements, and the associated timelines will help you plan your journey effectively and avoid common delays.

Who Can Apply?

To be eligible for NMC registration as an overseas nurse, you must have completed a nursing programme of at least 3 years duration, hold current registration or a licence to practise as a nurse in your home country, and have practised as a nurse for at least 12 months after qualifying. The NMC will assess your training and experience to determine whether you meet the required standards.

Stage 1: Eligibility Assessment and CBT

Step 1.1: Create Your NMC Online Account

The first step is to create an account on the NMC website and complete the initial eligibility questionnaire. You will need to provide details about your nursing qualification, registration status, and professional experience. The NMC charges a non-refundable registration fee of GBP 153.

Step 1.2: Submit Supporting Documents

You will need to submit several documents including: your nursing qualification certificate, academic transcripts, current registration certificate from your home country, evidence of post-qualification practice, professional indemnity arrangement evidence, a health declaration, character reference, and proof of identity. All non-English documents must be officially translated.

Step 1.3: NMC Eligibility Assessment

The NMC will assess your application to determine if you meet the education and practice requirements. This assessment typically takes 4-8 weeks. If your application is successful, you will receive an Authorisation to Test (ATT) letter, which allows you to book the Computer Based Test (CBT).

Step 1.4: Computer Based Test (CBT)

The CBT is a multiple-choice exam that tests your knowledge, comprehension, application, and analysis of professional nursing practice. It consists of 120 questions to be completed in 4 hours. The questions cover professional values, communication and interpersonal skills, nursing practice, decision making, and leadership management and team working. You can take the CBT at test centres in many countries worldwide or via online proctoring.

Pass mark: The CBT is scored using a modified Angoff method. Results are typically available within 48 hours. You have a maximum of 3 attempts to pass the CBT as part of one application. The test fee is GBP 83.

Stage 2: English Language Requirements

IELTS Academic Requirements

The NMC accepts IELTS Academic with the following minimum scores: Overall: 7.0, Reading: 7.0, Writing: 6.5, Listening: 7.0, Speaking: 7.0. The NMC does not accept IELTS General Training or IELTS One Skill Retake. Your test must have been taken within the last 2 years. Importantly, you can combine two test scores if both are valid, taken within 6 months, and you achieve at least 6.5 in all sections across both tests with no single section below 6.5.

OET Requirements

The NMC accepts OET with the following minimum grades: Reading: Grade B, Writing: Grade C+, Listening: Grade B, Speaking: Grade B. Like IELTS, your OET results must be from within the last 2 years. You can also combine two OET test certificates under the same combining rules as IELTS.

Which Test Should I Choose?

For nurses, OET is often the preferred choice because it uses healthcare-specific scenarios and vocabulary that you are already familiar with from your professional training. Many nurses find the OET Writing task (a referral letter) more intuitive than IELTS Academic Writing. However, if you need registration for purposes beyond nursing (e.g., a university course), IELTS may be more versatile. Consider your strengths, preparation time, and test availability when making your decision.

Stage 3: OSCE Test of Competence

[Image: Photograph of an NMC OSCE test centre showing a simulated hospital ward with assessment stations and nursing equipment]

Step 3.1: Receive Your OSCE Invitation

After passing both the CBT and the English language requirement, the NMC will send you an invitation to book your OSCE. You must sit your OSCE at one of the approved test centres in the UK. Currently, these are operated by Oxford Brookes University, the University of Northampton, and Ulster University.

Step 3.2: Prepare for the OSCE

The OSCE consists of 10 stations that assess your practical nursing skills and clinical decision-making. You should begin your preparation as early as possible, ideally before you even arrive in the UK. Many overseas nurses require 4-8 weeks of intensive preparation. The OSCE fee is GBP 794 per attempt, and you have a maximum of 3 attempts.

Step 3.3: Travel to the UK for OSCE

You will need to travel to the UK to sit your OSCE. Most overseas nurses enter the UK on a Health and Care Worker visa sponsored by their employer. Your employer will typically provide OSCE preparation support and may cover your OSCE fee. Ensure your visa allows sufficient time for preparation and the exam itself.

Step 3.4: OSCE Results

OSCE results are typically available within 5-7 working days. If you pass all 10 stations, you will receive confirmation from the NMC that you have met the Test of Competence requirements. If you fail any station, you will receive detailed feedback on areas for improvement and can book a resit.

Stage 4: Registration and Receiving Your NMC PIN

After successfully passing the OSCE, the final step is to complete your NMC registration and receive your Personal Identification Number (PIN). This process involves the following:

  1. Complete registration declaration: Confirm that the information you provided is accurate and that you meet the NMC's health and character requirements.
  2. Pay the registration fee: The initial registration fee is GBP 120 (subject to change - check the NMC website for current fees).
  3. Set up Direct Debit for annual fee: The NMC annual retention fee is currently GBP 120.
  4. Receive your PIN: Once all requirements are met and fees are paid, you will receive your NMC PIN. This process typically takes 2-10 working days.

Your NMC PIN is your licence to practise as a registered nurse in the UK. You must renew your registration annually and meet the NMC's revalidation requirements every three years. Revalidation involves completing 450 practice hours, 35 hours of continuing professional development, receiving feedback, and writing reflective accounts.

Common Delays and How to Avoid Them

Delay 1: Incomplete Documentation

The NMC rejects applications with missing or incorrect documents, which can delay your eligibility assessment by several weeks. Solution: Use the NMC document checklist, ensure all documents are certified copies where required, and arrange official translations well in advance.

Delay 2: English Test Failures

Failing to meet the English language requirement on the first attempt can add 1-3 months to your timeline. Solution: Take a diagnostic test before your official exam, allow adequate preparation time (6-8 weeks minimum), and consider professional preparation support.

Delay 3: CBT Failures

While the CBT pass rate is high for well-prepared candidates, failures do occur and require rebooking. Solution: Use quality CBT question banks, take mock tests under timed conditions, and review areas of weakness identified in practice tests.

Delay 4: OSCE Failures

The OSCE has a significant failure rate, particularly on the first attempt. Each resit costs GBP 794 and requires rebooking. Solution: Begin preparation early, use video-based training resources, practise clinical skills regularly, and consider an OSCE preparation course.

Delay 5: Visa Processing Issues

Visa applications can be delayed due to incomplete paperwork, document verification issues, or high application volumes. Solution: Work closely with your sponsoring employer, submit your visa application as early as possible, and ensure all financial and employment documents are complete.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does NMC registration take for overseas nurses?

The full NMC registration process for overseas nurses typically takes 6-12 months from initial application to registration. This includes eligibility assessment (4-8 weeks), CBT preparation and sitting (2-4 weeks), English test preparation and sitting (4-8 weeks), OSCE preparation and sitting (6-12 weeks), and final registration processing (2-4 weeks).

Can I choose between IELTS and OET for NMC registration?

Yes, the NMC accepts both IELTS Academic and OET for nursing. For IELTS, you need an overall score of 7.0 with at least 6.5 in Writing and 7.0 in Reading, Listening, and Speaking. For OET, you need at least Grade C+ in Writing and Grade B in Reading, Listening, and Speaking.

How much does NMC registration cost in total?

The total cost of NMC registration for overseas nurses is approximately GBP 2,500-3,500. This includes the NMC registration fee (GBP 153), CBT fee (GBP 83), English test fee (IELTS ~GBP 185 or OET ~GBP 587), OSCE fee (GBP 794), visa and immigration health surcharge costs, plus travel and accommodation for the OSCE.

Can I work as a nurse in the UK before completing NMC registration?

No, you cannot work as a registered nurse in the UK without an NMC PIN. However, many overseas nurses are employed by NHS trusts in non-registered roles (such as healthcare assistants or nursing associates) while they complete their OSCE and final registration. Your employer will support you through this process.
