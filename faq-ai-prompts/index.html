Prompt engineering is the practice of crafting effective instructions (prompts) to guide AI models like ChatGPT and Claude to produce accurate, useful, and relevant outputs. It combines communication skills, logic, and creativity.
Yes, prompt engineering has emerged as a legitimate career role. Companies hire prompt engineers to optimise AI interactions, automate workflows, and build AI-powered products. It is also a valuable skill for marketers, developers, and analysts.
No, you do not need coding skills to start with prompt engineering. However, knowing basic Python or JavaScript helps if you want to work with AI APIs, build automated workflows, or develop AI applications.
ChatGPT (by OpenAI) is known for versatile conversations, code generation, and plugin support. Claude (by Anthropic) excels at long document analysis, safety, and nuanced reasoning. Both are powerful large language models.
A good prompt is clear, specific, and structured. Include context, define the desired output format, specify the role the AI should play, and provide examples. Break complex tasks into steps for better results.
A system prompt sets the AI’s behaviour, role, and constraints (e.g., “You are a medical tutor”). A user prompt is the specific question or instruction from the user. Together they shape the AI’s response.
Zero-shot prompting means asking the AI to perform a task without giving any examples. It relies entirely on the model’s pre-trained knowledge. Works well for simple, straightforward tasks.
Few-shot prompting involves providing 2 to 5 examples of the desired input-output pattern. This helps the AI understand the format and style you want, improving accuracy for more complex or specific tasks.
Chain-of-thought prompting asks the AI to show its reasoning step by step before giving the final answer. This improves accuracy on logic, maths, and reasoning tasks by making the thought process explicit.

Use low temperature (0.0 to 0.3) for factual, consistent answers. Use higher temperature (0.7 to 1.0) for creative, varied outputs. Most chat interfaces handle this automatically.
Provide specific context, use structured formats, ask the AI to cite sources, break complex questions into steps, use few-shot examples, and ask the AI to verify its own answers before responding.
Prompt chaining breaks a complex task into a sequence of smaller prompts, where the output of one prompt becomes the input of the next. This improves quality and control over the final result.
