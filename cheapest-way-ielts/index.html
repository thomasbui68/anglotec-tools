The True Cost of IELTS Preparation

Before exploring free alternatives, understand what paid preparation typically costs. IELTS preparation courses range from £200 for basic online courses to £2,000+ for intensive in-person programmes. Private tutoring costs £30-£100 per hour. Premium apps and platforms charge £10-£50 monthly subscriptions. Mock tests with professional feedback cost £50-£150 each. Textbooks and materials add £50-£200. For a candidate using multiple paid resources, total preparation costs can exceed £3,000.

These costs are prohibitive for many candidates, particularly those from developing countries where average monthly salaries may be lower than the cost of a single preparation course. The good news is that with the right free resources and disciplined study approach, you can achieve equivalent or better results without spending anything on preparation materials.

Zero-Cost 8-Week IELTS Study Plan

This intensive plan assumes 3-4 hours of study per day and requires no financial investment.

Weeks 1-2: Foundation Building

Reading: Read one BBC News article daily, noting new vocabulary. Complete two IELTS reading practice tests from free online sources. Writing: Study IELTS Liz writing videos. Write one Task 1 and one Task 2 per week. Submit to Anglotec AI for free feedback. Listening: Listen to BBC World Service daily. Complete two practice listening tests. Speaking: Record yourself answering one Part 2 cue card daily. Practise Part 1 questions with a mirror.

Weeks 3-4: Skill Development

Increase practice test frequency to three per week for each section. Focus on weak areas identified in feedback. Begin using IELTS Simon daily lessons. Join free online IELTS study groups for peer speaking practice.

Weeks 5-6: Intensive Practice

Complete full mock tests every three days. Analyse all errors thoroughly. Focus on time management strategies. Continue daily speaking recordings and writing submissions for AI feedback.

Weeks 7-8: Exam Preparation

Complete full timed mock tests under exam conditions. Review all feedback and common errors. Practise only with authentic IELTS materials. Ensure you are comfortable with test day procedures.

Zero-Cost 12-Week Study Plan

For candidates with more time, this plan at 2-3 hours daily builds skills more gradually:

Weeks 1-3: Focus on English foundation — grammar review, vocabulary building, listening immersion. Use BBC Learning English extensively. Weeks 4-6: Introduce IELTS-specific practice. What to Avoid in Free IELTS Prep

Not all free resources are equal. Avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Outdated materials: IELTS format changes periodically. Ensure resources are current for 2026.
  • Unqualified advice: Many YouTube channels and blogs provide incorrect information. Verify against official sources.
  • Passive consumption: Watching videos without practising actively yields minimal improvement. Always practise what you learn.
  • Inconsistent study: Sporadic studying is ineffective. Maintain a regular schedule even if daily time is limited.
  • Ignoring weak areas: It is tempting to practise what you enjoy. Focus extra time on your weakest sections.

When to Consider Paid Resources

While free preparation is sufficient for most candidates, some situations may justify investment:

  • Repeated test failures: If you have taken IELTS multiple times without achieving your target, a targeted paid course may identify issues free resources have not addressed.
  • Extremely tight deadlines: If you need your target score within weeks, intensive paid preparation may accelerate progress.
  • Specific weaknesses: If one section is far below target, a few hours of targeted tutoring may be worthwhile.
  • Lack of self-discipline: If you struggle to study independently, a structured paid course provides external accountability.

Even in these cases, begin with free resources and add paid support only where genuinely needed.

Free Speaking Practice Strategies

Speaking is often the hardest section to practise alone. These free strategies help:

  1. Shadowing: Listen to native English speakers (BBC, podcasts) and speak along with them, mimicking pronunciation and intonation.
  2. Self-recording: Record yourself answering every Part 2 cue card from the current topic list. Listen back critically.
  3. Language exchange: Use free apps to find English speakers learning your language. Exchange 30 minutes of each language.
  4. Online communities: Join free IELTS Discord servers and Facebook groups where members practise speaking together via video call.
  5. AI speaking practice: Use Anglotec AI's free speaking tool for structured mock speaking tests with feedback.

Free Writing Improvement Methods

Writing improvement requires feedback. These free methods provide it:

  1. AI feedback: Submit essays to Anglotec AI for instant, detailed feedback on all four criteria.
  2. Peer review: Exchange essays with study partners in online IELTS communities. Reviewing others' work also improves your own.
  3. Self-assessment: Use official IELTS band descriptors to score your own essays. Be honest and critical.
  4. Model analysis: Study model answers from reliable sources. Analyse why they are effective. Imitate their structure and techniques.
  5. Daily practice: Write something every day — even a paragraph. Consistency produces more improvement than occasional intensive sessions.
