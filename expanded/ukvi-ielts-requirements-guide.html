What is UKVI IELTS?

UKVI IELTS (IELTS for UK Visas and Immigration) is a specific version of the IELTS test that has been approved by the UK Home Office for visa and immigration applications. If you are applying for a UK visa and need to prove your English language proficiency, you must take your English test at an approved Secure English Language Test (SELT) provider, and UKVI IELTS is the most widely recognised option.

The UK government introduced the SELT framework to ensure that all visa applicants meet consistent English language standards. Only tests taken at approved centres and explicitly designated as SELT tests are accepted for visa applications. A regular IELTS test taken at a non-SELT centre cannot be used for your visa application.

UKVI IELTS is available in two formats:

  • IELTS Academic UKVI - For those applying for higher education or professional registration (e.g., doctors, nurses)
  • IELTS General Training UKVI - For those migrating to the UK or applying for work experience, training programmes, or secondary education

In addition, the IELTS Life Skills test is available for specific visa categories that only require speaking and listening skills at levels A1, A2, or B1 of the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR).

UKVI IELTS vs Regular IELTS

Understanding the difference between UKVI IELTS and regular IELTS is crucial for visa applicants. While the test content, format, and difficulty level are identical, there are important administrative differences:

Visa Types & Score Requirements

The English language requirement varies significantly depending on which UK visa you are applying for. The following sections detail the specific requirements for each major visa category.

Skilled Worker Visa (formerly Tier 2 General)

The Skilled Worker Visa is the primary route for skilled professionals coming to work in the UK, including doctors, nurses, engineers, IT professionals, and teachers.

Standard Requirement

B1

CEFR Level B1 (Intermediate) in all four skills: reading, writing, listening, and speaking.

IELTS Equivalent

4.0

Overall band score of at least 4.0 in IELTS for UKVI. Individual band scores may vary depending on the SELT provider used.

Most applicants meet this requirement through their SELT certificate. However, many regulated professions (especially healthcare) have their own higher English language requirements that supersede the visa minimum:

  • Doctors (GMC registration) - IELTS 7.5 overall (7.0 in each band) or OET Grade B
  • Nurses (NMC registration) - IELTS 7.0 overall (7.0 in reading, listening, speaking; 6.5 in writing) or OET Grade B
  • Dentists (GDC registration) - IELTS 7.0 overall or OET Grade B
  • Pharmacists (GPhC registration) - IELTS 7.0 overall or OET Grade B

If your profession requires a higher standard, meeting that professional requirement will automatically satisfy the visa English requirement.

Student Visa (formerly Tier 4)

The Student Visa route covers all international students coming to the UK for higher education. Requirements vary depending on the level of study:

Course LevelCEFR LevelIELTS Equivalent
Degree level and above (NQF Level 6+)B25.5 overall (minimum 5.5 in each band)
Below degree level (NQF Level 3-5)B14.0 overall (minimum 4.0 in each band)
Pre-sessional coursesVariesAs specified by the institution

Most UK universities set their own English language requirements that are higher than the visa minimum. Typical university requirements are:

  • Russell Group universities - IELTS 6.5 to 7.5 overall
  • Other universities - IELTS 6.0 to 6.5 overall
  • Foundation programmes - IELTS 5.0 to 5.5 overall

Always check the specific requirements of your chosen university before taking your test. The university's requirements will take precedence if they are higher than the visa minimum.

Family Visa (Spouse / Partner / Fiancé)

If you are applying to join a partner or spouse who is a British citizen or settled person in the UK, you must demonstrate English language ability at CEFR Level A1 for your initial application.

Two-stage requirement: You need A1 for your initial 30-month visa, and you must pass A2 level when you apply to extend your visa after 2.5 years in the UK. After 5 years, when applying for indefinite leave to remain (settlement), you will need B1 level.

The most efficient approach for family visa applicants is to take the IELTS Life Skills A1 test, which only assesses speaking and listening - the two skills required at this level. This test takes approximately 16-18 minutes and costs less than the full IELTS test.

Approved SELT Providers

The UK Home Office approves four SELT providers for visa applications. You must take your test with one of these providers at an approved test centre:

IELTS SELT Consortium

IELTS for UKVI and IELTS Life Skills. Most widely available with centres in 140+ countries.

LanguageCert

LanguageCert International ESOL SELT. Available at B1, B2, C1, and C2 levels. Online and in-person options.

Pearson (PTE)

PTE Academic UKVI and PTE Home (A1, A2, B1). Computer-based testing with fast results (typically 48 hours).

Trinity College London

SELT tests at GESE (Speaking & Listening) and ISE (all four skills) levels. Available at centres in UK and internationally.

PSI Services

Skills for English UKVI (newer provider, B1 to C2 levels). Computer-delivered tests.

Each provider offers different advantages in terms of test format, availability, result speed, and cost. IELTS remains the most widely recognised and accepted, particularly for professional registration purposes.

How to Book UKVI IELTS

Booking your UKVI IELTS test is straightforward if you follow these steps carefully:

1

Check Your Visa Requirements

Confirm the exact CEFR level and test type required for your specific visa application. Check the UK government guidance for the latest requirements.

2

Choose Your Test Type

Select between IELTS Academic UKVI, IELTS General Training UKVI, or IELTS Life Skills depending on your visa route and professional requirements.

3

Find a SELT-Approved Test Centre

Visit the official IELTS website to find your nearest SELT-approved centre. Not all IELTS centres offer UKVI tests, so verify carefully.

4

Book Online and Pay

Complete the online booking form with your passport details. Payment is required at the time of booking. Fees are typically £200-£220 for IELTS UKVI and £150-£160 for IELTS Life Skills.

5

Receive Confirmation

You will receive a confirmation email with your test date, time, and venue. You will also receive details of what to bring on test day.

How to Prepare for UKVI IELTS

Preparing for UKVI IELTS requires the same approach as preparing for any IELTS test, since the content is identical. Here is a structured preparation plan:

Understand the Test Format

The IELTS test has four sections:

  • Listening (30 minutes) - 4 sections with 40 questions covering conversations, monologues, academic discussions, and lectures.
  • Reading (60 minutes) - 3 passages with 40 questions. Academic version has academic texts; General Training has workplace and everyday texts.
  • Writing (60 minutes) - Task 1 (20 minutes) and Task 2 (40 minutes). Academic Task 1 is graph/chart/diagram description. General Training Task 1 is letter writing.
  • Speaking (11-14 minutes) - Part 1 (introduction), Part 2 (long turn/cue card), and Part 3 (discussion).

Test Day: What to Expect

On your test day, you must bring the same passport you used to book the test. No other form of ID is accepted. Arrive at least 30 minutes before your scheduled start time to complete check-in procedures.

The test day typically runs as follows:

  • 08:00 - 08:30 - Arrival and registration (photograph and fingerprint capture)
  • 09:00 - 09:40 - Listening test (with headphones provided)
  • 09:40 - 10:40 - Reading test (no extra time for transferring answers)
  • 10:40 - 11:40 - Writing test
  • Afternoon - Speaking test (scheduled separately, usually on the same day)
Important reminders: No watches, phones, or electronic devices are allowed in the test room. You may bring a clear bottle of water with no label. Pencils and erasers are provided. Listen carefully to all instructions given by the invigilators.

For visa applications, the UKVI only accepts tests that meet or exceed the required CEFR level. Your results are valid for 2 years from the test date. After this period, you will need to retake the test if you have not yet completed your visa application.

Alternatives to IELTS for UK Visa

While IELTS is the most popular choice, you have several other approved options:

  • PTE Academic UKVI - Computer-based test with results in 48 hours. Some candidates find the format more accessible.
  • LanguageCert International ESOL SELT - Available online and in-person. Flexible scheduling.
  • Trinity College London SELT - Particularly good for spoken English assessment. Available at centres in the UK.
  • Skills for English UKVI (PSI) - Computer-delivered, results within 5 days.
  • OET (Occupational English Test) - For healthcare professionals only. Accepted by GMC, NMC, and other healthcare regulators.

If you are a healthcare professional, you should also check whether your regulatory body accepts the OET (Occupational English Test). The OET is designed specifically for healthcare professionals and uses medical scenarios, which many candidates find more relevant to their daily work.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Booking the wrong test - Taking regular IELTS instead of UKVI IELTS is the most common and costly mistake.
  • Not checking university/professional requirements - The visa minimum may be lower than what your university or professional body requires.
  • Not verifying the test centre is SELT-approved - Only specific centres offer UKVI tests.
  • Booking too late - Test dates fill up quickly, especially in high-demand locations.
  • Not preparing for the specific test format - Each SELT provider has a different format; prepare accordingly.
  • Ignoring one skill area - Many candidates focus on their weakest skill but neglect maintaining their strongest.
  • Using expired results - IELTS results are only valid for 2 years for visa purposes.

Expert Preparation Tips

Based on 40 years of experience preparing candidates for English language exams, here are our top tips for UKVI IELTS success:

For the Listening Test

  • Practise with a variety of accents - British, Australian, American, and Canadian accents are all used
  • Read the questions before the audio starts to know what to listen for
  • Write your answers on the question paper first, then transfer them
  • Pay attention to word limits (e.g., "NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS")
  • Check your spelling - incorrect spelling means no mark

For the Reading Test

  • Skim the passage first to get the general idea, then read questions
  • Manage your time - spend no more than 20 minutes per passage
  • Don't leave any answers blank - there is no penalty for wrong answers
  • Pay attention to True/False/Not Given questions - "Not Given" is often the tricky option

For the Writing Test

  • Task 2 carries more marks - allocate 40 minutes to it
  • Plan your essay before writing - 5 minutes of planning saves rewriting
  • Use a clear paragraph structure with topic sentences
  • Vary your vocabulary and sentence structures
  • Leave 2-3 minutes at the end to check for grammar and spelling errors

For the Speaking Test

  • Be natural and conversational - the examiner wants to see your real English ability
  • In Part 2, use the full 2 minutes - keep speaking until the examiner stops you
  • Use the 1-minute preparation time to jot down key points
  • In Part 3, give extended answers with reasons and examples
  • Don't memorise scripts - examiners can tell and it negatively affects your score

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is UKVI IELTS valid for visa applications?

UKVI IELTS results are valid for 2 years from the date of your test. Your test certificate must be valid at the time you submit your visa application.

Can I use regular IELTS for a UK visa?

No. You must take a SELT (Secure English Language Test) at an approved centre. Regular IELTS taken at a non-SELT centre is not accepted for UK visa applications.

How much does UKVI IELTS cost?

As of 2025, IELTS for UKVI costs approximately £200-£220, and IELTS Life Skills costs approximately £150-£160. Prices vary slightly by country and test centre.

How many times can I retake UKVI IELTS?

There is no limit to how many times you can take UKVI IELTS. However, you must wait at least 5 days between test dates if you have already taken the test within that period.

Can I combine two IELTS test results?

Generally, no. You must meet the required scores in a single test for most visa categories. Some professional bodies (like the NMC) allow score combining across two tests, but the Home Office typically does not for visa purposes.

What if I only need to meet the visa minimum, not professional registration requirements?

If your profession requires higher English standards (e.g., doctors, nurses), meeting those requirements will automatically satisfy the visa requirement. You only need to meet the higher standard.

Can I take UKVI IELTS in my home country?

Yes, IELTS for UKVI is available in over 140 countries at approved SELT centres. Check the official IELTS website for your nearest centre.

How long does it take to get UKVI IELTS results?

Results are typically available 13 calendar days after your test. You can view them online using your candidate number, and your TRF will be sent by post.

Do I need to take all four skills for every visa type?

No. Some visa categories only require speaking and listening (e.g., Sportsperson visa at A1 level). Check the specific requirements for your visa route. IELTS Life Skills only tests speaking and listening.

Is UKVI IELTS harder than regular IELTS?

No. The test content, format, and difficulty are identical. The only differences are administrative - UKVI tests are taken at SELT-approved centres with enhanced security procedures.
