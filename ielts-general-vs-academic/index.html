IELTS General Training vs Academic: Complete Comparison

Applying for immigration, a job, or university? The difference between IELTS General Training and IELTS Academic could determine your application's success. Here's everything you need to know.

When Do You Need IELTS General Training?

🇨🇦

Canada Immigration (Express Entry)

IELTS General Training is the standard for Canada's Express Entry system, Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP), and most work permits. Higher CLB levels (Canadian Language Benchmarks) directly increase your CRS score.

🇦🇺

Australia Immigration

General Training is accepted for Australia's General Skilled Migration (subclass 189, 190, 491), employer-sponsored visas, and some permanent residency pathways. Points-based system rewards higher scores.

🇳🇿

New Zealand Immigration

Required for New Zealand's Skilled Migrant Category, work-to-residence visas, and some partnership visas. NZQA accepts both Academic and General for most purposes.

Skilled MigrantWork Visa
🇬🇧

UK General Migration

Some UK visa categories accept General Training, though IELTS UKVI versions are often required. Check your specific visa requirements as Academic may be needed for certain routes.

💼

Work & Training Programmes

Employers and vocational training programmes often require General Training as proof of practical, everyday English ability rather than academic English proficiency.

🏠

Secondary Applicants (Dependants)

In many immigration systems, secondary applicants (spouses, partners) can take General Training while the primary applicant may need Academic for their specific route.

When Do You Need IELTS Academic?

🎓

University Admissions (All Levels)

All universities worldwide require IELTS Academic for undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral admissions. General Training is NOT accepted for university entry. Typical requirements: 6.0-7.5 overall.

🏥

Medical & Nursing Registration

NMC UK, GMC UK, AHPRA Australia, and medical boards worldwide require IELTS Academic. Healthcare professionals must demonstrate academic-level English for patient safety.

⚖️

Professional Registration

Engineering councils, accounting bodies, legal associations, and teaching regulators typically require Academic as proof of professional-level English communication.

🔬

Research & Academic Positions

Postdoctoral research positions, academic fellowships, and research grants typically require IELTS Academic to ensure you can publish papers and present at conferences.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is IELTS General Training easier than Academic? +
Most test-takers find General Training easier because the Reading and Writing sections use everyday language and practical contexts rather than academic topics. However, the Speaking and Listening sections are identical. The scoring scale is the same, so a Band 7 in General Training equals a Band 7 in Academic. That said, immigration authorities and universities understand these differences - you typically can't substitute one for the other.
Can I use IELTS General Training for university admission? +
No. Universities worldwide only accept IELTS Academic for admissions. If you need both university admission and immigration, you should take IELTS Academic, which may satisfy both requirements depending on the immigration programme.
Which test should I take for Canada Express Entry? +
IELTS General Training is the standard for Canada's Express Entry system. Your CLB (Canadian Language Benchmark) level is calculated from your General Training scores. Higher scores directly increase your CRS points. For the Federal Skilled Worker Program, you need CLB 7 minimum (IELTS 6.0 in each section).
Do the Listening and Speaking sections differ? +
No. The Listening and Speaking sections are identical in both IELTS Academic and General Training. The only differences are in the Reading and Writing sections. This means if you're preparing for both tests, your Listening and Speaking preparation is the same.
Can I switch from Academic to General Training after booking? +
No, these are separate test bookings. If you book the wrong test type, you'll need to cancel and rebook. Most test centres charge a transfer fee. Always confirm which test type your institution, employer, or immigration programme requires before booking.
Is there a difference in how scores are calculated? +
Both tests use the same 0-9 band scale. However, the raw score to band conversion differs slightly between Academic and General Training Reading because the question difficulty differs. For example, 30 correct answers in General Training Reading might give Band 6.0, while 30 correct in Academic Reading might give Band 5.5.
