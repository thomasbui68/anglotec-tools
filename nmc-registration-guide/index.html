NMC Registration: The Complete Process

Registering with the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) is a multi-step process that typically takes 6–12 months from start to finish. Here's the complete roadmap to your NMC PIN number:

1

Check Your Eligibility

Before starting your NMC registration, confirm you meet the basic requirements: you must have completed a nursing or midwifery programme of at least 3 years, be registered (or eligible to register) as a nurse in your home country, and have no criminal convictions that would prevent registration.

2

Complete Your English Language Test

The NMC requires proof of English language proficiency. You must achieve IELTS 7.0 in all four sections (Reading, Writing, Listening, Speaking) OR OET Grade B in all four sub-tests. These scores can be achieved across two sittings. Our OET and IELTS courses are designed specifically for nurses.

3

Pass the NMC CBT Exam

The Computer Based Test (CBT) consists of 115 multiple-choice questions covering nursing theory and practice. Part A tests numeracy (15 questions) and Part B tests clinical theory (100 questions). You have 3 hours to complete it and can take it at a test centre in your home country. Passing score is typically around 68%.

4

Submit Your NMC Application

Create an account on the NMC Online portal and submit your registration application. You'll need to provide evidence of your nursing qualification, registration in your home country, English language test results, CBT pass certificate, professional references, and a police clearance certificate. The NMC registration fee is £153.

5

NMC Assessment of Your Qualification

The NMC will assess your nursing qualification to confirm it's substantially equivalent to a UK nursing programme. This process typically takes 2–4 months. They may contact your training institution for verification. If there are gaps in your training, you may be required to complete an adaptation programme.

6

Pass the NMC OSCE Exam

Once your qualification is approved, you'll be invited to book your OSCE (Objective Structured Clinical Examination) at one of 5 approved UK test centres. The OSCE consists of 10 clinical stations testing your practical skills, clinical decision-making, and professional communication. Pass this, and you receive your NMC PIN.

7

Receive Your NMC PIN and Start Work

Congratulations! With your NMC PIN, you're now a registered nurse in the UK and can apply for NHS Band 5 nursing positions. The starting salary is £28,834–£34,581 depending on location. We provide job application support to all our graduates.

Common NMC Registration Mistakes to Avoid

Thousands of nurses delay their NMC registration through avoidable mistakes. Here are the most common errors and how to avoid them:

❌

Incomplete Documentation

The NMC rejects applications with missing or incorrect documents. Double-check every requirement before submitting. Common issues include missing reference letters, unverified translations, and expired certificates.

  • Use our document checklist
  • Get translations certified
  • Check expiry dates on all docs
  • Follow NMC naming conventions
  • Submit clear, scanned copies
❌

Underestimating the CBT

Many nurses assume the CBT will be easy because they're experienced clinicians. The CBT tests UK-specific nursing standards, NMC Code, and UK healthcare policies that may differ from your home country.

  • Start CBT prep early
  • Study the NMC Code thoroughly
  • Practise UK numeracy standards
  • Use our CBT question bank
  • Take timed practice tests
❌

Poor OSCE Preparation

The OSCE has a national pass rate of only 67%. Nurses who fail typically haven't practised under exam conditions or don't understand the marking criteria. Invest in proper OSCE training.

  • Book OSCE prep before arriving
  • Learn the marking criteria inside out
  • Practise with timed scenarios
  • Focus on communication skills
  • Take multiple mock OSCEs

Frequently Asked Questions

▶ How do I start my NMC registration application?

Start by creating an account on the NMC Online portal at nmc.org.uk. Before submitting, ensure you have: your nursing qualification certificate, current registration in your home country, a valid passport, English language test results (or booking confirmation), and contact details for two professional referees. We recommend completing our free eligibility check first at tools.anglotec-ai.com to confirm you meet all requirements.

▶ Can I use IELTS or OET for NMC registration?

Yes — the NMC accepts both IELTS and OET. For IELTS, you need 7.0 in all four sections (Reading, Writing, Listening, Speaking). For OET, you need Grade B (350+) in all four sub-tests. You can combine scores from two sittings for both tests, but no individual score can be more than 0.5 (IELTS) or half a grade (OET) below the requirement. Many nurses find OET easier because it uses healthcare-specific content.

▶ How long is my CBT result valid for?

Your CBT pass result is valid for 2 years from the date you passed. You must complete your NMC registration (including passing the OSCE) within this 2-year window. If your CBT expires before you complete registration, you'll need to retake it. This is why we recommend completing your OSCE as soon as possible after your CBT pass — don't let time run out!

▶ What if my nursing qualification isn't accepted by the NMC?

If the NMC determines your nursing qualification is not substantially equivalent to a UK programme, you have several options. You may be asked to complete an adaptation programme or additional training modules. In some cases, you may need to complete a Return to Practice programme. Our team can advise you on the best pathway based on your specific situation and help you plan your next steps.

▶ Can I work in the UK before completing NMC registration?

No — you cannot work as a registered nurse in the UK without a valid NMC PIN number. However, some NHS trusts offer pre-registration employment programs where you can work in a healthcare support role while completing your registration. These roles typically pay £11–£14 per hour and can provide valuable UK healthcare experience. Contact NHS recruitment agencies for opportunities.

▶ How much do NHS Band 5 nurses earn?

As of 2026, NHS Band 5 nurses earn £28,834–£34,581 per year on the Agenda for Change pay scale. Nurses in London receive additional London weighting (5–20% depending on zone). After 2 years, you can progress to Band 6 (£35,000–£42,000). Additional earnings come from overtime, weekend shifts, and night duty enhancements. The NHS also offers a generous pension scheme, 27–33 days annual leave, and sick pay.

▶ Do I need UK work experience to register with the NMC?

No — you do not need UK work experience to register with the NMC. Your nursing qualification and registration from your home country, combined with passing the required tests (English, CBT, OSCE), are sufficient. However, gaining some familiarity with UK healthcare systems through our courses can significantly help your OSCE preparation and your confidence when starting work.
