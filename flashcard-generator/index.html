Hypertension

/ha?-per-TEN-shun/

High Blood Pressure

A condition in which the force of the blood against the artery walls is too high. Blood pressure is considered high when readings are consistently 140/90 mmHg or higher.

"The patient was diagnosed with hypertension and started on antihypertensive medication."
