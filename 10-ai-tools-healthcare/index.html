Frequently Asked Questions

Will AI replace healthcare professionals?

No. AI is designed to augment, not replace, healthcare professionals. It handles administrative tasks, assists with diagnostics, and provides decision support. The human elements of care, empathy, communication, and complex clinical judgment remain irreplaceable.

Is AI in healthcare safe?

Regulated AI tools undergo rigorous validation before deployment. They are classified as medical devices and must meet MHRA (UK) or FDA (US) standards. However, AI outputs should always be reviewed by qualified clinicians.

Which AI tool should I start with as a nurse?

Start with Anglotec for professional development, exam preparation, and clinical communication. For clinical practice, UpToDate provides excellent decision support. For documentation efficiency, explore voice AI tools if available in your organisation.

How much do healthcare AI tools cost?

Enterprise tools like Nuance DAX and Watson Health cost thousands per clinician annually. However, tools like Anglotec offer substantial free tiers, and Babylon Health is free for patients. Many NHS trusts provide UpToDate and ClinicalKey access to staff.

Do I need technical skills to use healthcare AI?

No. Most healthcare AI tools are designed for clinical users without technical backgrounds. Anglotec, in particular, requires no technical knowledge. Enterprise tools may require brief training from your IT department.

How does Anglotec differ from ChatGPT for healthcare?

Anglotec is specifically trained on healthcare scenarios, medical terminology, NHS protocols, and exam formats. It provides 9,000+ healthcare-specific prompts, understands clinical workflows, and offers features like OSCE simulation that general AI cannot match.
