OET Registration Guide

How to Register for OET: Step-by-Step Guide

Everything you need to know about booking your OET exam: where to register, what documents you need, how much it costs, and how to choose the right test venue.

Start Your Free OET Preparation

Quick Summary

To register for OET, create an account on the official OET website (oet.com), select your profession, choose paper-based or computer-based testing, pick a venue and date, upload your ID, and pay the exam fee (approximately AUD 587). Registration opens 3 months before each test date and closes approximately 3-4 weeks before the test.

OET Registration: Step-by-Step Process

Registering for the OET exam is straightforward if you follow the correct steps. Here is the complete process from start to finish:

1

Create Your MyOET Account

Visit the official OET website and click "Register" or "Book a Test." You will need to provide:

  • ✓Full name (exactly as it appears on your ID document)
  • ✓Valid email address (all communications will go here)
  • ✓Phone number with country code
  • ✓Mailing address
  • ✓A secure password
Important: The name on your OET account must exactly match the name on your ID document. Any discrepancy will prevent you from taking the test, and name changes after registration may incur a fee.
2

Select Your OET Profession

OET offers 12 different versions tailored to specific healthcare professions. Choose the one that matches your registration body requirements:

Nursing - For NMC UK registration
Medicine - For GMC UK registration
Dentistry - For GDC registration
Pharmacy - For GPhC registration
Physiotherapy - For HCPC registration
Radiography - For HCPC registration
Occupational Therapy
Speech Pathology
Dietetics, Podiatry, Optometry, Veterinary Science
3

Choose Paper-Based or Computer-Based OET

OET is available in two formats. Both are accepted by all regulatory bodies:

OET on Paper

  • - Traditional pen-and-paper test
  • - Available at more test venues worldwide
  • - Results in 16 business days
  • - Speaking test on a different day
  • - Familiar format for many candidates

OET on Computer

  • - Taken at a test centre on a computer
  • - Faster results: 10-12 business days
  • - Speaking test via video call on the same day
  • - Typing may be faster for Writing section
  • - Growing number of venues available
4

Select Your Test Venue and Date

OET is offered at over 140 venues across 40+ countries. Use the venue search tool on the OET website to find your nearest location.

How to Choose the Right Venue

  • Proximity: Choose the closest venue to minimize travel stress on test day.
  • Availability: Popular venues fill up quickly, especially in major cities.
  • Format: Not all venues offer computer-based testing. Verify before booking.
  • Frequency: Some venues offer monthly tests; others may have fewer dates.

2025 Test Dates: OET is offered at least once per month at most venues. Check the OET test dates page for specific dates at your chosen venue.

5

Upload Your ID Document

OET requires a valid, government-issued photo ID. The document you upload must be the same one you bring on test day.

Accepted ID Documents

  • ✓ Valid passport (most common)
  • ✓ National identity card (EU/EEA only)
  • ✓ Biometric residence permit (UK only)
  • ✗ Driving licences are NOT accepted
  • ✗ Student IDs are NOT accepted

ID Requirements

  • - Must be valid (not expired) on test day
  • - Must contain a recognizable photograph
  • - Name must match your MyOET account exactly
  • - Upload a clear, high-resolution scan or photo
6

Pay the OET Registration Fee

The OET exam fee is AUD 587 (approximately USD 380 or GBP 310, subject to exchange rates). Payment is made online during registration.

Accepted Payment Methods

  • - Visa and Mastercard credit/debit cards
  • - PayPal
  • - Bank transfer (in select countries, may take 3-5 business days to process)

Note: Registration is not confirmed until payment is received. If paying by bank transfer, allow extra time before the booking deadline.

7

Confirm Your Booking

After payment, you will receive a confirmation email from OET within 24 hours. This email contains:

  • ✓Your unique candidate number
  • ✓Test date, time, and venue address
  • ✓Instructions for test day
  • ✓What to bring on test day
Save your confirmation email. You will need to show it (printed or on your phone) at the test venue along with your ID document.

What to Bring on OET Test Day

Arriving unprepared is one of the most common reasons candidates are denied entry. Use this checklist to ensure you have everything you need:

✅ Required Items

  • ✓
    Valid ID document - The same document uploaded during registration. Passport is safest.
  • ✓
    Booking confirmation - Printed or digital copy from MyOET account.
  • ✓
    HB pencils - For paper-based tests. Bring at least 2-3 sharpened pencils.
  • ✓
    Eraser - Clean eraser for paper-based tests.
  • ✓
    Water bottle - Clear, label-free bottle only.

❌ Prohibited Items

  • ✗
    Mobile phones - Must be switched off and stored in designated areas.
  • ✗
    Smart watches - All watches are prohibited, including analog.
  • ✗
    Bags and purses - Leave at home or store as instructed.
  • ✗
    Food and drink - Except the allowed clear water bottle.
  • ✗
    Pens - Only HB pencils allowed for paper-based tests.

After Registration: Your Next Steps

Once you have secured your OET test date, it is time to prepare strategically. Here is what to do between registration and test day:

12+ Weeks Before Test

If you registered early, use this time to build foundational skills. Focus on medical vocabulary, grammar review, and familiarizing yourself with the OET format. Take a diagnostic practice test to identify weak areas.

8 Weeks Before Test

Begin intensive skill practice. For Writing, practice referral letters and discharge letters specific to your profession. For Speaking, role-play patient consultations daily. Complete at least one full practice test per week.

4 Weeks Before Test

Switch to timed practice. Complete full mock tests under exam conditions. Review all mistakes meticulously. Focus on time management for Reading and Listening. Get feedback on your Writing samples.

1 Week Before Test

Do light review only. Focus on high-yield vocabulary, transition phrases, and test strategies. Prepare your test day bag with ID, pencils, and confirmation. Plan your travel to the venue. Get plenty of rest.
