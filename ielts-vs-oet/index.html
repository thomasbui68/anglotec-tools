Detailed Section-by-Section Comparison

Understanding how each test section differs will help you decide which exam plays to your strengths:

🏆 OET Wins for Nurses

Writing Section Comparison

This is where OET has the biggest advantage for healthcare professionals.

IELTS Writing: You must write a 150-word report describing a graph/chart AND a 250-word argumentative essay on a general topic. Many nurses struggle with the essay format and academic vocabulary.

OET Writing: You write one professional letter (usually a referral, discharge, or transfer letter) based on case notes. This mirrors real nursing work and uses vocabulary you already know.

  • OET uses nursing vocabulary you know
  • IELTS requires academic writing skills
  • OET letter format is predictable
  • IELTS essay topics are random
🏆 OET Wins for Nurses

Speaking Section Comparison

The speaking test format differs dramatically between the two exams.

IELTS Speaking: A 11–14 minute interview with an examiner covering personal topics, a 2-minute presentation on a random subject, and abstract discussion questions. The topics can be anything from hobbies to space exploration.

OET Speaking: Two 5-minute nurse-patient role plays where you interact with a simulated patient in a healthcare scenario. You receive a card explaining your role and tasks before each scenario.

  • OET uses healthcare contexts you know
  • IELTS topics are unpredictable
  • OET tests professional communication
  • Both test fluency and pronunciation

Reading & Listening Comparison

Both sections are more evenly matched, but OET still has advantages for healthcare workers.

Reading: IELTS uses 3 long academic texts on general topics. OET Part A uses 4 short medical texts, Part B uses 6 workplace texts, and Part C uses 2 longer medical articles. OET texts use medical vocabulary you're familiar with.

Listening: IELTS uses general academic recordings with various accents. OET uses healthcare consultations and lectures. Nurses find OET listening easier because the contexts are familiar.

  • OET reading uses medical texts
  • IELTS reading is more academic
  • OET listening uses consultations
  • Both test similar core skills

When Should You Choose IELTS?

Despite OET being our recommendation for most nurses, IELTS is the better choice in these specific situations:

🎓

You Need It for University

If you're planning to study at a UK university (e.g., a master's in nursing), most universities require IELTS specifically. While some accept OET, IELTS is universally recognised by all UK universities. Taking IELTS once covers both your NMC registration and university admission.

✈️

You Need It for Immigration

If you need an English test for UK visa purposes beyond the NMC (e.g., a Skilled Worker visa or settlement application), you'll need IELTS for UKVI. OET is not accepted for general UK immigration purposes — only for professional registration.

💰

Budget Is Your Main Concern

IELTS is significantly cheaper than OET (£185 vs £365). If cost is your primary concern and you're confident in your general academic English skills, IELTS may be the more economical choice. Just remember: failing and retaking costs more than choosing the right test first time.

IELTS Is Better If:

  • You need the test for university admission (not just NMC)
  • You need an English test for UK immigration purposes
  • Your general academic English is stronger than your medical English
  • You want faster results (3–5 days for computer-delivered)
  • There's no OET test centre near you
  • Budget is your primary concern

When Should You Choose OET?

OET is our recommendation for the vast majority of healthcare professionals. Here's why and when it's the clear winner:

🩺 Designed for Healthcare

The Content Is Nursing-Specific

Every section of OET uses healthcare scenarios, medical terminology, and professional communication contexts. You're tested on skills you actually use at work — not on writing essays about climate change or discussing abstract philosophy.

  • Healthcare vocabulary you already know
  • Real workplace scenarios
  • Professional writing formats
  • Nurse-patient communication focus
📈 Higher Pass Rates

OET Has a 78% Nurse Pass Rate

Data consistently shows that healthcare professionals perform better on OET than IELTS. The familiar contexts reduce cognitive load, allowing you to focus on language performance rather than understanding unfamiliar topics.

  • 78% first-attempt pass rate (vs 52% for IELTS)
  • Familiar content reduces test anxiety
  • Professional scenarios feel natural
  • Shorter preparation time needed
⏱️ Faster Preparation

Prepare in Half the Time

Most nurses prepare for OET in 4–8 weeks compared to 8–16 weeks for IELTS. The familiar content means you're learning exam technique rather than building entirely new vocabulary and writing skills.

  • 4–8 weeks typical preparation
  • Focus on exam technique, not new vocabulary
  • Shorter time to NMC registration
  • Less time away from work

Frequently Asked Questions

▶ Can I switch from IELTS to OET if I've already started preparing?

Yes — many nurses switch from IELTS to OET after struggling with the academic format. Your general English skills are transferable, and your nursing-specific vocabulary actually gives you an advantage on OET. Most nurses find the transition relatively easy, especially for the Writing and Speaking sections. We offer OET preparation courses that can get you exam-ready in 4–8 weeks, even if you've been studying IELTS for months.

▶ Can I combine IELTS and OET scores?

No — you cannot combine IELTS and OET scores for NMC registration. You must use scores from one test type only. However, the NMC does allow you to combine scores from two sittings of the SAME test (two IELTS sittings or two OET sittings), provided all scores meet the minimum requirement and were taken within 6 months (IELTS) or 12 months (OET) of each other.

▶ Is OET accepted by the GMC for doctors?

Yes — the General Medical Council (GMC) accepts OET with Grade B in all four sub-tests for doctor registration. This is equivalent to IELTS 7.5 overall with 7.0 in each section. Many doctors find OET easier for the same reasons nurses do: healthcare-specific content, professional writing format, and familiar clinical scenarios in the speaking test. The requirements are the same as for NMC nursing registration.

▶ Does OET cost more than it's worth?

While OET costs roughly double IELTS (£365 vs £185), the higher pass rate means you're less likely to need expensive resits. Many nurses who fail IELTS multiple times end up spending more on resits than they would have on a single OET attempt. Factor in the shorter preparation time (meaning less time not working) and OET often works out more cost-effective overall. Our data shows OET students reach NMC registration an average of 6 weeks faster.

▶ Can I use OET for UK university admission?

Some UK universities accept OET for nursing and healthcare-related courses, but acceptance is not universal. If you need English proof for university admission (especially for non-healthcare courses), IELTS is the safer choice as it's accepted everywhere. Contact your target university's admissions office to confirm their OET policy before making your decision. If in doubt, take IELTS to cover all bases.
