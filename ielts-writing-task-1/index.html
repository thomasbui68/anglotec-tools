Overview of IELTS Writing Task 1

IELTS Writing Task 1 accounts for one-third of your total writing score. You have 20 minutes to write at least 150 words describing visual information (Academic) or writing a letter (General Training). While Task 2 carries more weight, a strong Task 1 score can mean the difference between band 6.5 and 7.0 overall.

Examiners assess your response using four criteria: Task Achievement (25%), Coherence and Cohesion (25%), Lexical Resource (25%), and Grammatical Range and Accuracy (25%). To score Band 7+, you must meet all descriptors at that level across every criterion.

Key Facts at a Glance

  • Time: 20 minutes recommended
  • Word count: Minimum 150 words (no upper limit, but 160-180 is ideal)
  • Score weight: 33% of writing module
  • Assessment: 4 criteria, each worth 25%

All Graph Types Explained

Academic IELTS Task 1 presents various visual formats. Each requires a slightly different approach. Below are all graph types with strategies for each.

Line Graphs

Show trends over time. Identify peaks, troughs, intersections, and overall patterns.

Key language: rose steadily, peaked at, levelled off, fluctuated, plateaued

Bar Charts

Compare quantities across categories. Look for highest/lowest values and significant differences.

Key language: considerably higher, marginally lower, ranked second, accounted for

Pie Charts

Show proportions of a whole. Focus on largest/smallest segments and ratios between them.

Key language: represented, constituted, comprised, the lion's share, a mere

Tables

Present precise numerical data. Extract key figures rather than listing every number.

Key language: stood at, recorded, registered, with figures of, compared to

Process Diagrams

Show stages in a process. Describe sequentially using passive voice and stage connectors.

Key language: the first stage involves, subsequently, following this, the final step

Maps

Show geographical changes over time. Compare before/after using directional language.

Key language: located in the north, adjacent to, demolished, constructed, converted into

Mixed Charts & Multiple Graphs

Some tasks combine two chart types (e.g., bar chart + pie chart). You must identify relationships between the data sets and present an integrated response.

Key language: correlated with, in contrast to, mirrored the trend, while X showed Y

Task 1 Structure & Format

A well-structured response improves your Coherence and Cohesion score. Follow this proven paragraph structure for Academic Task 1:

Paragraph 1: Introduction (1 sentence)

Paraphrase the question prompt. Do not copy it directly. State what the graph shows.

Example: "The line graph illustrates changes in the percentage of people using public transport in three major cities over a 30-year period."

Paragraph 2: Overview (2 sentences)

Identify 2-3 main trends or key features without data. This is crucial for Band 6+.

Example: "Overall, public transport usage increased in all three cities, though at different rates. London saw the most dramatic rise, while Tokyo experienced modest growth."

Paragraph 3: Body Paragraph 1 (3-4 sentences)

Describe the first set of key features with specific data comparisons. Group related information.

Paragraph 4: Body Paragraph 2 (3-4 sentences)

Describe remaining features. Ensure you cover all significant data points.

Grammar for Band 7+

Grammatical Range and Accuracy requires varied sentence structures and minimal errors. Here are the essential grammar patterns:

1. Complex Sentences with Subordinate Clauses

"Although online sales increased in all regions, the growth was most pronounced in Asia, where internet penetration had risen sharply."

2. Passive Voice (especially for processes)

"The raw materials are first heated to 500 degrees, after which they are combined with chemical additives."

3. Comparative & Superlative Structures

"Car ownership was considerably higher in urban areas than in rural regions. London had the highest proportion of households with two or more vehicles."

4. Participle Clauses

"Starting at just 5% in 1990, internet usage rose to over 70% by 2020, surpassing all predictions."

5. Prepositional Phrases for Time

"During the first decade, figures remained relatively stable. By the end of the period, a dramatic shift had occurred."

Writing the Overview Paragraph

The overview is the most important paragraph. Without it, you cannot score above Band 5 for Task Achievement. It must summarise the main trends or most significant features without including specific data.

Golden Rule

Write 2 sentences that capture the "big picture." Ask yourself: What are the 2 most important things this graph shows? Which categories were highest/lowest? What was the overall direction of change?

Bad overview: "In 1990, 20% of people used cars and this rose to 30% in 2000." (includes data)

Good overview: "Overall, car usage increased throughout the period, while bicycle commuting declined significantly. Public transport remained the most popular method of travel." (no data, identifies trends)

Sample 1: Line Graph - Renewable Energy

Band 8.0 Response

Question: The line graph below shows the percentage of electricity generated from renewable sources in four countries from 1990 to 2020.

The line graph illustrates the proportion of electricity produced from renewable sources in Germany, Spain, the UK, and Norway over a thirty-year period from 1990 to 2020.

Overall, all four countries demonstrated an upward trend in renewable energy generation, though the extent of this increase varied considerably. Norway consistently maintained the highest proportion throughout the entire period, while the UK started at the lowest point but experienced the most dramatic growth in the final decade.

In 1990, Norway already led with approximately 55% of its electricity coming from renewable sources. This figure rose steadily to reach nearly 75% by 2020, with a particularly sharp increase between 2005 and 2015. Germany began at around 8% and saw gradual growth until 2010, after which its percentage climbed more rapidly to finish at approximately 35%.

Spain and the UK both started from relatively low bases, at roughly 5% and 2% respectively. Spain's figures fluctuated somewhat during the 1990s before entering a period of sustained growth from 2000 onwards, eventually reaching about 30%. The UK, conversely, remained largely static until 2010 but then underwent a remarkable transformation, surpassing Spain by 2015 and ending at approximately 40% in 2020.

Sample 2: Bar Chart - University Applications

Band 7.5 Response

Question: The bar chart shows the number of university applications received by five faculties at a UK university in 2015 and 2020.

The bar chart compares the volume of applications received by five different faculties at a British university in two separate years, 2015 and 2020.

Overall, application numbers increased across all faculties during the five-year period, with Business and Law experiencing the most substantial growth. Medicine received the fewest applications in both years, while Business remained the most popular choice throughout.

Business received the highest number of applications in both years, rising from approximately 3,500 in 2015 to just over 5,000 in 2020. Law saw a similarly impressive increase, with figures climbing from 2,000 to around 3,200 over the same period. Engineering also recorded growth, albeit more modest, from roughly 1,800 applications to approximately 2,400.

In contrast, Medicine and Arts experienced more moderate increases. Medicine had the lowest number of applicants in both years, with figures rising only marginally from about 800 to 1,100. Similarly, Arts saw applications grow from 1,200 to 1,600, maintaining its position as the second least popular faculty.

Sample 3: Process Diagram - Cement Production

Band 8.0 Response

Question: The diagram below shows the stages and equipment used in the cement-making process.

The diagram illustrates the process by which cement is manufactured, outlining the various stages from raw material extraction to the final packaging stage.

Overall, the process consists of five main stages, beginning with the crushing of limestone and clay and culminating in the packaging of cement. Two distinct materials are required as input, and the production involves both physical and chemical transformations.

In the first stage, limestone and clay are crushed together in a crusher to produce a fine powder. This mixture is then fed into a mixer, where it is blended thoroughly before being passed through a rotating heater. The heating stage is critical, as the intense heat triggers a chemical reaction that transforms the raw materials into a new substance.

Following this, the resulting material is ground in a grinder to produce cement, which is then bagged into large 50kg sacks ready for distribution. The entire process is linear, with each stage feeding directly into the next without any recycling or feedback loops involved.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Avoiding these common errors can raise your score by a full band. Here are the mistakes examiners see most frequently:

Mistake 1: No Overview Paragraph

Many candidates skip the overview and jump straight into details. This caps your Task Achievement score at Band 5. Always include a clear overview as your second paragraph.

Mistake 2: Listing All Data

Trying to mention every number leads to confusion and exceeds word limits. Select only the most significant figures to support your key points.

Mistake 3: Copying the Question

Copying phrases from the prompt does not count toward your word count and demonstrates lack of paraphrasing skill. Always rewrite the introduction.

Mistake 4: Giving Opinions

Task 1 requires objective description only. Never include personal opinions, recommendations, or explanations for why trends occurred (unless asked).

Mistake 5: Repetitive Vocabulary

Using "increase" and "decrease" repeatedly limits your Lexical Resource score. Maintain a list of 15-20 synonyms and vary your language throughout.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many words should I write for Task 1?

You must write at least 150 words. There is no upper limit, but 160-180 words is ideal. Writing too much wastes time that could be spent on Task 2, and increases the chance of errors.

What happens if I don't write an overview?

Without an overview, your Task Achievement score is capped at Band 5, regardless of how good your grammar and vocabulary are. The overview is mandatory for Band 6+.

Can I use personal opinions in Task 1?

No. Task 1 requires objective description of data only. Save your opinions and arguments for Task 2. Never explain why you think a trend occurred unless specifically instructed.

Should I describe every data point?

No. Select the most significant features: highest and lowest values, major changes, similarities and differences between categories. Mentioning every number makes your response list-like and disorganised.

How do I improve my vocabulary score?

Use precise, less common lexical items. Avoid repetition by maintaining a vocabulary bank of synonyms. Use collocations correctly (e.g., "dramatic increase," "significant decline," "remained relatively stable"). Spelling must be accurate.

Is British or American spelling acceptable?

Both are accepted, but you must be consistent throughout your response. Do not mix British and American spellings in the same essay.
