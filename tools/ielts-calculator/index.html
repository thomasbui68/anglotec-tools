    The IELTS overall band score is calculated as the average of your four module scores (Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking). The average is rounded to the nearest 0.5 band. For example, if you score 6.5 in Listening, 7.0 in Reading, 6.0 in Writing, and 7.0 in Speaking, your average is 6.625, which rounds up to 6.5 overall.     IELTS uses a specific rounding convention: scores ending in .25 round up to .5, and scores ending in .75 round up to the next whole band. For example, an average of 6.25 becomes 6.5, while 6.75 becomes 7.0. Scores ending below .25 round down. This IELTS score calculator online automatically applies these official rounding rules to give you an accurate result.

    Understanding Your IELTS Band Score

    The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) uses a 9-band scoring scale to measure English language proficiency. Each of the four test modules — Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking — is scored individually, and your overall band score is the average of these four scores.

    IELTS Band Score Descriptors

    Each band score corresponds to a specific level of English ability:

    • Band 9 (Expert User): Complete fluency with precise, accurate, and nuanced language use.
    • Band 7-8 (Good/Very Good User): Strong command with only occasional errors. Handles complex arguments effectively.
    • Band 5-6 (Modest/Competent User): Adequate command for most situations, though with noticeable errors.
    • Band 3-4 (Limited/Extremely Limited): Basic understanding limited to familiar situations.
    • Band 1-2 (Non-User/Intermittent): Little to no practical ability to use English.

    Tips to Improve Your IELTS Score

    Regardless of your current level, consistent practice is the key to improvement. Focus on your weakest module first, practice with timed mock tests, expand your vocabulary systematically, and seek feedback from qualified IELTS instructors.
