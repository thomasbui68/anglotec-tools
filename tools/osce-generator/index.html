PLAB 2 OSCE Preparation Tips

Essential strategies to help you pass your OSCE exam on the first attempt

💡

Structure Your Consultations

Always follow a clear structure: ICE (Ideas, Concerns, Expectations), red flags, and a management plan. Examiners award marks for structured approaches.

💬

Practice Communication Skills

Breaking bad news, counselling, and explaining diagnoses are heavily tested. Use empathy statements and check understanding regularly.

⏰

Master the 8-Minute Timer

Each station is only 8 minutes. Practice with a timer to ensure you can complete history, examination, and management within time limits.

📝

Know Your Examiner Checklist

Understanding what examiners look for helps you hit every marking point. Review checklists after each practice session.

🚑

Prioritise Emergency Stations

ABC approach is critical in emergency scenarios. Always assess Airway, Breathing, and Circulation first before anything else.

🎓

Study Common Scenarios

Chest pain, abdominal pain, headache, and diabetes are frequently tested. Ensure you have structured approaches for these common presentations.

Frequently Asked Questions

The PLAB 2 OSCE (Objective Structured Clinical Examination) is the second part of the Professional and Linguistic Assessments Board test. It consists of 16 clinical stations, each lasting 8 minutes, where candidates must demonstrate clinical skills including history taking, physical examination, communication, and practical procedures.

PLAB 2 consists of 16 OSCE stations. Two of these are rest stations, leaving 14 assessed stations. Each station lasts 8 minutes with 2 minutes of reading time between stations.
