IELTS Academic vs General Training: Which Do You Need?

A detailed comparison of IELTS Academic and General Training with an interactive decision tree. Find out exactly which version you need for NMC, GMC, university, visa, and work applications.

Take the Free IELTS Diagnostic

Quick Answer

If you are a nurse applying for NMC UK registration, a doctor applying for GMC registration, or applying for university, you need IELTS Academic. If you are applying for a UK visa, permanent residency, or non-academic professional registration, you may need IELTS General Training or IELTS for UKVI. Most healthcare professionals need IELTS Academic.

IELTS Academic vs General Training: Side-by-Side Comparison

Detailed Section-by-Section Differences

The Listening and Speaking sections are identical in both versions. The differences lie in Reading and Writing:

🎧

Listening Section (Identical)

Both Academic and General Training use the exact same Listening test. You will hear 4 recordings:

Section 1

A conversation between two people in an everyday social context (e.g., booking accommodation).

Section 2

A monologue in an everyday social context (e.g., a speech about local facilities).

Section 3

A conversation between up to 4 people in an educational or training context (e.g., students discussing an assignment).

Section 4

A monologue on an academic subject (e.g., a university lecture).

📖

Reading Section (Different)

IELTS Academic Reading

  • - 3 long passages from academic sources (journals, textbooks, newspapers)
  • - Topics: science, history, psychology, environment, technology
  • - Complex vocabulary and abstract concepts
  • - Question types include: matching headings, True/False/Not Given, summary completion, multiple choice
  • - 60 minutes, 40 questions

IELTS General Training Reading

  • - Section 1: 2-3 short everyday texts (notices, advertisements, timetables)
  • - Section 2: 2 work-related texts (job descriptions, training materials)
  • - Section 3: 1 longer general interest passage
  • - Simpler vocabulary, more practical topics
  • - 60 minutes, 40 questions
✍

Writing Section (Different Task 1)

IELTS Academic Writing

Task 1 (20 minutes, 150+ words): Describe, summarize, or explain visual information. You may be given a graph, chart, table, diagram, or process. You need to identify key trends, compare data, and explain stages.

Task 2 (40 minutes, 250+ words): Write an essay responding to a point of view, argument, or problem. Topics are academic and may cover education, technology, health, environment, or society.

IELTS General Training Writing

Task 1 (20 minutes, 150+ words): Write a letter based on a given situation. The letter may be formal (complaint to a company), semi-formal (request to a manager), or informal (letter to a friend).

Task 2 (40 minutes, 250+ words): Write an essay on a general topic. The essay style is the same as Academic Task 2, but topics tend to be more everyday and accessible.

🎤

Speaking Section (Identical)

Both versions use the exact same Speaking test, which is a face-to-face interview with an examiner lasting 11-14 minutes:

Part 1 (4-5 min)

Introduction and interview about yourself, your home, work, studies, and interests.

Part 2 (3-4 min)

Cue card talk. You speak for 1-2 minutes on a given topic with 1 minute preparation time.

Part 3 (4-5 min)

Two-way discussion on abstract issues related to the Part 2 topic.

Can I Switch Between Academic and General Training?

Many candidates wonder if they can take one version and later switch to the other. Here is what you need to know:

Taking Both Versions

Yes, you can take both IELTS Academic and General Training. They are completely separate tests with separate scores. There is no restriction on taking both. However, remember that you cannot combine scores from Academic and General for any single application. NMC and GMC only accept IELTS Academic.

Switching After Taking One

If you took IELTS General Training but now realize you need Academic for NMC registration, you must take the full IELTS Academic test. Your General Training scores will not count toward your NMC application. Plan carefully to avoid unnecessary expense.

Academic is More Versatile

IELTS Academic is accepted for almost all purposes that General Training covers (with the exception of some migration programs that specifically require GT). If you are unsure of your future plans, taking Academic gives you more flexibility.

UKVI Versions Are Separate

IELTS for UKVI (Academic) and IELTS for UKVI (General Training) are separate tests. If you need a UK visa, ensure you book the UKVI version, as standard IELTS is not accepted for visa purposes.
