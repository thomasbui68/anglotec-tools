What Is the PLAB 2 Exam?

The Professional and Linguistic Assessments Board (PLAB) Part 2 is a practical Objective Structured Clinical Examination (OSCE) that assesses whether international medical graduates (IMGs) can apply their medical knowledge and clinical skills at the level of a doctor starting their second year of Foundation Programme training (F2) in the UK.

PLAB 2 consists of 18 scenarios, each lasting 8 minutes, with 2 minutes of reading time between stations. The exam tests history taking, physical examination, communication skills, clinical management, and practical procedures. It is held exclusively at the General Medical Council's clinical assessment centre in Manchester, UK, which means every candidate must travel to England to sit the exam.

For doctors seeking GMC registration and the ability to practise medicine in the UK, passing PLAB 2 is a mandatory step after clearing PLAB 1. Understanding the full financial commitment is essential for proper planning and budgeting.

GMC PLAB 2 Exam Fee

The GMC PLAB 2 exam fee for 2025 is £966. This fee is payable when you book your exam slot through your GMC Online account. It is non-refundable if you cancel within 35 days of your exam date, and you can only reschedule under exceptional circumstances with supporting documentation.

Key facts about the exam fee:

  • The fee must be paid in full at the time of booking
  • You need a valid PLAB 1 pass (within the last 3 years) to book
  • Cancellation more than 35 days before: partial refund minus administrative fee
  • Cancellation within 35 days: no refund unless exceptional circumstances
  • Rescheduling due to GMC cancellation: free transfer to next available slot

Important Note

The £966 fee is set by the GMC and subject to annual review. Always check the official GMC website for the most current pricing before budgeting.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does PLAB 2 cost in total?
The total PLAB 2 exam cost ranges from approximately £1,850 (budget preparation) to £4,600+ (premium preparation). The mandatory GMC exam fee is £966, with travel, accommodation, and courses adding significantly to the total.
Is the PLAB 2 exam fee refundable?
The £966 GMC fee is partially refundable if you cancel more than 35 days before your exam date, minus an administrative fee. Cancellations within 35 days are not refunded unless you provide evidence of exceptional circumstances (serious illness, bereavement, etc.).
Do I need to attend a PLAB 2 course to pass?
While many candidates find courses helpful, they are not mandatory. With disciplined self-study, quality online resources, and regular practice using tools like the OSCE Generator, many IMGs pass without attending expensive face-to-face courses. The key is consistent, targeted practice.
Can I reduce the PLAB 2 preparation cost?
Yes. Strategies include: booking travel early, sharing accommodation, using online courses instead of face-to-face options, utilising free resources, and practising with AI-powered tools like the OSCE Generator. Many candidates reduce costs by 40-60% with smart planning.
How many times can I re-sit PLAB 2?
You can attempt PLAB 2 a maximum of four times. If you fail on your fourth attempt, you must wait 12 months before booking your fifth attempt. Each attempt costs the full £966 exam fee plus associated travel and accommodation expenses.
Are there any hidden costs I should know about?
Common overlooked costs include: UK visa fees (£100-200), currency exchange fees, travel insurance, professional indemnity insurance (£50-100), medical equipment for practice, and post-exam GMC registration fees (£165). Budget an extra £300-500 for these items.
Is there an alternative to PLAB for GMC registration?
Yes. The Medical Licensing Assessment (MLA) is being introduced as an alternative route. Additionally, doctors with postgraduate qualifications recognised by the GMC (such as MRCP, MRCS, MRCPCH) may be eligible for registration without PLAB. Check the GMC website for the most current accepted qualifications.
