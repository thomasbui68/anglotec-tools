What is OET and Why Do Nurses Need It?

The Occupational English Test (OET) is the only English language test designed specifically for healthcare professionals. For nurses seeking registration in the UK, Australia, New Zealand, Ireland, Dubai, Singapore, and other countries, OET for nurses has become the preferred pathway over IELTS because it uses real healthcare scenarios and workplace communication tasks.

Unlike general English tests, OET nursing assesses your ability to communicate effectively in medical settings. The test reflects the actual language demands of nursing practice, including patient consultations, handovers, referral letters, and discharge summaries. This makes it not only more relevant but often easier for healthcare professionals who already have strong medical vocabulary.

Since its introduction in the late 1980s, OET has gained recognition from healthcare regulators worldwide. In the UK, the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) accepts OET as proof of English proficiency for international nurse registration. The required grade is typically C+ in reading and listening, and B in writing and speaking.

The 4 OET Sub-Tests Explained for Nurses

The OET exam consists of four sub-tests: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking. Each is assessed independently, and you must achieve the required grade in each section for nursing registration. Here is a detailed breakdown of each sub-test:

Listening (Approx. 45 min)

Part A: Two consultations with healthcare professionals where you extract specific information. Part B: Six short workplace extracts. Part C: Two longer presentations or interviews on healthcare topics. The listening section uses healthcare accents including British, Australian, and others.

Required Grade: C+ (NMC UK)

Reading (60 min)

Part A: Four short texts requiring rapid information location (15 min). Part B: Six short workplace texts requiring comprehension. Part C: Two longer healthcare passages requiring detailed understanding and inference. Topics are general healthcare rather than nursing-specific.

Required Grade: C+ (NMC UK)

Writing (45 min)

A profession-specific task based on typical workplace situations. For nurses, this is typically a nursing referral letter, discharge letter, or transfer letter based on patient case notes. You must select relevant information and organize it appropriately for the target reader.

Required Grade: B (NMC UK)

Speaking (Approx. 20 min)

A one-on-one role-play with an interlocutor. There are two role-plays, each lasting about 5 minutes. For OET speaking for nurses, you typically play the role of a nurse interacting with a patient, relative, or caregiver. The setting is a community health centre or hospital ward.

Required Grade: B (NMC UK)

OET Grading System for Nurses

OET uses grades from A (highest) to E (lowest). For NMC registration in the UK, nurses need at least Grade B in writing and speaking, and Grade C+ in reading and listening. Some other regulators may have different requirements, so always check with your specific nursing board.

OET Registration for Nurses: Step-by-Step

Registering for the OET exam is straightforward but requires attention to detail. Follow these steps to ensure a smooth registration process:

1

Create Your OET Account

Visit the official OET website at occupationalenglishtest.org and create a myOET account. You will need a valid email address and identification document (passport is preferred). Ensure your name matches exactly what appears on your ID.

2

Select Your Test Date and Venue

OET is offered at test centres worldwide and via OET@Home for online proctored testing. Select your preferred date, giving yourself at least 6-8 weeks of preparation time. Popular dates fill up quickly, so book early.

3

Choose Nursing as Your Profession

When registering, select "Nursing" as your profession. This ensures your writing task and speaking role-plays are nursing-specific. Choosing the wrong profession means you will receive tasks for a different healthcare role.

4

Pay the Test Fee

The OET test fee is approximately AUD 587 (check current rates on the official website). Payment is accepted via credit card. Some employers sponsor OET fees for nurses they are recruiting, so check with your employer.

5

Upload Identification Documents

Upload a clear scan of your passport or national ID. The name on your ID must exactly match your registration name. Any discrepancy will prevent you from taking the test.

6

Receive Confirmation and Prepare

You will receive a confirmation email with your test details. Results are typically available 16 business days after the test. Use the preparation time wisely with structured study using quality OET nursing preparation materials.

Registration Tip: If you are applying for NMC registration in the UK, ensure you understand the exact grade requirements before booking. The NMC requires Grade B in writing and speaking, and C+ in reading and listening. You can combine scores from two test sittings under certain conditions, known as OET "test combining."

OET Writing for Nurses: Mastering the Referral Letter

The OET writing nursing referral letter is the sub-test that causes the most difficulty for nurses. Only about 55% of candidates achieve Grade B on their first attempt. Understanding the format, assessment criteria, and common pitfalls is essential for success.

Understanding the Writing Task

In the OET writing sub-test for nurses, you are given patient case notes and a task statement describing the recipient and purpose of the letter. Common letter types include referral letters to specialists, discharge letters to GPs, transfer letters to other facilities, and letters to community nursing services.

You have 45 minutes to read the case notes, select relevant information, and write a letter of approximately 180-200 words. The challenge is not writing enough, it is selecting the right information from the extensive case notes provided. Typically, only about 30-40% of the case note information should be included in your letter.

OET Writing Assessment Criteria

Your letter is marked against six criteria, each scored from 0 to 7:

The 6 OET Writing Criteria:

  • Purpose - Is the purpose of the letter immediately apparent and sufficiently described?
  • Content - Have you included all relevant case note information and excluded irrelevant details?
  • Conciseness & Clarity - Is the information presented efficiently without unnecessary words or repetition?
  • Genre & Style - Is the tone appropriate (formal, polite, professional) and suitable for the reader?
  • Organisation & Layout - Is the letter well-structured with logical paragraphing and appropriate sequencing?
  • Language - Is grammar, vocabulary, spelling, and punctuation accurate and effective?

Sample Nursing Referral Letter Structure

Here is the recommended structure for an OET writing nursing referral letter:

Referral Letter Template for Nurses

[Date]

Dr. [Recipient Name]
[Recipient Address/Department]

Re: [Patient Name], DOB: [Date of Birth]

Dear Dr. [Name],

Purpose: I am writing to refer [Patient Name], who requires [specific reason for referral].

Medical History: [Select relevant medical history from case notes - 2-3 sentences]

Current Condition: [Presenting complaint and relevant examination findings]

Management to Date: [What has been done so far]

Reason for Referral: [Clear statement of why you are referring]

Please do not hesitate to contact me if you require any further information.

Yours sincerely,
[Your Name and Title]
[Your Position]

OET Speaking for Nurses: Role-Play Strategies

OET speaking for nurses involves two simulated nurse-patient or nurse-carer interactions. Unlike general English speaking tests, OET speaking assesses your clinical communication skills, empathy, and ability to explain medical information clearly to patients.

Understanding the Speaking Structure

The speaking sub-test begins with a short warm-up conversation (not assessed) about your professional background. Then you complete two role-plays, each lasting approximately 5 minutes. You receive a role-play card 2-3 minutes before each role-play begins, giving you preparation time.

Each role-play card describes the setting, your role, the patient's situation, and the tasks you need to accomplish. Common OET speaking for nurses scenarios include explaining a medication change, discussing discharge plans, addressing a patient concern about treatment, or educating a patient about managing a chronic condition.

Key Skills for OET Nursing Speaking

Essential Speaking Skills for Nurse Role-Plays:

  • Rapport Building - Greeting the patient warmly and establishing trust
  • Explaining Clearly - Using plain language to explain medical terms
  • Empathy - Showing understanding of the patient's feelings and concerns
  • Reassurance - Providing appropriate comfort and confidence
  • Checking Understanding - Asking patients to confirm they understand
  • Task Completion - Addressing all items on the role-play card
  • Professional Tone - Maintaining appropriate professional boundaries
  • Structure - Organizing the conversation logically

Sample OET Speaking Role-Play Card for Nurses

Understanding the role-play card format is crucial. Here is what a typical nursing speaking task looks like:

Sample Role-Play Card: Nursing

Setting: Hospital ward
Your Role: Registered Nurse

Patient: Mr. James Cooper, 68 years old, admitted yesterday with exacerbation of COPD. He is due to be discharged tomorrow with new medications.

Tasks:
• Explain the changes to his medication regimen
• Teach him correct inhaler technique
• Discuss warning signs that require immediate medical attention
• Address any concerns he may have about managing at home

Language Patterns for Nursing Speaking

Successful OET speaking candidates use specific language patterns that demonstrate clinical communication competence:

Empathy phrases: "I understand this must be worrying for you," "It is completely normal to feel concerned about...," "I can see this has been difficult." Explanation phrases: "What this means is...," "In simple terms...," "To put it another way..." Checking phrases: "Does that make sense?," "Do you have any questions about what we have discussed?," "Is there anything else you would like me to explain?"

OET Listening and Reading: Strategies for Nurses

While OET nursing candidates often focus on writing and speaking, listening and reading are equally important. Both require Grade C+ for NMC registration and should not be underestimated.

OET Listening Strategies

The listening sub-test consists of three parts with a total of 42 questions. The audio is played once only, making concentration and effective note-taking essential.

Part A (Consultation Extracts) requires you to complete health professional notes while listening to two consultations. Practice writing quickly and using abbreviations common in nursing documentation. Listen for specific information such as symptoms, dates, medication names, and dosages.

Part B (Short Workplace Extracts) tests your ability to understand the gist, detail, opinion, and purpose of short audio clips. These might include handovers, team briefings, or patient requests. Read the question and options carefully before the audio plays.

Part C (Presentation Extracts) involves two longer recordings of healthcare presentations or interviews. These test your ability to follow extended speech, understand opinions and attitudes, and identify main ideas and supporting details.

OET Reading Strategies

The reading sub-test has three parts completed in 60 minutes. Effective time management is crucial.

Part A (Expeditious Reading) gives you 15 minutes to answer 20 questions across four short texts. This is a speed-reading task. Skim the questions first, then scan the texts for specific information. Do not read every word. Practice this under timed conditions.

Part B (Careful Reading) contains six short workplace texts such as policy extracts, guidelines, or memos, each with one multiple-choice question. The texts are profession-generic but healthcare-related. Read the text carefully and eliminate obviously wrong options.

Part C (Careful Reading) consists of two longer healthcare articles with eight multiple-choice questions each. These test deeper comprehension including inference, attitude, and reference. Read the questions before diving into the passage, and pay attention to qualifying words like "all," "some," "never," and "always."

Common Mistake: Many nurses perform well in Parts B and C but lose marks in Part A due to poor time management. You only have 15 minutes for Part A. Practice speed-reading techniques and do not spend too long on any single question. If stuck, move on and return if time permits.

OET Preparation Tips for Nurses: 8-Week Study Plan

Effective OET for nurses preparation requires a structured approach. Based on 38 years of language teaching experience, we recommend this 8-week preparation plan:

1

Weeks 1-2: Assessment and Foundation

Take a full diagnostic practice test to identify your weakest areas. Review the OET format thoroughly. Build your medical vocabulary with nursing-specific terms. Focus on understanding the writing task format and speaking role-play structure.

2

Weeks 3-4: Skill Building

Practice one writing task every two days, using sample case notes. Focus on selecting relevant information and organizing it logically. Do daily listening practice using healthcare podcasts and OET listening materials. Practice reading under timed conditions.

3

Weeks 5-6: Intensive Practice

Increase to daily writing practice with feedback. Use our AI speaking tool for role-play practice at least 3 times per week. Take full listening and reading tests under timed conditions. Review and learn from all mistakes.

4

Weeks 7-8: Exam Simulation and Refinement

Take complete mock exams under exam conditions. Focus on eliminating recurring errors in writing. Refine your speaking introduction and closing patterns. Ensure you can complete all reading sections within the 60-minute time limit comfortably.

Common Mistakes Nurses Make in OET

Avoiding common errors is one of the fastest ways to improve your OET score. Here are the mistakes we see most frequently:

1. Including All Case Notes in Writing

The most common writing mistake is copying too much from the case notes. Only include information relevant to the letter recipient and purpose. Including irrelevant details loses marks under the Content and Conciseness criteria.

2. Using Informal Language

OET writing requires formal professional language. Avoid contractions (don't, can't), colloquialisms, and overly casual phrases. Use full forms and professional medical terminology appropriate for the reader.

3. Poor Letter Structure

Letters without clear paragraphing, missing purpose statements, or illogical sequencing lose Organisation marks. Always include a clear opening stating the purpose, body paragraphs with logical flow, and an appropriate closing.

4. Speaking Too Fast or Too Slow

In speaking, nervous candidates often speak too fast, making errors, or too slowly, sounding unnatural. Aim for a natural, moderate pace. Pause between ideas. Remember, fluency does not mean speed.

5. Not Checking Understanding

Failing to check patient understanding in speaking role-plays is a common error. Always ask "Does that make sense?" or "Do you have any questions?" This demonstrates effective clinical communication.

6. Medical Jargon Without Explanation

In speaking, using medical jargon without explaining it in simple terms loses marks. Remember, you are speaking to patients, not colleagues. Explain "hypertension" as "high blood pressure" and "myocardial infarction" as "heart attack."

7. Ignoring Listening Part A Instructions

Part A instructions specify exactly how many words you can use (typically 1-3 words). Writing more than the allowed word count means your answer is marked wrong, even if the content is correct.

8. Poor Time Management in Reading

Spending too long on Part A leaves insufficient time for Parts B and C. Remember Part A is only 15 minutes. Practice with a timer and learn to move on from difficult questions.

Pro Tip from Anglotec Instructors: The biggest differentiator between Grade C+ and Grade B in writing is conciseness. Practice reducing your letters to 180-200 words without losing essential information. Every unnecessary word is a potential mark lost.

Frequently Asked Questions About OET for Nurses

What is the OET test for nurses?

OET (Occupational English Test) for nurses is an English language proficiency test designed specifically for healthcare professionals. It assesses your ability to communicate effectively in nursing contexts through four sub-tests: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking, all using healthcare-related content and scenarios.

What grade do nurses need in OET for NMC registration?

For NMC registration in the UK, nurses need at least Grade B in the writing and speaking sub-tests, and Grade C+ in the reading and listening sub-tests. These grades must be achieved in a single sitting or through test combining within a 6-month window.

Is OET easier than IELTS for nurses?

Most nurses find OET for nurses easier than IELTS because the content is healthcare-specific and uses familiar medical vocabulary. The writing task (a referral letter) and speaking role-plays reflect real nursing work, making the test more relevant and less abstract than IELTS.

How do I write an OET nursing referral letter?

An OET writing nursing referral letter requires selecting relevant patient information from case notes and organizing it into a professional letter. Include the purpose clearly, relevant medical history, current condition, management to date, and reason for referral. Keep the letter between 180-200 words and use formal, professional language.

What happens in OET speaking for nurses?

OET speaking for nurses involves two 5-minute role-plays where you act as a nurse communicating with a patient, relative, or carer. You receive a role-play card beforehand describing the scenario and tasks. The interlocutor plays the patient. You are assessed on clinical communication skills, language accuracy, and relationship building.

Can I combine OET scores from different test dates?

Yes, the NMC accepts OET score combining. You can combine scores from two test sittings taken within a 6-month window. You must achieve at least the minimum required grade in all four sub-tests across the two sittings, and no single sub-test grade can be below C+ in either sitting.

How long is OET valid for nursing registration?

OET results are valid for two years for most nursing registration bodies. However, always check with your specific regulator as requirements can change. The NMC in the UK currently accepts OET results that are within two years at the point of application.

How much does the OET exam cost for nurses?

The OET exam fee is approximately AUD 587 (around USD 390 or GBP 310, subject to exchange rates). This covers all four sub-tests. Resitting individual sub-tests is possible at a reduced cost if you do not achieve the required grades on your first attempt.
