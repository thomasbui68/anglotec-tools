The PLAB Route: Step-by-Step

Your pathway to practising medicine in the United Kingdom

Step 1: Verify Primary Medical Qualification

GMC checks your medical degree is acceptable. Most WHO-recognised degrees qualify. Processing: 2-4 weeks.

Step 2: English Language Test

Pass IELTS (7.5 overall, 7.0 each) or OET (Grade B all sub-tests). Required before booking PLAB 1.

Step 3: PLAB 1 Exam

180 MCQs over 3 hours. Tests clinical knowledge. Held 4 times/year worldwide. Cost: ~£247. Pass mark varies (~60-65%).

Step 4: PLAB 2 Exam (UK Only)

18-station OSCE clinical exam in Manchester. Tests practical skills and communication. Cost: ~£906. Must pass within 2 years of PLAB 1.

Step 5: Apply for GMC Registration

Submit PLAB certificates, references, and declarations. Fee: ~£165. Processing: 2-5 working days.

Step 6: Apply for NHS Jobs

Apply for FY2 stand-alone posts, trust-grade roles, or specialty training. Secure job offer for visa.

The USMLE Route: Step-by-Step

Your pathway to residency and medical practice in the United States

Step 1: USMLE Step 1

280 MCQs over 8 hours. Tests basic sciences. Pass/Fail since 2022. Cost: ~$660. Preparation: 4-8 months.

Step 2: USMLE Step 2 CK

~318 questions over 9 hours. Tests clinical knowledge. Scored 1-300. Cost: ~$660. Preparation: 3-6 months.

Step 3: ECFMG Certification

Verify credentials through ECFMG. Required for IMG residency eligibility. Processing: variable.

Step 4: US Clinical Experience (USCE)

Observerships, externships, or research. Critical for competitive specialties. 3-12 months typically.

Step 5: ERAS Application + NRMP Match

Apply through ERAS. Interview at programs. Enter NRMP Match. Match Day in March. Cost: ~$400+ per specialty.

Step 6: Residency Training (3-7 years)

PGY-1 through PGY-3 to 7 depending on specialty. USMLE Step 3 taken during PGY-1. Salary: $60K-80K/year.

Choose PLAB If...

💰

You Want Lower Upfront Costs

The PLAB route costs roughly £2,500-3,500 total vs $8,000-25,000+ for USMLE. This makes the UK significantly more accessible for doctors from developing countries.

🏠

Work-Life Balance Matters

UK doctors work max 48 hours/week. The NHS prioritises doctor wellbeing. US residents commonly work 60-80+ hours. If lifestyle and family time are priorities, the UK wins.

⚡

You Want a Faster Route

From start to GMC registration, PLAB can be completed in 6-12 months. USMLE typically takes 2-4 years before you start earning. If time is critical, PLAB is faster.

🛂

You Want Clearer Immigration

The UK's Health and Care Worker visa is straightforward for doctors with job offers. US H-1B visas involve a lottery system with no guarantee. UK permanent residence is achievable in 5 years.

🎯

Less Competitive Matching

There is no central "Match" in the UK. You apply directly to NHS trusts. While competition exists, you're not competing in a single national matching system with 50-60% IMG match rates.

🇬🇧

You Prefer the UK Healthcare Model

The NHS is a single-payer system with universal coverage. If you align with this philosophy and want to practise in a public healthcare system, the UK is your destination.

Choose USMLE If...

💵

Maximum Earning Potential

US specialists earn $250,000-600,000+ annually - significantly more than UK consultants (£85,000-120,000+). If long-term financial return justifies the upfront investment, the US offers unmatched earning potential.

🔬

You Want Cutting-Edge Research

US medical schools and teaching hospitals lead global medical research. If academic medicine, research fellowships, or pioneering clinical trials are your goals, the US offers unparalleled opportunities.

🌍

Global Career Flexibility

US board certification is recognised worldwide. A US-trained doctor can practise in the Middle East, Singapore, Australia, and beyond with relative ease. UK GMC registration has more limited international transferability.

🏥

Specific Competitive Specialties

If you're targeting orthopaedic surgery, dermatology, ophthalmology, or interventional cardiology, US training is considered the gold standard and offers the best fellowship opportunities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I pursue both PLAB and USMLE simultaneously? +
Technically yes, but it's not recommended. Both pathways require intense preparation and significant financial investment. Focus on one route based on your career goals, financial situation, and personal circumstances. Many doctors who fail the USMLE Match pivot to PLAB as a backup.
Is PLAB easier than USMLE? +
PLAB is generally considered less difficult than USMLE. PLAB tests clinical management at a level equivalent to a UK medical school graduate, while USMLE Step 1 and 2 CK test basic and clinical sciences at a deeper level. However, "easier" is relative - both require dedicated preparation.
What is the IMG match rate for US residency? +
The overall IMG match rate in the NRMP is approximately 50-60%, but this varies dramatically by specialty. Internal Medicine and Family Medicine have higher IMG match rates (60-70%), while competitive specialties like Dermatology, Orthopaedic Surgery, and Ophthalmology have match rates below 10% for IMGs.
Do I need OET or IELTS for PLAB? +
Yes. The GMC requires proof of English proficiency before you can book PLAB 1. Accepted tests: IELTS Academic (7.5 overall with 7.0 in each section) or OET (Grade B in all 4 sub-tests). Many doctors find OET easier because it uses medical scenarios.
How long does the entire PLAB process take? +
From starting preparation to GMC registration: typically 6-12 months. This includes: 2-4 months for English test prep and exam, 2-3 months for PLAB 1 prep and exam, 2-3 months for PLAB 2 prep and exam (in UK), plus 1-2 months for GMC application processing.
Can I work while preparing for PLAB in the UK? +
You cannot work as a doctor without GMC registration. However, some doctors come to the UK on a visitor visa for PLAB 2, then switch to a Health and Care Worker visa once they secure a job offer. You cannot work in any capacity on a standard visitor visa.
What happens if I fail PLAB? +
You can retake PLAB 1 unlimited times, but must pass PLAB 2 within 2 years of passing PLAB 1. There is a limit of 4 attempts for PLAB 2. After 4 failures, you must wait 12 months before reapplying. Each attempt requires paying the full exam fee again.
