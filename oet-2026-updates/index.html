Overview of OET 2026 Changes

The Occupational English Test (OET) remains the gold standard English language assessment for healthcare professionals seeking to work in English-speaking environments. In 2026, several important updates have been introduced that affect nurses, doctors, and other healthcare professionals. Understanding these changes is crucial for international nurses pursuing registration with the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) in the UK, AHPRA in Australia, or other regulatory bodies.

OET 2026 brings updates across all four sub-tests, expanded delivery options, and enhanced support for candidates. The test continues to be recognised by healthcare regulators and employers worldwide, with particular strength in the UK, Australia, New Zealand, Ireland, Singapore, and Dubai. For nurses specifically, OET remains one of the most popular English language tests due to its healthcare-specific content that reflects real workplace scenarios.

The 2026 updates have been designed to make the test more accessible, more reflective of contemporary healthcare communication, and better aligned with the evolving needs of healthcare regulators. These changes build on OET's established reputation as the only English test specifically designed for healthcare professionals.

OET Format Changes for Nurses 2026

The overall structure of OET remains consistent in 2026, with four sub-tests assessing Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking skills in a healthcare context. However, several content and format refinements have been introduced to keep the test current with healthcare communication practices.

Test Duration Adjustments

The total test duration remains approximately 3 hours, but the allocation within sub-tests has been slightly adjusted. The Listening sub-test continues at approximately 45 minutes, while the Reading sub-test timing has been optimised to 60 minutes. The Writing sub-test remains at 45 minutes, and the Speaking sub-test at approximately 20 minutes. These timings now include clearer on-screen timers for computer-based tests, helping candidates manage their time more effectively.

Healthcare Context Updates

OET's defining feature is its use of real healthcare scenarios. In 2026, these scenarios have been updated to reflect contemporary healthcare settings. Listening extracts now include telehealth consultations, electronic health record discussions, and multidisciplinary team meetings conducted via video conferencing. Reading passages address topics such as patient safety technology, AI-assisted diagnostics, and personalised medicine approaches. These updates ensure that OET preparation also builds relevant professional knowledge.

New Question Types in Reading

The Reading sub-test Part C has introduced a new question type that assesses the ability to evaluate multiple sources of information. Candidates may be presented with two short texts on a related topic and asked to identify agreements, disagreements, or complementary information between the sources. This new format reflects the real-world task of synthesising information from multiple professional sources, a common requirement in clinical practice.

OET Scoring Updates 2026

OET uses a graded scoring system from A (highest) to E (lowest) for each sub-test. In 2026, several updates to score reporting and recognition have been introduced that benefit candidates and receiving institutions.

Numerical Score Reporting

A significant 2026 development is the introduction of numerical score reporting alongside the traditional letter grades. Each sub-test now provides a score out of 500, with grade boundaries clearly defined. This numerical reporting makes it easier for candidates and institutions to understand performance levels and track progress over multiple test attempts. The numerical scores map directly to the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) levels, providing additional context for international recognition.

Enhanced Score Reports

OET score reports have been enhanced in 2026 to provide more detailed performance feedback. Candidates now receive sub-skill breakdowns within each sub-test, identifying specific strengths and areas for improvement. For example, the Speaking score report provides separate indicators for Intelligibility, Fluency, Appropriateness of Language, and Resources of Grammar and Expression. This granular feedback helps candidates target their preparation more effectively.

NMC Score Requirements

The Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) in the UK continues to accept OET for registration purposes in 2026. The required scores remain at least a Grade C+ in Listening and Reading, and at least a Grade C+ in Writing and Speaking. However, the NMC has updated its guidance to explicitly confirm that OET@Home scores are accepted, providing greater flexibility for international nurses. Candidates should always verify the latest requirements on the NMC website, as these can be subject to review.

New Registration Requirements for OET 2026

Registration procedures for OET have been streamlined in 2026, with several changes designed to make the booking process more straightforward and accessible.

Simplified Identity Verification

The identity verification process has been simplified for 2026. Candidates can now verify their identity using a wider range of government-issued documents, including digital identity cards where available. The verification process is completed online before test day, reducing check-in time and administrative burden at test centres.

Flexible Booking Options

OET now offers more flexible booking options, including the ability to change test dates up to 7 days before the scheduled test for a reduced fee. Candidates can also select specific time slots for their Speaking sub-test when taking the computer-based OET, allowing them to schedule around work commitments. This flexibility is particularly valuable for nurses who are often working shift patterns.

Group Registration for Employers

A new group registration system has been introduced in 2026 for healthcare employers sponsoring multiple candidates. This system allows hospitals, care homes, and recruitment agencies to register and pay for groups of candidates simultaneously, with discounted fees for bulk registrations. The system includes candidate management tools for tracking results and progress.

OET Listening Sub-Test Changes 2026

The Listening sub-test assesses the ability to follow and understand a range of health-related spoken materials. In 2026, several updates ensure the test remains relevant to contemporary healthcare communication.

Updated Audio Content

Listening audio extracts have been refreshed for 2026, featuring more diverse healthcare settings and scenarios. Part A (consultation extracts) now includes telehealth consultations alongside traditional face-to-face patient interactions. Part B (short workplace extracts) features discussions about electronic health records, clinical handovers using structured frameworks, and patient safety alerts. Part C (presentation extracts) includes conference presentations on topics such as precision medicine, healthcare AI, and global health initiatives.

Accent Diversity

The 2026 Listening sub-test features a broader range of English accents, reflecting the multicultural nature of healthcare teams in English-speaking countries. In addition to British, Australian, and North American accents, candidates will encounter Irish, Scottish, South African, and New Zealand accents. This diversity prepares candidates for real-world healthcare workplaces where they will communicate with colleagues and patients from diverse linguistic backgrounds.

Question Format Refinements

Note-completion questions in Part A have been refined to include more realistic medical documentation formats. Candidates complete notes using authentic clinical terminology and abbreviations commonly used in healthcare settings. Multiple-choice questions in Parts B and C now feature more nuanced distractors that test detailed comprehension rather than surface-level understanding.

OET Reading Sub-Test Updates 2026

The Reading sub-test evaluates the ability to read and understand different types of text on health-related subjects. The 2026 updates maintain the three-part structure while refreshing content and introducing new assessment elements.

Part A: Expeditious Reading

Part A continues to test the ability to locate specific information from four short texts quickly. In 2026, the text types have been expanded to include medication information leaflets, clinical guideline summaries, hospital protocol documents, and patient discharge instructions. The 15-minute time limit remains, emphasising the skill of efficient information retrieval that nurses use daily in clinical practice.

Part B: Careful Reading (Short Texts)

Part B presents six short workplace texts with one multiple-choice question per text. New text types for 2026 include emails from patients using patient portals, text messages between colleagues, and excerpts from nursing competency frameworks. These updates reflect the evolving communication channels used in modern healthcare settings.

Part C: Careful Reading (Long Texts)

Part C features two longer articles of general healthcare interest. The 2026 update introduces the comparative reading question type described earlier, where candidates synthesise information from two sources on related topics. Articles address themes such as healthcare technology ethics, workforce wellbeing, patient-centred care models, and evidence-based practice developments.

OET Writing Sub-Test Modifications 2026

The Writing sub-test requires candidates to write a healthcare-specific letter based on case notes. For nurses, this is typically a referral letter, discharge letter, or transfer letter. The 2026 updates refine the assessment while maintaining the core letter-writing task.

Updated Case Note Formats

Case notes provided for the writing task now reflect contemporary healthcare documentation practices. Electronic health record printouts, structured assessment forms, and multidisciplinary team meeting summaries are among the new formats candidates may encounter. These realistic formats help candidates develop skills directly transferable to their professional practice.

Refined Assessment Criteria

The OET Writing assessment criteria have been refined for 2026 to provide clearer guidance on performance expectations. The six criteria — Purpose, Content, Conciseness and Clarity, Genre and Style, Organisation and Layout, and Language — remain, but descriptor bands have been clarified with more detailed examples at each level. This transparency helps candidates understand exactly what examiners are looking for.

Letter Type Variety

The range of letter types has been expanded for 2026. In addition to the traditional referral, discharge, and transfer letters, candidates may now be asked to write letters responding to complaints, updating general practitioners on patient progress, or requesting additional services for patients. This variety ensures the test assesses a comprehensive range of professional writing skills.

OET Speaking Sub-Test Evolution 2026

The OET Speaking sub-test uses profession-specific role-play scenarios to assess communication skills. For nurses, the 2026 updates introduce new scenario types while maintaining the two-role-play structure.

New Role-Play Scenarios

Role-play scenarios for nurses have been updated for 2026 to reflect current healthcare priorities. New scenarios include conducting a medication review with a patient via telehealth, explaining AI-assisted diagnostic results to a patient, discussing end-of-life care preferences, and addressing vaccine hesitancy with evidence-based information. These scenarios test not only language skills but also the ability to navigate sensitive conversations that are increasingly common in nursing practice.

Assessment Criteria Refinement

The Speaking assessment criteria continue to assess Linguistic Competence (Intelligibility, Fluency, Appropriateness of Language, Resources of Grammar and Expression) and Clinical Communication Competence (Relationship Building, Understanding and Incorporating the Patient's Perspective, Information Gathering, Information Giving). In 2026, detailed performance descriptors have been expanded to help candidates understand the behaviours that demonstrate each criterion at different grade levels.

Interlocutor Training Updates

OET interlocutors (the examiners who conduct the Speaking sub-test) have received updated training for 2026 to ensure consistent delivery of role-play scenarios and fair assessment. Candidates can expect natural, responsive interaction during role-plays, with interlocutors adapting to their communication style while maintaining the scenario framework.

Computer-Based OET Expansion 2026

The computer-based OET (OET on Computer) continues to expand in 2026, with more test venues and improved technology delivering a smoother testing experience.

Increased Test Centre Availability

OET on Computer is now available at over 150 test centres globally, with new centres opening in India, the Philippines, the Middle East, and Africa. The increased availability means most candidates can find a test venue within reasonable travel distance. Test sessions are offered up to twice per month at each venue.

Interface Improvements

The computer-based test interface has been upgraded for 2026 with clearer navigation, improved audio quality for the Listening sub-test, and a more user-friendly text editor for the Writing sub-test. The reading interface now supports split-screen viewing for Part C comparative questions. These improvements make the computer-based test increasingly attractive for candidates comfortable with technology.

OET@Home Updates 2026

OET@Home, the remote testing option, has been significantly enhanced in 2026 in response to growing demand for flexible test delivery.

Expanded Global Access

OET@Home is now available in over 70 countries, with technical requirements updated to include Windows 11, MacOS 12, and certain Linux distributions. The test uses a secure browser application with AI-powered proctoring that maintains test integrity while allowing candidates to test from home.

NMC Recognition Confirmed

A crucial 2026 update is the explicit confirmation that OET@Home scores are accepted by the NMC for nurse registration. This confirmation provides peace of mind for candidates who prefer remote testing and eliminates any uncertainty about the recognition of online test scores.

NMC Recognition & Requirements 2026

The Nursing and Midwifery Council's recognition of OET for registration purposes continues in 2026, with updated guidance that provides clarity for international nurses.

Current NMC OET Requirements

As of 2026, the NMC requires nurses to achieve at least Grade C+ in the OET Listening and Reading sub-tests, and at least Grade C+ in the Writing and Speaking sub-tests. These requirements must be met in a single sitting or through OET's multiple-sit provision (taking sub-tests across different test dates within a specified timeframe).

Score Combination Rules

The NMC accepts OET scores achieved across two consecutive test dates, provided all four sub-tests are at the required grade within six months. This policy provides flexibility for candidates who may perform strongly in some sub-tests but need to retake others. Detailed guidance on score combination is available on the NMC website.

How to Prepare for OET 2026

Effective OET preparation requires a targeted approach that develops both English language skills and healthcare-specific communication abilities. The following strategies will help nurses prepare successfully for OET 2026.

Use Profession-Specific Materials

Unlike general English tests, OET requires familiarity with healthcare communication conventions. Use preparation materials specifically designed for nursing, including sample letters, role-play scenarios, and listening exercises with clinical content. The official OET website provides free sample tests and preparation tips.

Practice Under Timed Conditions

Time management is critical in OET, particularly for Reading Part A (15 minutes) and Writing (45 minutes). Regular timed practice helps develop the speed and efficiency needed for test day. Take full mock tests to build stamina for the approximately 3-hour exam.

Focus on Weak Areas

Use the detailed score reports available after practice tests to identify specific weaknesses. If Writing is a challenge, focus on letter structure and case note analysis. If Speaking needs improvement, practice role-plays with a study partner or record yourself for self-review.

Develop Clinical Vocabulary

Build a strong vocabulary of clinical terms, medical abbreviations, and professional expressions commonly used in healthcare settings. This vocabulary is essential for all four sub-tests and will also serve you in your nursing career in an English-speaking environment.
