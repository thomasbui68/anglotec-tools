48-24 Hours Before: The Wind-Down Phase

The day before your exam is not for cramming. It's for consolidation, confidence-building, and physical preparation. Your brain needs rest to perform at its peak.

STOP

What NOT to Do

  • Do NOT do full practice tests — you'll exhaust yourself
  • Do NOT learn new vocabulary — you won't retain it
  • Do NOT stay up late studying — sleep is more valuable
  • Do NOT drink alcohol or excessive caffeine
  • Do NOT eat heavy, unfamiliar foods
  • Do NOT panic — you've prepared for this
DO

What TO Do

  • Review your vocabulary list lightly
  • Read through your best essay samples
  • Organise all documents and materials
  • Plan your journey to the test centre
  • Set multiple alarms
  • Do light exercise (walk, stretch)
  • Relax with English music or light reading

The Cramming Trap

Research shows that last-minute cramming increases anxiety without improving performance. Your brain forms memories during sleep, not during cramming. The night before the exam, a rested brain outperforms a tired brain by 20-30%. Trust your months of preparation. The work is done.

Your Hour-by-Hour Exam Day Timeline

Follow this timeline to arrive at the test centre calm, prepared, and ready to perform at your best.

Evening Before

6:00 PM — Stop All Study

Close your books. Light review only: flick through vocabulary cards, read your best writing samples for confidence. No new material. Prepare your bag with required documents (valid ID, pencils, eraser, water bottle). Set 2 alarms. Plan your breakfast and journey.

Evening Before

9:00 PM — Wind Down Routine

No screens for 30 minutes before bed. Read something light in English (a magazine article, not a textbook). Do 5 minutes of deep breathing or meditation. Visualise yourself walking into the test centre calmly and performing well. Sleep by 10:00 PM for 8+ hours of rest.

Morning

6:30 AM — Wake Up Energised

Wake naturally if possible. Drink a glass of water. Do 5 minutes of light stretching. Eat a light, protein-rich breakfast (eggs, toast, fruit). Avoid heavy carbs that make you sleepy. Review 10 vocabulary words while eating. Speak English aloud to yourself to "warm up" your language brain.

Morning

7:30 AM — Final Preparation

Check you have: valid passport or national ID, booking confirmation, pencils (HB), eraser, sharpener, clear water bottle (no label). Use the bathroom. Do 2 minutes of positive affirmations: "I am prepared. I will perform well. I trust my preparation." Leave for the test centre with time to spare.

Arrival

8:30 AM — Arrive at Test Centre

Arrive 30 minutes early. Use the bathroom. Find your seat. Take deep breaths. Chat lightly with other candidates in English to warm up your speaking brain. Do NOT discuss difficult questions or topics — it will increase anxiety. Stay calm and focused on your own preparation.

During Test

9:00 AM — The Exam Begins

Listening first. Put on headphones early and test the volume. Read questions during preview time carefully. Stay calm if you miss an answer — move on immediately. Reading: manage your time strictly (20 min per passage). Writing: plan for 5 minutes before writing. Speaking: smile, breathe, and expand every answer.

Section-by-Section Last-Minute Tips

These tips apply in the final 24 hours and during the exam itself. Use them to maximise every section score.

Listening

Listening Quick Wins

  • Test headphone volume BEFORE the test starts
  • Read ahead during the 30-second previews
  • Predict answer type (name, number, date)
  • Write answers in ALL CAPS
  • Missed an answer? Move on instantly
  • Check spelling in transfer time
  • The answer often comes AFTER a distractor
Reading

Reading Quick Wins

  • Skim passage in 2 minutes first
  • Read questions before reading the text
  • True/False/Not Given: False = contradicts text
  • Not Given = not mentioned anywhere
  • Answer questions in order (usually sequential)
  • Transfer answers with 5 minutes left
  • Never leave a blank — guess if unsure
Writing

Writing Quick Wins

  • Task 2 first (worth double the marks)
  • Plan for 5 minutes before writing
  • Use 4 paragraphs: intro, 2 body, conclusion
  • Include 5+ linking words per paragraph
  • Leave 3 minutes for proofreading
  • Check word count: 150+ Task 1, 250+ Task 2
  • No spelling errors in common words
Speaking

Speaking Quick Wins

  • Arrive early, warm up by speaking English
  • Part 1: 2-3 sentences per answer minimum
  • Part 2: Use the full 2 minutes
  • Part 3: Give opinion + reason + example
  • Smile — it improves your fluency
  • If you don't understand, ask for clarification
  • It's okay to pause briefly to think

The Secret Weapon: Positive Mindset

Studies show that candidates who approach the exam with confidence score 0.3-0.5 bands higher than equally prepared but anxious candidates. Why? Anxiety consumes working memory that should be used for answering questions. Your mindset is a real, measurable factor in your score. Walk in believing you've prepared well — because you have.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I study the night before?

Light review only — 30 minutes maximum. Read through vocabulary lists, review your best writing samples for confidence, and look at sample speaking questions. Do NOT attempt new practice tests or learn new material. Sleep is far more valuable than last-minute cramming.

What should I eat before the exam?

Eat a light, protein-rich breakfast (eggs, toast, fruit, yogurt). Avoid heavy carbohydrates (large pasta meals, pancakes) that can make you sleepy. Avoid excessive caffeine — one cup of coffee is fine, but too much increases anxiety and bathroom breaks.

What if I'm running late?

Contact the test centre immediately. Most centres will NOT admit candidates who arrive after the test has started. This is why arriving 30 minutes early is crucial. Plan your journey with extra time for traffic, public transport delays, or getting lost.

What if I don't understand a Speaking question?

It's perfectly acceptable to ask the examiner to repeat or rephrase the question. Say: "I'm sorry, could you repeat that please?" or "I'm not sure I understand — could you rephrase that?" This does NOT affect your score. It's better to ask than to answer the wrong question.

Can I go to the bathroom during the test?

For Listening, Reading, and Writing: No bathroom breaks are allowed during the timed sections. Go BEFORE the test starts. For Speaking, you can use the bathroom while waiting, but not during the interview itself.

What if I make a mistake in Writing?

Cross it out neatly with a single line and continue. Do NOT use correction fluid or spend excessive time rewriting. Minor errors are normal — examiners expect them. Focus on completing both tasks within the time limit rather than producing a perfect first draft.

When do I get my results?

For computer-delivered IELTS: results in 3-5 days. For paper-based IELTS: results in 13 days. You'll receive an email notification and can view your results online. Your Test Report Form will be mailed to you or available for collection depending on the test centre.
