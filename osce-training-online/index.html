Frequently Asked Questions

▶ What is the NMC OSCE exam and who needs to take it?

The NMC OSCE (Objective Structured Clinical Examination) is the final part of the NMC Test of Competence for internationally educated nurses and midwives seeking registration in the UK. It consists of 10 clinical stations that test your practical nursing skills, clinical decision-making, and professional communication. You must pass the OSCE within 2 years of passing your CBT exam. Our online OSCE course prepares you comprehensively for every station type.

▶ How long should I prepare for the OSCE?

We recommend 4–8 weeks of dedicated preparation for the NMC OSCE. Our OSCE Essential package is designed for 4 weeks, Professional for 6 weeks, and Intensive for 8 weeks. The exact time depends on your recent clinical experience, familiarity with UK nursing protocols, and confidence with practical skills. Our diagnostic assessment at enrolment will recommend the ideal preparation timeline for your situation.

▶ Can I really prepare for the OSCE online?

Yes — our OSCE training online has a 94% first-attempt pass rate. While you need access to basic equipment (stethoscope, BP cuff, etc.) for physical practice, the majority of OSCE preparation is about learning the correct procedures, understanding the marking criteria, and practising your communication skills. Our 1-to-1 video mock sessions let you perform scenarios live with feedback, and our skills videos show every procedure from the examiner's perspective. Many students supplement with in-person skills practice at their workplace.

▶ What changed in the 2026 NMC OSCE blueprint?

The NMC updated its OSCE test blueprint in November 2025 with several key changes: the APIE stations now use a revised marking structure with clearer criteria for each phase; two new skills were added to the skills station bank; professional values stations now include additional scenarios on safeguarding; and the overall exam timing was adjusted. Many older OSCE preparation courses have not updated their materials. Our curriculum was rewritten by NHS trainers specifically for the 2026 blueprint.

▶ Where can I take my NMC OSCE exam?

The NMC OSCE is conducted at approved test centres across the UK. As of 2026, there are 5 approved test centres located in Manchester, Oxford, Northampton, and two locations in London. When you book your exam through the NMC, you'll be able to choose your preferred location and date. Our OSCE preparation course includes a test centre guide with travel information, what to bring, and what to expect on the day.

▶ What happens if I fail the OSCE?

Failing the OSCE means paying £794 for a resit and waiting at least 10 days before you can rebook. More importantly, it delays your NMC registration and your ability to start working as a nurse in the UK. This is why investing in proper OSCE training online is so important — our 94% first-attempt pass rate means most of our students never face this situation. Our Intensive package includes a pass guarantee with free additional preparation if needed.

▶ Do I need any equipment for the online course?

For our online OSCE course, you'll need a computer with a webcam, a stable internet connection, and ideally a basic nursing equipment kit for physical practice (stethoscope, BP cuff, thermometer, syringes for practice). We provide a recommended equipment list on enrolment — total cost is typically under £50. Many students also practise at their workplace using clinical equipment. The course materials, videos, and mock sessions are all delivered online.

▶ How do I book my OSCE exam?

After passing your CBT and English language test, you'll receive an authorisation to test from the NMC. You can then book your OSCE exam through the NMC's designated test provider. Our team guides all Intensive package students through this booking process and helps you choose the optimal test date based on your preparation progress. We recommend booking your exam only after completing at least 70% of your OSCE preparation course.
