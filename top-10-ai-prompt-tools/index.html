Prompt Engineering Best Practices

1. Be Specific and Clear

Vague prompts produce vague outputs. Instead of "Write about diabetes," try "Write a 500-word patient education leaflet about Type 2 diabetes management, including diet, exercise, and medication adherence, at a Year 8 reading level."

2. Assign a Role

Giving the AI a specific role improves output quality. "You are an experienced NHS consultant specialising in cardiology" produces more authoritative medical content than a generic request.

3. Provide Context

Context helps the AI understand your needs. Include background information, target audience, format requirements, and constraints in your prompt for best results.

4. Use Chain-of-Thought

For complex tasks, ask the AI to think step by step. "Explain your reasoning" or "Walk through your process step by step" improves accuracy for clinical reasoning and problem-solving tasks.

5. Iterate and Refine

Your first prompt rarely produces the best output. Treat prompt engineering as a conversation. Refine based on responses, add constraints, and adjust tone until you get the desired result.

6. Save Effective Prompts

Build a personal library of prompts that work well. Tools like Anglotec let you save, categorise, and reuse your best prompts, dramatically improving your productivity over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is prompt engineering?

Prompt engineering is the practice of designing and optimising inputs (prompts) to AI models to produce the most accurate, relevant, and high-quality outputs. It involves understanding how AI models interpret instructions and crafting prompts that guide the model toward desired results.

Do I need to be a programmer to use prompt engineering tools?

No. While some tools like LangChain Hub are developer-focused, most prompt tools including Anglotec are designed for non-technical users. If you can write clear instructions, you can use prompt engineering tools effectively.
