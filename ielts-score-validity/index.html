IELTS Score Validity: How Long is IELTS Valid?

Understand the 2-year validity rule, when you need to retake IELTS, whether you can combine scores, and what NMC, GMC, and UKVI require from international healthcare professionals.

Check Your IELTS Status

Quick Answer

IELTS scores are valid for 2 years from the date of your test. After this period, most institutions and regulatory bodies will no longer accept your certificate. However, some organizations may accept older scores on a case-by-case basis if you can demonstrate continuous English use. For NMC and GMC registration, scores must be valid at the time of application and meet their specific recency requirements.

The IELTS 2-Year Validity Rule Explained

The 2-year validity period exists because language skills deteriorate without regular use. IELTS, like all major language tests, sets this window to ensure that reported scores reflect a candidate's current English proficiency level.

When Does the 2-Year Period Start?

The validity period starts from the test date, not the result release date. For example:

Test Date: 15 March 2025

Results Released: 28 March 2025

Valid Until: 15 March 2027

Why Only 2 Years?

  • Language attrition: Research shows that language skills decline without regular exposure and use.
  • Fairness: Ensures all candidates competing for the same position or place have been assessed to the same recency standard.
  • Immigration requirements: Visa bodies need current evidence of language ability.
  • Professional standards: Regulatory bodies like NMC need assurance of current communication competence.

IELTS Validity Requirements by Regulatory Body

Different organizations have specific rules about IELTS score validity, recency, and acceptance. Here is what you need to know for the most common healthcare registration pathways:

NMC

Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC UK)

For nurses and midwives registering in the UK

Score Requirements

  • - IELTS Academic only (General Training NOT accepted)
  • - Overall Band 7.0
  • - Minimum Band 7.0 in Listening, Reading, Writing
  • - Minimum Band 7.0 in Speaking
  • - All scores must be from one test sitting

Validity Rules

  • - Score must be valid (within 2 years) at time of application
  • - NMC strongly prefers scores from the last 12 months
  • - You cannot combine scores from two different test dates
  • - If your score expires during the application process, you must retake
Important: The NMC does NOT accept IELTS General Training, IELTS Life Skills, or IELTS Indicator. You must take IELTS Academic at an approved test centre.
GMC

General Medical Council (GMC UK)

For doctors registering in the UK

Score Requirements

  • - IELTS Academic only
  • - Overall Band 7.5
  • - Minimum Band 7.0 in each of the 4 sections
  • - All scores from one test sitting

Validity Rules

  • - Score must be valid at time of application to the GMC
  • - Must be achieved within the 2 years immediately preceding application
  • - The GMC may request additional evidence of English proficiency
UKVI

UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI)

For Skilled Worker visa and Tier 2 applications

Score Requirements

  • - IELTS for UKVI (Academic or General Training)
  • - Minimum CEFR Level B1 (typically Band 4.0-4.5)
  • - For healthcare roles, higher scores may be required

Validity Rules

  • - Score must be valid at time of visa application
  • - Standard 2-year validity applies
  • - Must be from an approved SELT (Secure English Language Test) centre
  • - Test must be listed on UKVI's approved providers register
HCPC

Health and Care Professions Council (HCPC)

For allied health professionals (physiotherapists, radiographers, etc.)

Score Requirements

  • - IELTS Academic
  • - Overall Band 7.0
  • - Minimum Band 6.5 in each section
  • - All scores from one test sitting

Validity Rules

  • - Standard 2-year validity applies
  • - Score must be valid at time of HCPC application
  • - HCPC also accepts OET as an alternative

Can You Combine IELTS Scores?

One of the most common questions from healthcare professionals is whether they can combine their best scores from multiple IELTS test sittings. The answer depends on the regulatory body:

Regulatory BodyScore Combining Allowed?Details
NMC (Nursing)NoAll scores must be from a single test sitting. No exceptions.
GMC (Doctors)NoAll scores must be from a single test sitting.
HCPCNoAll scores must be from a single test sitting.
NMC (with OET)NoYou cannot combine IELTS and OET scores either. Must use one test type entirely.

What This Means for You

If you score Band 7.0 in Listening, Reading, and Writing but only Band 6.5 in Speaking on your first attempt, you cannot keep the three Band 7.0 scores and retake only Speaking. You must retake the entire IELTS test and achieve the required scores in all four sections in a single sitting.

This rule makes IELTS particularly challenging for candidates who have one significantly weaker skill. It is why many nurses choose OET instead, as the profession-specific content can make the test more accessible for healthcare professionals.

What Happens When Your IELTS Score Expires?

If your IELTS certificate is older than 2 years, different scenarios apply depending on your situation:

Scenario 1: Application Already Submitted

If you have already submitted your NMC or GMC application using a valid IELTS score, and the score expires while your application is being processed, you are generally not required to submit a new score. The score only needs to be valid at the time of application submission. However, if the regulatory body requests a new score for any reason, you must comply.

Scenario 2: Application Not Yet Submitted

If your IELTS score has expired and you have not yet submitted your registration application, you must retake the IELTS test and submit a new, valid score. There are no exceptions to this rule for NMC or GMC applications.

Scenario 3: Visa Application After Registration

For your Skilled Worker visa application, UKVI requires a valid SELT score. If your original IELTS score has expired by the time you apply for your visa, you will need to retake an approved English test. Some candidates use this opportunity to take IELTS for UKVI specifically.

Scenario 4: Employer's Requirements

Individual NHS trusts and employers may have their own English language requirements. Some may accept an expired IELTS score if you have been working in an English-speaking environment since taking the test. Always check with your specific employer.

Retaking IELTS: What You Need to Know

There is no limit to how many times you can take the IELTS test. However, there are important rules and considerations for retaking:

IELTS Retake Rules

  • 1.
    You can retake IELTS as many times as you wish. There is no waiting period between tests.
  • 2.
    You can retake the entire test or, at some centres, take IELTS One Skill Retake (OSR) to retake just one section.
  • 3.
    Each test attempt requires a new registration and full payment of the test fee.
  • 4.
    Your new score does not replace your old score. All scores remain valid for their respective 2-year periods.

IELTS One Skill Retake (OSR)

Launched in 2023, OSR allows you to retake one section (Listening, Reading, Writing, or Speaking) within 60 days of your original test date.

Important Limitations

  • - NMC does NOT accept OSR scores. You must achieve all required scores in a single sitting.
  • - GMC does not currently accept OSR either.
  • - Check with your specific regulatory body before relying on OSR.
  • - OSR is not available at all test centres.
