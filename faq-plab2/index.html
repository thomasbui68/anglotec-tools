PLAB 2 is the second part of the Professional and Linguistic Assessments Board test. It is a practical OSCE-style exam that checks whether international medical graduates can apply their medical knowledge and practise safely in the UK.
Any international medical graduate who qualified outside the UK, EEA, or Switzerland and wants to practise medicine in the UK must pass PLAB 2 after passing PLAB 1.
PLAB 2 is an OSCE consisting of 18 stations, each lasting 8 minutes, plus 2 rest stations. Stations test clinical examination, history taking, communication, practical procedures, and emergency management.
PLAB 2 is held exclusively at the GMC Clinical Assessment Centre in Manchester, UK. You will need to travel to Manchester to take the exam.
You can book PLAB 2 through your GMC Online account. You must first have passed PLAB 1. Available dates are released throughout the year and fill up quickly, so book early.
PLAB 2 costs £905 as of 2025. PLAB 1 costs an additional £255. You should also budget for travel, accommodation, and visa costs for your trip to Manchester.
PLAB 2 uses the borderline regression method to set the pass mark. The exact score varies by exam session. You must achieve the required standard across the stations to pass.
PLAB 2 results are released within 3 weeks of your exam date. You will receive an email and can view your results in your GMC Online account.
You can attempt PLAB 2 a maximum of four times. If you fail four times, you are no longer eligible to take PLAB and cannot register with the GMC through this route.
You need: a primary medical qualification from an approved institution, PLAB 1 pass (valid for 3 years), and IELTS 7.5 overall (or OET Grade B) if required. You must also have GMC approval.
PLAB 2 stations include: history taking, physical examination, clinical procedures, communication and ethics, emergency scenarios, data interpretation, and counselling stations. Each tests a different clinical competency.
Most international candidates need a Standard Visitor Visa to travel to the UK for PLAB 2. The GMC provides a support letter you can use as part of your visa application.
Wear professional attire such as a shirt and trousers or formal business wear. You do not need a white coat or stethoscope. Dress as you would for a professional medical interview.
No, the GMC provides all necessary equipment for the exam. You only need to bring your passport, booking confirmation, and a watch.
Use the GMC’s PLAB 2 blueprint, attend a preparation course, practise with mock OSCEs, study NICE guidelines, practise communication skills, and use Anglotec AI’s PLAB 2 preparation tools with scenario-based practice.
The PLAB 2 blueprint outlines the skills, topics, and domains tested in the exam. It includes history taking, examination, practical procedures, acute care, communication, and ethics. Download it free from the GMC website.
After passing PLAB 2, you can apply for full GMC registration with a licence to practise. Once registered, you can apply for NHS doctor positions and start your medical career in the UK.
PLAB 2 results do not expire. However, you must apply for GMC registration within two years of passing PLAB 2. If you wait longer, you may need to provide additional evidence of recent clinical experience.
PLAB 1 is a written multiple-choice exam taken at test centres worldwide. PLAB 2 is a practical OSCE exam held only in Manchester, UK. You must pass PLAB 1 before taking PLAB 2.
After PLAB 2 and GMC registration, you can apply for non-training NHS posts such as Trust Grade or locum positions. For specialist training, you will need to apply through competitive recruitment pathways such as specialty training programmes.
