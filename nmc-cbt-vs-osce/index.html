If you're an overseas nurse registering with the UK's Nursing and Midwifery Council, you'll need to pass BOTH the CBT and OSCE. Here's exactly what each test involves and how to prepare for success.

The Complete NMC Registration Process

Step-by-step from overseas nurse to UK registered nurse

Step 1

eligibility & Qualification Check

NMC verifies your nursing qualification, training hours (minimum 4,600 hours), and registration in your home country. Create an NMC Online account and submit documents. Processing: 2-6 weeks.

Step 2

English Language Test

Pass IELTS Academic (7.0 overall, 6.5 each) or OET (Grade B all sub-tests) or PTE Academic (76 overall, 66 each). You can combine scores from two sittings for OET.

Step 3

CBT (Part 1) - THEORY

Book through Pearson VUE. 120 MCQs covering nursing theory, UK standards, drug calculations, professional values. Must pass within 2 years. Cost: £83.

Step 4

Admission to the Register (Provisional)

After passing CBT and English test, NMC grants provisional registration. You can now apply for UK nursing jobs and obtain your Health and Care Worker visa.

Step 5

Arrive in the UK

Take up your nursing position. Your employer will support your OSCE preparation. Most employers provide study time and resources. You're on a pre-registration PIN.

Step 6

OSCE (Part 2) - PRACTICAL

Book at an approved OSCE test centre. 10 stations testing clinical skills, assessment, communication, and professionalism. Must pass within 3 attempts and within 8 months of employment start. Cost: £794.

Step 7

Full NMC Registration

Upon passing OSCE, your provisional registration becomes full registration. You receive your NMC PIN number and can practise as a registered nurse in the UK without restrictions.

CBT in Detail: What to Expect

The computer-based theory examination broken down

💻

Test Format

120 multiple-choice questions answered on a computer at a Pearson VUE test centre. You have 4 hours to complete the test. Questions are a mix of single-best-answer and extended matching format. Some questions include images (ECGs, wound photos, medication labels).

📋

Topics Covered

Professional values, communication and interpersonal skills, nursing practice and decision-making, leadership and management, drug calculations (essential), safeguarding (adults and children), mental health awareness, and infection control.

⚠️

Critical Questions

The CBT includes "critical questions" on patient and public safety. You MUST answer these correctly to pass, regardless of your overall score. These cover essential nursing judgement scenarios.

🧮

Drug Calculations

Expect 10-15 drug calculation questions. These are often the section that causes failures. You must demonstrate competency in calculating dosages, IV rates, and medication concentrations. Calculators are provided on-screen.

📍

Booking & Availability

CBT can be taken at Pearson VUE centres in 150+ countries. You can book online through your NMC account. Available most days of the week with flexible timing. Results emailed within 48 hours.

📚

Preparation Tips

Study the NMC Code (2018), UK nursing standards, and practise drug calculations daily. Use NMC's test blueprint (published on their website). Anglotec AI provides CBT practice questions aligned with the NMC curriculum.

OSCE in Detail: What to Expect

The practical clinical examination broken down station by station

🏥

Test Format

The OSCE consists of 10 stations, each lasting 15-20 minutes. You'll rotate through stations with short breaks. Stations use both simulated patients (actors) and clinical skill manikins. An examiner observes and scores your performance at each station.

🩺

APIE Stations (4 stations)

Assessment, Planning, Implementation, and Evaluation. These are the core nursing process stations. You'll assess a patient, develop a care plan, implement nursing interventions, and evaluate outcomes. These stations carry the most weight.

💊

Medicines Management (2 stations)

One station tests your ability to safely administer medication including checks, calculations, and patient education. The other tests dispensing and clinical pharmacology knowledge.

🤝

Professional Values & Communication

Stations testing your ability to communicate effectively with patients, families, and colleagues. Scenarios include breaking bad news, dealing with complaints, informed consent, and ethical dilemmas.

🩹

Clinical Skills Stations

Practical demonstrations of: vital signs, wound care/dressing, catheterisation (theory), injection technique, basic life support (BLS), manual handling, and infection control procedures.

📍

Test Centres

Approved OSCE test centres are located at various NHS trusts across England and Northern Ireland. You must book through your employer, who typically arranges your OSCE slot. Major hubs include Oxford, Northampton, and Ulster University.

How to Prepare: CBT vs OSCE

📖

CBT Preparation (4-8 weeks)

• Read the NMC Code (2018) thoroughly
• Study UK nursing standards and guidelines
• Practise 50+ drug calculations daily
• Use the NMC test blueprint as your guide
• Take timed practice CBT tests
• Join overseas nurse Facebook groups for tips
• Focus on UK-specific protocols (may differ from your country)

🎭

OSCE Preparation (6-12 weeks)

• Attend your employer's OSCE training programme
• Practise ALL clinical skills repeatedly
• Role-play communication scenarios with colleagues
• Learn the APIE framework inside-out
• Understand UK documentation standards
• Practise with OSCE-specific manikins if possible
• Watch OSCE preparation videos on YouTube

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between CBT and OSCE? +
CBT (Computer Based Test) is a 120-question multiple-choice exam testing your theoretical nursing knowledge. You take it BEFORE coming to the UK, at a Pearson VUE centre in your home country. OSCE (Objective Structured Clinical Examination) is a practical exam with 10 clinical stations testing hands-on skills, communication, and professional judgement. You take it AFTER arriving in the UK, at an approved OSCE test centre. Both are mandatory for NMC registration.
Can I take the OSCE before coming to the UK? +
No. The OSCE can only be taken at approved test centres in the UK. You must first pass the CBT and obtain your Health and Care Worker visa, then travel to the UK to take up employment before you can sit the OSCE. Most nurses take the OSCE within 3-6 months of arriving in the UK.
What happens if I fail the OSCE 3 times? +
This is a serious situation. After 3 failed OSCE attempts, the NMC will close your application. Your employer may terminate your employment contract since you cannot work as a registered nurse. Without employer sponsorship, your visa status is at risk. Some nurses switch to different visa categories or return home. This is why thorough preparation is absolutely critical - never underestimate the OSCE.
How long do I have to pass the OSCE after arriving in the UK? +
You must pass the OSCE within 8 months (12 months in some cases) from your employment start date. Additionally, your CBT result is only valid for 2 years, so you must pass the OSCE within that window. Most employers expect you to take your first OSCE attempt within 3 months of arrival.
What is the hardest part of the CBT? +
Drug calculations are consistently the most challenging section for overseas nurses. Many countries use different medication names, units, and calculation methods. The NMC expects competency in dosage calculations, IV infusion rates, and medication concentrations. Practise daily until you can solve any calculation confidently in under 2 minutes.
What is the hardest part of the OSCE? +
The APIE (Assessment, Planning, Implementation, Evaluation) stations are the most challenging because they test your entire nursing process in a timed environment. Communication stations can also be difficult if you're not familiar with UK cultural norms around patient interaction. Many nurses also struggle with UK-specific documentation requirements.
Does my employer help with OSCE preparation? +
Yes, most NHS employers provide structured OSCE preparation programmes for overseas nurses. This typically includes: dedicated study time, skills lab access, practice sessions with manikins, communication role-play training, and sometimes a mock OSCE. However, the quality varies between employers. Ask about OSCE support during your interview.
