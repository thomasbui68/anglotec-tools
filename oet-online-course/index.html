Frequently Asked Questions

▶ What OET grade do I need for NMC registration?

The Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) requires at least Grade B (350+) in all four OET sub-tests — Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking. You can achieve these across two sittings maximum, but no single sub-test can be below Grade C+. Our OET Intensive package guarantees Grade B in every sub-test, making it the safest choice for NMC registration.

▶ How long does OET preparation take for nurses?

Most nurses complete our best OET preparation course in 4–8 weeks. The exact timeline depends on your current English level and how many hours you can study per day. Our Intensive package students typically achieve Grade B within 6 weeks by studying 2–3 hours daily. We provide a free diagnostic test at enrolment that tells you exactly how long you need.

▶ Is the OET course really nursing-specific?

Absolutely. Unlike generic English courses, every lesson in our OET online course uses real nursing scenarios, medical terminology, and UK healthcare contexts. Our curriculum is written by NHS nurses who understand exactly what the OET exam tests and what you'll face in your daily NHS work. Writing practice uses real case notes, speaking practice uses authentic nurse-patient role plays, and reading materials come from actual medical journals.

▶ Can I take the OET exam online?

OET offers both paper-based and computer-based testing at authorised venues worldwide. It is not a fully at-home exam. We help you find your nearest test centre and book your exam at the optimal time based on your preparation progress. Most major cities have monthly OET test dates.

▶ What's included in the NMC CBT preparation?
