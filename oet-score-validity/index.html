OET Score Validity: How Long Do OET Results Last?

Understand OET validity periods, NMC and GMC requirements, retake rules, and how OET scores compare to IELTS for healthcare professionals seeking UK registration.

Check Your OET Status

Quick Answer

OET scores are valid for 2 years from the date of the test. This is the same validity period as IELTS. For NMC UK registration, your OET scores must be valid at the time you submit your application, and you must achieve at least a Grade C+ in Reading and Listening, and Grade B in Writing and Speaking (or Grade B in all four sections, depending on the current NMC requirements).

Understanding OET Score Validity

The Occupational English Test (OET) uses a grading system from A (highest) to E (lowest). Like IELTS, OET results are officially valid for 2 years from the test date. However, individual regulatory bodies may impose additional requirements regarding score recency and minimum grades.

2yr

Standard Validity

OET scores are valid for 2 years from the test date, identical to IELTS. Count from the day you sat the test, not the results release date.

A-E

OET Grading Scale

OET uses letter grades A through E. Most UK regulatory bodies require Grade B (equivalent to IELTS 7.0) in the relevant sections.

500+

500+ Test Venues

OET is available at over 140 venues in 40+ countries, with both paper-based and computer-based options at most locations.

NMC OET Requirements: Current Rules (2025)

The Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) sets specific OET grade requirements for nurses and midwives seeking UK registration. These requirements have evolved, so ensure you are following the most current guidance:

NMC OET Grade Requirements

OET Section Minimum Grade Required IELTS Equivalent
Listening C+ IELTS 6.5 equivalent
Reading C+ IELTS 6.5 equivalent
Writing B IELTS 7.0 equivalent
Speaking B IELTS 7.0 equivalent

Key Point: All four OET sections must be passed in a single sitting. You cannot combine scores from different test dates. For example, if you achieve B in Writing and Speaking but only C in Listening, you must retake the entire OET test.

Test Score Combining (NMC Update)

In a significant policy update, the NMC now allows score combining under specific conditions:

  • - You can combine two OET test sittings completed within 6 months of each other
  • - You must achieve at least the minimum required grade in all 4 sections across the two sittings
  • - No single section can be below the minimum grade in either sitting
  • - Both tests must be the same version (e.g., both OET Nursing)
  • - This applies to tests taken on or after 3 October 2023

What This Means for You

The score combining rule is a game-changer for many candidates. For example:

First Sitting: Listening C+, Reading C+, Writing B, Speaking C+

Second Sitting: Listening C+, Reading C+, Writing C+, Speaking B

Result: Combined scores meet NMC requirements!

OET Retake Rules and Policies

If you do not achieve the required grades on your first attempt, OET has straightforward retake policies:

Standard Retake Rules

  • ✓
    You can retake OET as many times as needed. There is no limit.
  • ✓
    You must retake all four sub-tests in a single sitting unless you are eligible for the OET Remarked service.
  • ✓
    Each retake requires a new registration and full payment of the test fee.
  • ✓
    There is no mandatory waiting period between attempts.

OET Remark (Re-marking) Service

If you believe your score does not reflect your performance, you can request a remark within 5 weeks of your results release.

  • - Fee: Approximately AUD 120-150 per sub-test
  • - Results of remark available in 15-20 business days
  • - Your score can go up, down, or stay the same
  • - If your grade changes, the remark fee is refunded
  • - You can request a remark for one or multiple sections

How to Check Your OET Score Validity

Staying on top of your OET score validity is essential for planning your NMC or GMC application. Here is how to verify and track your scores:

Step 1: Find Your Test Date

Your OET Statement of Results shows your test date. This is the date from which the 2-year validity period is calculated. Log into your MyOET account to view all your test dates and results.

Step 2: Calculate the Expiry Date

Add exactly 2 years to your test date. For example, if you took OET on 15 January 2025, your scores are valid until 15 January 2027. Use a calendar or our free validity tracker to avoid calculation errors.

Step 3: Plan Your Application Timeline

Submit your NMC or GMC application well before your scores expire. The NMC application process can take 3-6 months from submission to decision. Aim to submit at least 6 months before expiry to allow for any delays or requests for additional information.
