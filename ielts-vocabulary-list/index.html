Why Vocabulary Matters for IELTS Band 7+

Vocabulary accounts for 25% of your score in both Writing and Speaking, and directly impacts your performance in Reading and Listening. Band 7 requires you to use less common lexical items with some awareness of style and collocation. Band 8+ requires wide-ranging, precise vocabulary used flexibly and accurately. This list covers the 500 most high-impact words organized by the IELTS topics that appear most frequently.

How to Use This Vocabulary List

To maximize your learning, follow this systematic approach:

1

Study 10 Words Daily

Focus on one category per week. Learn 10 new words each day and review the previous day's words.

2

Write Example Sentences

For each word, write your own sentence using it in an IELTS-style context. This builds active vocabulary.

3

Learn Collocations

Words rarely appear alone. Learn common collocations (e.g., "dramatically increase", "significant impact").

4

Use in Practice Essays

Deliberately use 5-10 new words in every practice essay and speaking session to build fluency.

Frequently Asked Questions

For Band 7, you need to demonstrate the ability to use "less common lexical items" with some awareness of style and collocation. There is no fixed number, but research suggests that candidates scoring Band 7+ typically have an active vocabulary of 6,000-8,000 words. Focus on mastering the 500 words in this list, as they are the highest-impact words for IELTS topics.
Contextual learning is far more effective than rote memorisation. For each word, read the example sentence, then write your own sentence using the word in an IELTS-relevant context. Use the words in your practice essays and speaking sessions. Research shows that you need to encounter a word 7-10 times in different contexts before it becomes part of your active vocabulary.
The key is to learn collocations (words that commonly appear together) rather than individual words. For example, learn "dramatically increase" not just "dramatically" and "increase" separately. Also, ensure you understand the register and connotation of each word. Using a sophisticated word incorrectly is worse than using a simpler word correctly. Practice with feedback from a teacher or AI tool.
Start with the categories most relevant to your weaker areas. For Writing Task 1 (Academic), prioritise Category 7 (Describing Trends). For Writing Task 2, focus on Categories 5 (Society & Government), 8 (Opinion & Argument), and 9 (Connectors). For Speaking, Categories 1 (Education), 2 (Health), and 10 (Academic Formality) are most useful. All healthcare professionals should prioritise Category 2 (Health).
Many of these words are useful for OET, particularly Category 2 (Health & Healthcare) words which are directly relevant to the OET context. However, OET requires profession-specific vocabulary (medical terminology, healthcare collocations) that goes beyond this general IELTS list. For OET Writing, focus on referral letter phrases and medical terminology specific to your profession.
At 10 words per day, you can cover all 500 words in 50 days (approximately 7 weeks). At 20 words per day, you can complete the list in 25 days. However, learning is not just about exposure - you need to use the words actively. Plan for 8-10 weeks of consistent study, with daily review of previously learned words using spaced repetition.
