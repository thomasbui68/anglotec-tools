Top 10 NHS Interview Tips

  1. Research the Trust — Know their values, recent CQC rating, and any major initiatives.
  2. Use the STAR method — Structure answers: Situation, Task, Action, Result.
  3. Know the 6 Cs — Care, Compassion, Competence, Communication, Courage, Commitment.
  4. Know the NHS Values — Working Together, Respect and Dignity, Commitment to Quality, Compassion, Improving Lives, Everyone Counts.
  5. Prepare real examples — Use genuine clinical experiences, not hypothetical answers.
  6. Dress professionally — Smart business attire shows you take the opportunity seriously.
  7. Ask questions — Prepare 2-3 thoughtful questions about the ward, team, or development.
  8. Be honest — If you don't know something, say so and explain how you'd find out.
  9. Show enthusiasm — Panel wants to see passion for nursing and patient care.
  10. Practise aloud — Record yourself answering questions to improve fluency.

Frequently Asked Questions

What questions are asked in an NHS nurse interview?

NHS nurse interviews typically include clinical scenario questions, NHS Values-based questions, teamwork and communication questions, safeguarding scenarios, and questions about professional development. This tool covers all of these categories.

How should I prepare for a Band 5 nurse interview?

Research the Trust thoroughly, practise the STAR method for answering questions, prepare examples from your clinical experience, know the 6 Cs and NHS Values, and practise answering questions aloud using this tool.

How long should my answers be?

Aim for 1–2 minutes per answer. Use the STAR method to keep responses structured. The 2-minute timer in practice mode helps you gauge appropriate length.

What are the NHS Values I should know?

The six NHS Values are: Working Together for Patients, Respect and Dignity, Commitment to Quality of Care, Compassion, Improving Lives, and Everyone Counts. Be ready to demonstrate these in your answers.

Can I use these answers in my interview?

Use the model answers as a guide for structure and content, but always adapt them with your own experiences. Panels can tell when answers are memorised, so make them personal and authentic.
