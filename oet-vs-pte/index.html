Why Choose OET? (The Healthcare-Focused Option)

🏥

Medical Context Throughout

Every section uses healthcare scenarios. Reading uses medical journal articles. Writing asks for a referral letter. Speaking uses nurse-patient or doctor-patient role-plays. You'll never be asked to write about climate change or describe a graph about tourism.

📝

Writing That Mirrors Your Job

OET Writing is a professional letter (referral, discharge, transfer) based on case notes. This is exactly what nurses and doctors do daily. PTE's essay writing is academic and less relevant to clinical practice.

🗣️

Speaking: Real Medical Role-Plays

You'll interact with a trained interlocutor playing a patient or carer. Scenarios include explaining medications, breaking bad news, or reassuring anxious relatives. This mirrors real clinical communication.

✅

Accepted by ALL Medical Regulators

OET is accepted by the NMC, GMC, AHPRA (Australia), Irish Medical Council, Dubai Health Authority, and many more. PTE is NOT accepted by GMC for doctor registration.

🎯

Vocabulary You Already Know

Clinical terminology, anatomy, and medical abbreviations are built into the test. Healthcare professionals find the content familiar, reducing the cognitive load of learning test-specific vocabulary.

🔁

Individual Sub-test Retake

Failed just one section? OET allows you to retake individual sub-tests within 12 months. This saves time, money, and stress compared to retaking the entire exam.

Why Choose PTE? (The Fast & Flexible Option)

⚡

Results in 48 Hours

PTE Academic results arrive within 48 hours - crucial if you have a job offer with a start date or visa deadline. OET takes 12-16 working days, which can delay your registration process significantly.

💰

Significantly Cheaper

PTE Academic costs around £175 vs OET's £350-400. If you're on a budget or may need multiple attempts, PTE's lower cost is a major advantage.

📅

Daily Test Availability

PTE tests run 365 days a year at hundreds of centres globally. OET runs monthly at fewer locations. If you need to book quickly or retake soon, PTE offers far more flexibility.

🤖

No Human Examiner Anxiety

PTE's fully computerised format eliminates nervousness about interacting with examiners. For test-takers who experience performance anxiety with people, this can lead to a higher score.

NMC Score Requirements: OET vs PTE (2026)

The exact scores needed for UK nursing registration

🇬🇧

NMC UK - OET Requirements

Grade B (350+) in Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking. You can combine scores from two sittings taken within 6 months, as long as you don't drop below Grade C+ (300+) in any sub-test and achieve at least 350 in each sub-test across both sittings.

NMC UK - PTE Requirements

Overall score of 76 with no section below 66 (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking). Must be achieved in a single sitting. NMC does not currently accept PTE score combining for UK registration.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is OET easier than PTE for healthcare professionals? +
Most healthcare professionals find OET easier because the content is directly relevant to their work. You'll read medical journals, write referral letters, and do patient role-plays - tasks you already understand. PTE uses general academic content that requires learning test-specific strategies. However, PTE's 48-hour results and lower cost are significant advantages.
Can I use PTE for GMC registration as a doctor in the UK? +
No. The General Medical Council (GMC) only accepts IELTS (7.5 overall, min 7.0 each) or OET (Grade B in all sub-tests) for doctor registration in the UK. PTE Academic is not accepted by the GMC. If you're a doctor targeting the UK, you must take either IELTS or OET.
Does NMC accept PTE for UK nurse registration? +
Yes, from August 2024, the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) accepts PTE Academic with an overall score of 76 and no section below 66. However, many nurses still prefer OET because Grade B (350+) is perceived as more achievable than PTE 76, and OET offers sub-test retakes.
Can I combine OET scores from two sittings? +
Yes. The NMC allows OET score combining from two sittings taken within 6 months. You must achieve at least Grade C+ (300+) in every sub-test across both sittings, and at least Grade B (350+) in each sub-test when the two sittings are combined. This is a major advantage over PTE, which doesn't allow score combining.
How much does OET cost compared to PTE? +
OET costs approximately £350-400 (varies by country), while PTE Academic costs around £175. OET is roughly double the price. However, OET's sub-test retake option means you might only need to pay for one section if you fail, potentially making it cheaper overall if you don't pass first time.
How long are OET and PTE results valid? +
Both OET and PTE results are valid for 2 years from the test date. However, individual regulatory bodies may have their own validity requirements. The NMC and GMC both accept tests taken within 2 years of your application.
Which test has more preparation resources? +
PTE has more general prep resources due to its broader user base. OET has fewer total resources but they are highly specialised for healthcare. Official OET practice materials include sample tests for each profession (nursing, medicine, dentistry, etc.).
