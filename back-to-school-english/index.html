Back to School: Improve Your English This Term

Reset Your Study Routine for the New Term

After summer break, your brain needs time to readjust to structured learning. Here's how to transition smoothly into study mode and build momentum that lasts all term.

Week 1

Ease Back In Gently

  • Start with 30-minute study sessions
  • Review material from last term
  • Organise your study space and materials
  • Set 3 clear goals for this term
  • Gradually increase study time by 10 min/day
  • Don't overwhelm yourself on day one
Week 2

Build the Foundation

  • Increase to 60-minute focused sessions
  • Start daily vocabulary building (10 words/day)
  • Begin reading English content daily
  • Practice listening to English podcasts
  • Join or form a study group
  • Establish a consistent sleep schedule
Week 3

Full Study Mode

  • Aim for 90-120 minutes daily study
  • Complete your first practice test
  • Identify your strongest and weakest areas
  • Create a targeted improvement plan
  • Start timed practice for exam sections
  • Track your progress in a journal
Week 4

Momentum & Assessment

  • Second practice test to measure progress
  • Review and refine your study plan
  • Celebrate improvements from Week 1
  • Increase difficulty of practice materials
  • Focus extra time on weak areas
  • Plan your test date if confident

The "Study Mode" Transition Trick

Don't go from zero to six hours overnight. Your brain will resist. Instead, increase study time by 15 minutes each day. Day 1: 30 minutes. Day 2: 45 minutes. Day 3: 60 minutes. By Day 10, you'll be studying 2.5 hours comfortably. This gradual approach prevents burnout and builds sustainable habits.

Your 12-Week Autumn Term Study Plan

A structured week-by-week plan to take you from post-summer rusty to exam-ready by winter break. Adjust timing based on your test date.

Weeks 1-2: Assessment & Foundation

Goal: Establish your baseline and rebuild study habits.

  • Take a full diagnostic practice test (untimed if needed)
  • Score each section honestly
  • Review all mistakes — create an error log
  • Start daily vocabulary (10 new words/day)
  • Read one English article daily (BBC News, The Guardian)
  • Listen to English audio 20 minutes daily

Weeks 3-4: Skill Building

Goal: Develop core skills in each test section.

  • Complete one section-specific practice daily (Listening on Monday, Reading on Tuesday, etc.)
  • Learn test-specific strategies for each section
  • For Writing: learn the format and write 2 tasks per week
  • For Speaking: practice with a partner or record yourself 3x per week
  • Vocabulary: reach 200 new words
  • First timed practice test at end of Week 4

Weeks 5-6: Intensification

Goal: Increase difficulty and build exam stamina.

  • Complete full practice tests every weekend (timed)
  • Weekday study: focus on weakest section
  • Writing: complete timed tasks under exam conditions
  • Speaking: full mock tests with timer
  • Vocabulary: 300 total new words by end of Week 6
  • Analyse score trends — are you improving?

Weeks 7-8: Technique Refinement

Goal: Perfect your exam strategy and time management.

  • Practice with strict exam timing for ALL sections
  • Refine your approach to each question type
  • Eliminate careless mistakes through careful review
  • Build a "cheat sheet" of strategies that work for you
  • Complete 2 full tests per week
  • Vocabulary review: consolidate all words learned

Weeks 9-10: Peak Performance

Goal: Simulate exam conditions and build confidence.

  • Complete 2-3 full mock tests per week
  • Mock tests on the same day of the week as your real exam
  • Practice under realistic conditions: timed, no distractions
  • Review only — no new content
  • Focus on accuracy and consistency
  • Build a pre-exam routine

Weeks 11-12: Final Preparation & Test

Goal: Arrive at the exam room confident and prepared.

  • Week 11: Light review, confidence-building practice
  • Complete one final mock test early in the week
  • Review your best work for confidence
  • Prepare exam day documents and materials
  • Week 12: Take your exam! Rest 2 days before
  • Winter break celebration — you've earned it

4 Skills to Sharpen This Term

Focus your autumn term energy on building these four pillars of English exam success.

Listening

Rebuild Your Listening Ear

After a summer of your native language, your ear needs retraining. Start with slower content (BBC Learning English) and gradually increase speed. Practice daily — even 15 minutes makes a difference. Focus on note-taking: practice writing keywords while listening. Try podcasts about topics you enjoy to make it engaging.

Reading

Expand Your Reading Stamina

Exam reading requires sustained focus. Start with short articles (300 words) and gradually build to longer academic texts (900+ words). Practice skimming for main ideas in 2 minutes. Learn to identify topic sentences. Time yourself: aim to read 300 words in 3 minutes with comprehension.

Writing

Restore Your Written Fluency

Writing is usually the first skill to degrade after a break. Start with simple paragraphs and rebuild to full essays. Focus on sentence structure variety. Practice writing by hand if taking paper-based tests. Aim for one full writing task per week by Week 3, increasing to 2-3 per week by Week 6.

Speaking

Get Your Voice Back

Speaking confidence fades quickly without practice. Start talking to yourself in English daily — describe your day, your plans, your opinions. Record yourself and listen back. Find a speaking partner or join online practice groups. Practice for 10 minutes daily minimum; fluency returns faster than you think.

The "Little and Often" Principle

Research consistently shows that short, daily practice sessions (20-30 minutes) are more effective than occasional marathon sessions (3-4 hours). Your brain needs time to consolidate new information between sessions. Aim for daily contact with English — even 15 minutes of reading or listening counts. Consistency is the single biggest predictor of success.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get back into study mode after summer?

Start small and build gradually. Begin with 30-minute sessions and increase by 15 minutes every few days. Revisit material you already know to build confidence. Establish a consistent routine before increasing intensity. Most students are back in full study mode within 2-3 weeks.

When should I book my test?

Book your test 6-8 weeks before your target date. Autumn term (September-December) is a popular time, so test centres fill up quickly. Booking early secures your preferred date and gives you a fixed deadline to work toward — which is highly motivating.

How many hours should I study per week?

During term time, aim for 10-15 hours per week (about 90 minutes daily). If you have an exam within 6 weeks, increase to 20 hours. If your exam is more than 3 months away, 7-10 hours weekly is sufficient for steady progress. The key is consistency, not intensity.

Should I study every day or take rest days?

Study 6 days per week with 1 rest day. Daily contact with English is important for maintaining momentum and building fluency. Your rest day should still include passive English exposure — watching a show, listening to music, or casual reading. This keeps your brain engaged without formal study.

What if I feel overwhelmed at the start of term?

Feeling overwhelmed is completely normal. Focus on one small action today rather than the entire term ahead. Break your preparation into weekly chunks. Remember: every successful candidate felt overwhelmed at some point. The difference is they started anyway.

Can I improve my score by 1 band in one term?

Yes, a 1-band improvement in one 12-week term is achievable with consistent effort. Many students improve by 0.5-1 band in 8-10 weeks of dedicated study. Focus on your weakest section first — it has the most room for improvement. Track your progress with monthly practice tests.
