Overview of UK Scholarships 2026

The United Kingdom remains one of the world's premier destinations for international education, and significant scholarship funding is available to help talented students from around the world access UK degrees. In 2026, a wide range of scholarships funded by the UK government, individual universities, charitable trusts, and international organisations provide financial support covering tuition fees, living expenses, travel costs, and sometimes additional allowances.

For international students, the cost of studying in the UK can be substantial. Tuition fees for international students range from £12,000 to over £40,000 per year depending on the course and institution, while living costs in the UK average £12,000-£15,000 annually. Scholarships can dramatically reduce or entirely eliminate these costs, making UK education accessible to students who might otherwise be unable to afford it.

This comprehensive guide covers every major scholarship programme available to international students in 2026, with detailed information on eligibility, application procedures, award values, and success strategies. Whether you are planning to study at undergraduate, masters, or doctoral level, there is likely a scholarship that matches your circumstances.

Chevening Scholarships 2026

Chevening Scholarships are the UK government's flagship international scholarship programme, funded by the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) and partner organisations. They offer full financial support for one-year master's degrees at any UK university.

What Chevening Covers

Chevening Scholarships are among the most generous available, providing: full tuition fee payment (up to £18,000-£30,000 depending on course), a monthly living stipend (£1,200-£1,500 depending on location), economy return flights to the UK, an arrival allowance, visa application cost reimbursement, and a travel grant for attending Chevening events. The total value typically exceeds £35,000.

Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible for a Chevening Scholarship in 2026, you must: be a citizen of a Chevening-eligible country (over 160 countries are eligible), have an undergraduate degree equivalent to a UK 2:1, have at least two years of work experience (£2,800 hours), have an unconditional offer from a UK university by the deadline, and commit to returning to your home country for at least two years after completing your studies.

Application Process & Timeline

Applications for 2026/2027 Chevening Scholarships open in August 2026 and close in early November 2026. The process involves an online application form with four essay questions, documentation of qualifications and work experience, and two reference letters. Shortlisted candidates are invited to interview at British embassies or high commissions between February and April 2027. Results are announced from June 2027.

Tips for a Successful Chevening Application

Successful Chevening applications demonstrate clear leadership potential, a well-articulated career plan, specific goals for how the UK education will benefit the home country, and strong written communication skills. The essay questions require thoughtful, detailed responses that go beyond generic statements. Start your application early and seek feedback from previous Chevening scholars if possible.

Commonwealth Scholarships 2026

Commonwealth Scholarships are funded by the UK government through the Commonwealth Scholarship Commission (CSC) and support students from Commonwealth countries to study in the UK.

Types of Commonwealth Scholarships

Several scholarship types are available: Commonwealth Master's Scholarships for candidates from low and middle-income Commonwealth countries; Commonwealth PhD Scholarships for doctoral study; Commonwealth Split-site Scholarships for PhD candidates spending time at a UK university; Commonwealth Professional Fellowships for mid-career professionals; and Commonwealth Distance Learning Scholarships for part-time master's study by distance learning.

What Commonwealth Scholarships Cover

Commonwealth Scholarships provide: approved airfare from home country to the UK and return, full tuition fees, a monthly stipend (£1,200 for London, £1,100 elsewhere), warm clothing allowance, thesis grant (if applicable), study travel grant, and child allowance for widowed, divorced, or single parents. The total value for a master's scholarship is approximately £35,000-£45,000.

Eligibility

Applicants must be citizens or refugees of eligible Commonwealth countries, hold a first degree of at least upper second class (2:1) honours standard, and be unable to afford to study in the UK without a scholarship. Each nominating body (university, national nominating agency, or selected non-governmental organisation) may have additional eligibility criteria.

Application Timeline

Applications for 2026/2027 Commonwealth Master's Scholarships open in September 2026 and close in October 2026. Applications are made through a nominating body in your home country, not directly to the CSC. PhD applications follow a similar timeline. Results are typically announced between March and June 2027.

GREAT Scholarships 2026

GREAT Scholarships are jointly funded by the UK government's GREAT Britain Campaign and participating UK higher education institutions. They provide £10,000 towards tuition fees for one-year taught master's programmes.

Participating Countries

In 2026, GREAT Scholarships are available to students from Bangladesh, China, Egypt, Ghana, Greece, Kenya, India, Indonesia, Malaysia, Mexico, Nigeria, Pakistan, Thailand, Turkey, and Vietnam. Each country has specific scholarships available at specific universities.

How to Apply

To apply for a GREAT Scholarship, first apply for an eligible master's course at a participating university. Once you have an offer, complete the university's GREAT Scholarship application form, typically requiring a statement explaining how the scholarship will help your career and how you will be an ambassador for UK education. Deadlines vary by university but are typically between April and June 2026 for September 2026 entry.

University-Specific Scholarships 2026

Most UK universities offer their own scholarships for international students. These range from partial fee discounts to full scholarships covering all costs.

University of Oxford

Oxford offers numerous scholarships for international students through its colleges and departments. The Clarendon Fund provides over 200 full scholarships annually for graduate students, covering tuition and living costs. The Rhodes Scholarship supports exceptional students from specific countries. Application is typically through the graduate admissions process, with scholarships awarded based on academic excellence.

University of Cambridge

Cambridge offers the Cambridge International Scholarship for PhD students, Gates Cambridge Scholarships for international postgraduate students, and various college-specific awards. The Cambridge Trust provides funding for international students at all levels. Many scholarships require separate applications submitted alongside the course application.

Imperial College London

Imperial offers the President's PhD Scholarships (fully funded for 3.5 years), various faculty-specific master's scholarships, and the Lee Family Scholarship for students from Asia. International students are automatically considered for some scholarships upon course application.

University of Edinburgh

Edinburgh offers the Global Scholarship Programme with awards of £3,000-£10,000 for international students. The university also participates in the GREAT Scholarships programme. Most scholarships require a separate application after receiving an offer.

Other Major Universities

Universities such as Manchester, UCL, King's College London, Warwick, and Bristol all offer extensive scholarship programmes. Check each university's scholarship webpage for specific opportunities, eligibility criteria, and application deadlines. Many universities offer automatic consideration for merit-based scholarships upon course application.

Government & External Funding 2026

Beyond university-specific awards, several external funding sources support international students in the UK.

British Council Scholarships

The British Council offers various scholarship programmes including Women in STEM scholarships, scholarships for students with disabilities, and subject-specific awards. These are often run in partnership with UK universities and may cover full or partial costs.

Marshall Scholarships

Marshall Scholarships finance young Americans of high ability to study for a graduate degree in the UK. Up to 50 scholars are selected annually to study at any UK university. The scholarship covers tuition, living expenses, and travel.

Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters

While the UK's participation in Erasmus+ has changed post-Brexit, Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters programmes that include UK partner universities still offer EU-funded scholarships. These prestigious programmes allow students to study at multiple European universities including UK institutions.

Scholarships for Nursing Students 2026

International students pursuing nursing degrees in the UK have access to specific funding opportunities.

NHS Learning Support Fund

While primarily for UK students, some NHS Learning Support Fund elements are available to international nursing students in specific circumstances. Check with your university's nursing department for current availability.

University Nursing Scholarships

Many UK universities offer specific scholarships for international nursing students. These may include tuition fee discounts of £1,000-£5,000, funded placements, or support with NMC registration costs after graduation. Contact the admissions offices of nursing schools directly for details.

Charitable Funding

Charitable trusts such as the Florence Nightingale Foundation and the Royal College of Nursing Foundation offer funding for nursing students and registered nurses pursuing further education. Some awards are open to international students studying in the UK.

Masters Degree Funding Options

Masters degrees are the most common target for scholarship funding. Beyond the major programmes described above, consider:

  • Alumni discounts: Many UK universities offer 10-20% fee discounts for their own graduates progressing to postgraduate study.
  • Early payment discounts: Some universities offer discounts for paying tuition fees in full before enrolment.
  • Country-specific scholarships: Some universities offer scholarships specifically for students from certain countries.
  • Subject-specific funding: Research councils and professional bodies fund specific subject areas.
  • Employer sponsorship: If currently employed, your employer may sponsor your studies in exchange for a return-of-service commitment.

PhD & Research Funding 2026

PhD funding in the UK is generally more readily available than at other levels, reflecting the research-focused nature of doctoral study.

Research Council Studentships

UK Research and Innovation (UKRI) studentships provide full funding (tuition plus stipend of approximately £18,000 per year) for PhD research. While primarily aimed at UK students, some research council awards include international fees. Check with individual doctoral training programmes for international eligibility.

University PhD Scholarships

Most UK universities offer competitive PhD scholarships that cover international tuition fees and provide a living stipend. These are typically awarded based on academic merit and research proposal quality. Apply through the university's doctoral college or graduate school.

Undergraduate Scholarships 2026

Full undergraduate scholarships for international students are less common but do exist.

The University of Oxford offers the Oxford Reach Scholarship for students from underrepresented countries. The University of Cambridge offers the Cambridge International Scholarship. Several universities offer international excellence scholarships for outstanding undergraduate applicants. These typically provide £2,000-£5,000 per year toward tuition fees.

International students should also explore funding from their home governments, many of which sponsor students to study abroad. Organisations such as the Saudi Arabian Cultural Bureau, Kuwait Cultural Office, and similar bodies in other countries provide comprehensive funding for nationals studying in the UK.

General Eligibility Criteria

While each scholarship has specific requirements, common eligibility criteria include:

  • Nationality: Most scholarships are restricted to specific countries or regions.
  • Academic merit: Strong previous academic performance, typically equivalent to a UK 2:1 or first-class degree.
  • English proficiency: IELTS scores typically 6.5-7.5 depending on the scholarship and course.
  • Work experience: Some scholarships require professional experience (Chevening requires 2+ years).
  • Leadership potential: Many scholarships prioritise candidates who demonstrate leadership and community impact.
  • Return commitment: Some government-funded scholarships require a commitment to return to your home country after study.

How to Write a Winning Scholarship Application

Competition for UK scholarships is intense. These strategies improve your chances:

  1. Start early: Begin researching scholarships at least 12 months before your intended start date.
  2. Be specific: Tailor each application to the specific scholarship. Generic applications are easily identified and rarely successful.
  3. Show impact: Demonstrate how your UK education will benefit your home country or community. Funders want to see return on investment.
  4. Evidence everything: Provide specific examples of achievements, leadership, and impact rather than making unsupported claims.
  5. Write excellently: Your application essays must demonstrate exceptional English writing skills. Proofread meticulously and seek feedback.
  6. Get strong references: Choose referees who know you well and can speak specifically about your abilities and potential.
  7. Meet all deadlines: Late applications are not considered. Create a timeline and submit well before deadlines.

Alternative Funding Options

If you do not secure a full scholarship, consider these alternatives:

  • Student loans: Some countries offer government-backed loans for overseas study. UK private lenders also provide international student loans.
  • Part-time work: Student visa holders can work up to 20 hours per week during term time. Earnings can supplement living costs.
  • Family support: Many international students are funded by family contributions. Plan finances carefully to ensure sufficient funds.
  • Crowdfunding: Some students successfully use crowdfunding platforms to raise partial funding, particularly for courses with clear career benefits.
  • Graduate teaching assistantships: Postgraduate students may work as teaching or research assistants, earning income while gaining experience.

Preparation is key to scholarship success. Use these resources:

  1. Research thoroughly using official scholarship websites and university pages.

  3. Request transcripts and references well in advance.
  4. Draft and refine your personal statement and scholarship essays.
  5. Apply to multiple scholarships to increase your chances.
  6. Keep track of deadlines with a detailed calendar.
