Why OSCE Apps Are Essential for PLAB 2

The PLAB 2 exam consists of 16 Objective Structured Clinical Examination (OSCE) stations, testing your ability to apply clinical knowledge in simulated patient scenarios. Each station lasts 8 minutes with 90 seconds of reading time. The exam covers history taking, physical examination, practical procedures, communication skills, and emergency management. With a pass rate of around 70%, proper preparation is non-negotiable. OSCE training apps offer structured practice, instant feedback, and the flexibility to rehearse anywhere, anytime.

PLAB 2 OSCE Station Types

Understanding the 16 stations helps you prepare effectively.

History Taking Stations (4-5 stations)

You must take a focused history from a patient presenting with a specific complaint. Key skills include open questioning, red flag screening, ICE (ideas, concerns, expectations), and summarising. Common scenarios include chest pain, abdominal pain, headache, and breathlessness.

Physical Examination Stations (3-4 stations)

You are asked to examine a body system. You may need to explain the examination to the patient, carry it out, and present your findings. Common systems include cardiovascular, respiratory, abdominal, neurological, and musculoskeletal.

Practical Procedure Stations (2-3 stations)

You must demonstrate a clinical procedure such as venepuncture, cannulation, urinalysis, blood glucose testing, or catheterisation. These stations test your technical skills alongside communication and safety.

Communication Stations (3-4 stations)

These test your ability to communicate effectively in challenging situations. Scenarios include breaking bad news, explaining a diagnosis, dealing with an angry relative, discussing DNAR decisions, and obtaining informed consent.

Emergency Management Stations (1-2 stations)

You must manage an acutely unwell patient following ABCDE approach. Scenarios include anaphylaxis, cardiac arrest, acute asthma, sepsis, and severe bleeding. These are high-stakes stations requiring rapid, structured management.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many weeks should I prepare for PLAB 2?

Most candidates need 8-12 weeks of dedicated preparation, practising 3-4 hours daily. Use an OSCE app for daily scenario practice and supplement with group practice sessions on weekends.

Can an app replace face-to-face OSCE practice?

No app can fully replace face-to-face practice, especially for physical examination stations. However, AI-powered apps like Anglotec are excellent for daily history-taking and communication practice. Use apps alongside group practice for best results.

What is the PLAB 2 pass rate?

The PLAB 2 pass rate is approximately 70-75%. Candidates who use structured preparation, including OSCE apps, peer practice, and feedback, have significantly higher pass rates than those who prepare alone.

Are free OSCE apps enough to pass?

Free apps like OSCE Timer are useful but insufficient alone. The PLAB 2 exam requires comprehensive scenario exposure and interactive practice. Investing in a premium app like Anglotec provides the depth of practice needed for success.

How important is timing in PLAB 2?

Timing is critical. Each station is strictly 8 minutes. Running out of time is a common reason for failure. Apps with built-in timers, like Anglotec, help you internalise the pacing needed for each station type.

Which stations do candidates fail most often?

Communication stations (breaking bad news, difficult conversations) and emergency management stations have the highest failure rates. Physical examination stations also challenge candidates who have not practised the systematic approach.
