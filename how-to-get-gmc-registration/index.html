GMC Registration Guide

How to Get GMC Registration: Complete Guide for International Doctors

Everything international doctors need to know about gaining GMC registration to practice medicine in the UK. Covers PLAB, postgraduate qualifications, English tests, and the full application process.

Free GMC Registration Toolkit

Quick Summary

To register with the General Medical Council (GMC) and practice medicine in the UK, international doctors must demonstrate acceptable medical knowledge (via PLAB or recognized postgraduate qualification), English language proficiency (IELTS or OET), and fitness to practice. The process typically takes 6-18 months depending on your pathway and costs approximately GBP 2,000-4,000 in total.

Pathways to GMC Registration

The GMC offers several routes to registration depending on your qualifications, experience, and specialty. Choose the pathway that applies to your situation:

1

PLAB Route (Most Common)

The Professional and Linguistic Assessments Board (PLAB) test is the standard route for doctors whose primary medical qualification is not from an GMC-recognized institution and who do not have a recognized postgraduate qualification.

PLAB 1

  • - 180 MCQs, 3 hours
  • - Tests clinical knowledge
  • - Taken at centres worldwide
  • - Can be taken up to 4 times
  • - Fee: GBP 247

PLAB 2

  • - 18 clinical stations (16 assessed)
  • - Tests clinical and communication skills
  • - Taken in Manchester, UK only
  • - Can be taken up to 4 times
  • - Fee: GBP 906
2

Postgraduate Qualification Route

If you hold an accepted postgraduate qualification, you may be exempt from PLAB. The GMC maintains a list of recognized qualifications including MRCP, MRCS, FRCA, FRCR, and many others from Royal Colleges.

Common Recognized Qualifications

MRCP (UK) - Physicians
MRCS - Surgeons
FRCA - Anaesthetists
FRCR - Radiologists
MRCOG - Obstetricians/Gynaecologists
MRCPCH - Paediatricians
MRCEM - Emergency Medicine
MRCPsych - Psychiatrists
3

Sponsorship Route (MTI)

The Medical Training Initiative (MTI) allows international doctors to undertake structured training posts in the UK for up to 2 years. This route requires sponsorship from an approved UK healthcare organization.

  • - Available for doctors with 3+ years of postgraduate experience
  • - Sponsorship by an approved UK hospital or deanery
  • - Registration is time-limited to the training period
  • - IELTS/OET and EPIC verification still required
4

EEA/Swiss Qualified Doctor Route

Doctors who qualified in the European Economic Area (EEA) or Switzerland may be eligible for registration through the Relevant European Qualification (REQ) route. Post-Brexit rules have changed, so check current GMC guidance for the latest requirements.

Step-by-Step: GMC Registration via PLAB

Here is the complete process for international doctors taking the PLAB route to GMC registration:

1

Verify Your Primary Medical Qualification

Before starting the GMC process, your medical degree must be verified through EPIC (Electronic Portfolio of International Credentials), operated by ECFMG.

What You Need to Do

  • - Create an EPIC account at ecfmg.org/epic
  • - Upload your medical degree certificate and transcripts
  • - EPIC will verify your documents directly with your medical school
  • - This process takes 2-6 weeks
  • - Fee: USD 135 (plus USD 100 per credential verification)
2

Pass an English Language Test

You must demonstrate English proficiency through either IELTS or OET before taking PLAB 1.

IELTS Academic for GMC

  • - Overall Band 7.5
  • - Minimum Band 7.0 in all 4 sections
  • - Must be from a single sitting
  • - Valid for 2 years

OET (Medicine) for GMC

  • - Grade B in all 4 sections
  • - Must be from a single sitting
  • - Score combining not accepted
  • - Valid for 2 years
3

Create a GMC Online Account

Register for a GMC Online account at gmc-uk.org. This is where you will submit all documents, track your application, and manage your registration throughout your career in the UK.

4

Pass PLAB 1

PLAB 1 is a written exam consisting of 180 multiple-choice questions. It tests your ability to apply clinical knowledge to the care of patients as described in the GMC's Good Medical Practice guidelines.

PLAB 1 is offered multiple times per year at centres worldwide, including India, Pakistan, UAE, Egypt, Nigeria, and many other countries. You can take PLAB 1 up to 4 times.

5

Pass PLAB 2

PLAB 2 is an Objective Structured Clinical Examination (OSCE) consisting of 18 clinical stations. It tests your clinical skills, including diagnosis, management, communication, and professionalism.

What PLAB 2 Tests

  • - History taking and physical examination
  • - Practical procedures (IV cannulation, suturing, etc.)
  • - Communication skills (breaking bad news, consent)
  • - Emergency management scenarios
  • - Prescribing and interpreting investigations

Location: Clinical Assessment Centre, Manchester, UK only. Fee: GBP 906. You have 4 attempts to pass.

6

Submit Your Full GMC Application

After passing both PLAB exams, submit your full application for registration with a license to practice. You will need:

Documents Required

  • - Verified primary medical qualification (via EPIC)
  • - PLAB 1 and PLAB 2 pass certificates
  • - English language test results
  • - Certificate of Good Standing from all medical councils you have registered with
  • - Proof of internship / clinical experience
  • - Employment history and references
  • - Criminal record checks

Application Details

  • - Registration fee: GBP 65
  • - Processing time: 2-5 working days (if documents complete)
  • - Your application is reviewed by GMC caseworkers
  • - You may be asked for additional information
7

Receive GMC Registration

Once approved, your name will be added to the Medical Register, and you will receive your GMC reference number. You can now apply for medical posts in the UK and start your career as a registered doctor.

Important: Newly registered doctors typically work in non-training posts (Trust Grade, LAS, or Locum) before entering formal UK training programs. Building UK clinical experience is essential for specialty training applications.

GMC Registration Requirements by Doctor Type

The GMC registration process varies slightly depending on your background:

Recent Medical Graduates

If you graduated within the last 2 years and completed your internship/house officer year:

  • - Must have completed 12 months of internship / clinical training
  • - PLAB route or postgraduate qualification route
  • - English test required
  • - May need additional clinical attachments in the UK

Experienced Doctors (5+ Years)

If you have significant postgraduate experience:

  • - May qualify for the postgraduate qualification route
  • - Specialist registration pathway available if you hold equivalent specialist training
  • - CESR (Certificate of Eligibility for Specialist Registration) may be an option
  • - More likely to secure senior posts directly

Doctors from EEA Countries

Post-Brexit rules have changed for EEA-qualified doctors:

  • - May need to meet additional requirements
  • - EPIC verification still required for most
  • - English language test may be required
  • - Check current GMC guidance for your specific country

Doctors with US Training (USMLE)

US-trained doctors often have advantages:

  • - USMLE Steps 1, 2CK, and 2CS are well-respected
  • - US residency training may contribute to CESR applications
  • - Still need PLAB unless holding recognized UK postgraduate qualification
  • - Strong clinical experience valued by NHS employers
