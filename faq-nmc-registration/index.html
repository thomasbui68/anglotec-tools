The Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) is the professional regulatory body for nurses and midwives in the UK. It maintains the register of qualified nurses and sets standards for education, training, and conduct.
Any nurse or midwife who wants to work in the UK as a registered professional must be on the NMC register. This includes UK-trained nurses and all internationally educated nurses seeking to practise in the UK.
The stages are: 1) Eligibility and application, 2) Test of Competence Part 1 (CBT), 3) Test of Competence Part 2 (OSCE), and 4) Registration and PIN issue.
The CBT (Computer-Based Test) is a multiple-choice theory exam taken at a Pearson VUE test centre. It tests your nursing knowledge, clinical decision-making, and understanding of UK nursing standards. It has 120 questions.
The CBT pass mark varies as it uses standardised scoring. You typically need to score around 68% to 70%. Results are provided immediately after the exam at the test centre.
The CBT costs £83 for the first attempt. Re-sits cost the same amount. You must pay when booking through the Pearson VUE website.
The OSCE (Objective Structured Clinical Examination) is a practical clinical exam with 10 stations testing your hands-on nursing skills. It is taken at an approved test centre in the UK after passing the CBT.
You can take the CBT a maximum of three times as part of a single application. If you fail three times, your application closes and you must start a new application after additional preparation.
You also have three attempts to pass the OSCE. If you fail three times, you must complete additional training before starting a new application.
You need: a valid passport, nursing degree certificate, nursing licence from your home country, verification from your regulator, employment history, character references, English language evidence, and a professional indemnity arrangement.
The full process typically takes 3 to 6 months from initial application to registration. This includes document verification, CBT booking and completion, travel to the UK, OSCE preparation and testing.
Total costs include: application assessment (£83), CBT (£83), OSCE (£794), plus travel, accommodation, and visa costs. Some NHS employers reimburse or cover many of these costs.
The NMC accepts IELTS Academic (7.0 in all sections, with 6.5 allowed in writing if others are 7.0) or OET (Grade B in all sub-tests). Results must be from a single sitting.
No, the NMC does not accept combined scores from multiple IELTS tests. You must achieve the required scores in a single sitting. The same applies to OET scores.
NMC Online is the secure portal where you manage your registration. You can apply for registration, update your details, pay your annual fee, and access your registration information through the portal.
A PIN (Personal Identification Number) is your unique registration number with the NMC. Once registered, you receive a PIN that employers use to verify you are on the register and eligible to practise.
You must pay the annual registration fee (currently £120), complete 35 hours of CPD every 3 years, obtain 5 feedback pieces, write 5 reflective accounts, and have professional indemnity insurance.
Revalidation is the process you complete every three years to renew your NMC registration. It demonstrates you remain fit to practise by meeting continuing professional development and practice hours requirements.
You cannot work as a registered nurse until you are on the NMC register. However, many work as healthcare assistants (HCAs) or in non-registered roles while completing their registration.
The NMC accepts nursing qualifications from most countries, but they must meet UK standards. The NMC will assess your qualification during the application process. Check the NMC website for the list of approved qualifications and countries.
