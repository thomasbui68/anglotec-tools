Midwifery in the United Kingdom

Midwifery in the UK is a respected, autonomous profession regulated by the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC). UK midwives are recognised worldwide for their high standards of care, and the NHS actively recruits international midwives to meet growing demand. The UK offers excellent career progression, competitive salaries, and the opportunity to work in diverse settings from large tertiary hospitals to community birth centres. This guide covers everything you need to know to register and practise as a midwife in the UK.

Registration Pathway Step by Step

1

NMC Registration Requirements for Midwives

To practise as a midwife in the UK, you must register with the NMC. The process is similar to nursing registration but with midwifery-specific assessments. International midwives must demonstrate their training and experience meet UK standards.

NMC Midwifery Registration Requirements

  • Recognised midwifery qualification (minimum 3 years or equivalent)
  • Registered/licensed as a midwife in your home country
  • English language: IELTS Band 7 in all sections OR OET Grade B
  • At least 12 months post-registration practice as a midwife
  • Computer-Based Test (CBT) - midwifery-specific questions
  • Objective Structured Clinical Examination (OSCE) - midwifery stations
  • Health and character declaration
  • Certificate of Good Standing from your home regulator

Important Note

Midwives must meet the NMC's specific midwifery standards. If your training included both nursing and midwifery, you may be eligible for dual registration, which significantly improves your employment prospects.

2

The Midwifery CBT (Computer-Based Test)

The CBT for midwives tests your theoretical knowledge across the scope of midwifery practice. It covers antenatal care, intrapartum care, postnatal care, neonatal care, and professional issues. The pass mark is typically set around 60-65%.

CBT Content Areas

  • Antenatal screening and assessment
  • Normal labour and birth management
  • Complicated labour and emergency procedures
  • Postnatal care of mother and baby
  • Newborn examination and screening
  • Medicines management in midwifery
  • Professional accountability and ethics
  • Mental health in maternity care

Preparation: Allow 2-3 months of study. Focus on UK-specific guidelines including NICE intrapartum care guidelines, NMC midwifery standards, and NHS antenatal/postnatal protocols.

3

The Midwifery OSCE

The NMC OSCE for midwives consists of 10 stations testing practical skills, clinical decision-making, and professional communication specific to midwifery. It is held at approved test centres in the UK.

OSCE Station Types

  • Antenatal assessment station: Taking a history, risk assessment, care planning
  • Labour care station: Managing normal labour, foetal monitoring
  • Postnatal examination: Maternal and newborn assessment
  • Newborn feeding assessment: Breastfeeding support and evaluation
  • Medicines management: Drug calculations and administration
  • Emergency scenario: Postpartum haemorrhage, shoulder dystocia
  • Professional discussion: Ethical scenarios, safeguarding
  • Documentation station: Record keeping and care plans

OSCE Tips

Practise all core midwifery skills repeatedly until they are automatic. The exam tests both your clinical competence and your ability to communicate compassionately under pressure. Use Anglotec's midwifery OSCE scenarios for realistic practice.

4

Preceptorship and Your First NHS Midwifery Role

All newly registered midwives in the UK complete a preceptorship programme, typically lasting 12 months. You are supported by an experienced preceptor midwife who guides your transition to autonomous UK practice.

Preceptorship Programme Components

  • Supervised clinical practice with gradual caseload building
  • Core skills consolidation
  • Reflective practice and portfolio development
  • Study days on UK-specific midwifery topics
  • Peer support and group learning
  • Competency sign-off for autonomous practice

Band 5 starting salary: GBP 29,000 - 36,000. Most NHS trusts offer rotational posts covering labour ward, antenatal clinic, postnatal ward, and community, giving you broad experience.

5

Career Progression for Midwives

UK midwifery offers diverse career pathways. Beyond clinical practice, you can specialise, move into management, education, research, or consultant midwife roles.

Clinical Specialist Pathways (Band 6-7)

Diabetes in pregnancy, bereavement care, fetal medicine, home birth coordination, neonatal intensive care liaison, perinatal mental health, safeguarding, contraception and sexual health.

Management Pathway (Band 7-8)

Ward manager, community midwifery manager, maternity matron, head of midwifery, director of midwifery. Requires leadership qualifications and significant experience.

Consultant Midwife (Band 8a-8b)

The pinnacle of clinical midwifery. Consultant midwives are expert clinical practitioners who also lead research, education, and service development. Requires MSc or PhD level education.

Education and Research

Midwife lecturers train student midwives at universities. Research midwives lead clinical trials and contribute to evidence-based practice. Both require additional postgraduate qualifications.

6

Independent Midwifery and Alternative Settings

While most UK midwives work in the NHS, other settings exist. Independent midwives offer private care to women choosing home birth. Some midwives work in birth centres, private hospitals, or the charitable sector. These roles often offer greater autonomy but may have different employment terms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I work as a midwife in the UK without UK training?

Yes, international midwives can register with the NMC by passing the CBT and OSCE. Your home country midwifery qualification must be recognised by the NMC, and you need 12 months of post-registration practice.

How is UK midwifery different from other countries?

UK midwives are autonomous practitioners who lead normal pregnancy and birth care independently. The UK model emphasises normality, informed choice, and continuity of carer. The NMC standards may differ from your home country's regulatory requirements.

What is the pass rate for the midwifery OSCE?

The midwifery OSCE has a first-attempt pass rate of approximately 65-70%. Candidates who prepare thoroughly with structured practice have significantly higher pass rates.

Do I need IELTS or OET for midwifery registration?

Yes, the NMC requires IELTS Band 7 in all sections or OET Grade B in all sub-tests. The scores must be from a single sitting (not combined across multiple tests).

Can I work as a maternity support worker while waiting for registration?

Yes, many international midwives work as Maternity Support Workers (MSW) while completing their NMC registration. This provides valuable UK maternity experience and income while you study for your exams.
