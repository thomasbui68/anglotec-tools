What is IELTS for UKVI?

IELTS for UKVI (UK Visas and Immigration) is a Secure English Language Test (SELT) approved by the UK Home Office for visa applications. While the test content and format are identical to standard IELTS, IELTS for UKVI is administered under stricter security conditions and your results are verified through a unique UKVI reference number.

IELTS for UKVI Academic

The most common version for students applying to UK universities and degree-level courses. Required for Student Route visa applications below degree level or when the university specifically requests UKVI IELTS.

  • Same test content as standard IELTS Academic
  • Accepted by all UK universities for admissions
  • Required for below-degree-level Student Route visas
  • Results sent electronically to UKVI
  • Secure test day procedures with ID verification
  • Test Report Form (TRF) includes UKVI number

IELTS for UKVI General Training

Used for immigration purposes such as Skilled Worker visas, family visas, and settlement applications. Not typically used for university admissions.

  • Slightly different content from Academic version
  • Emphasis on everyday English rather than academic
  • Required for certain visa categories
  • Lower score requirements than Academic for some visas
  • Same security protocols as UKVI Academic
  • Check your specific visa requirements before booking

IELTS Life Skills — Alternative for Specific Visas

IELTS Life Skills is a simplified test for specific UK visa categories only: Family visa (spouse/partner), Family visa (parent), and Indefinite Leave to Remain / British Citizenship. It tests only Speaking and Listening at CEFR levels A1, A2, or B1. It is NOT accepted for university admissions or Student Route visas. Most international students will need IELTS for UKVI Academic.

Which Should You Take?

If you're applying for a degree-level course (bachelor's, master's, PhD) at a licensed Student Route sponsor university, standard IELTS Academic is usually sufficient. Take IELTS for UKVI Academic if: you're applying for a below-degree-level course (foundation, pathway, English language), your university specifically requires UKVI IELTS, or you want the security of knowing your test is accepted for all UK visa purposes. When in doubt, take IELTS for UKVI Academic — it covers all scenarios.

How to Register for IELTS for UKVI

Booking your IELTS for UKVI test is straightforward. Follow these steps to ensure a smooth registration process.

1

Choose Your Test Type

Decide between IELTS for UKVI Academic, General Training, or Life Skills based on your visa requirements.

2

Find a Test Centre

Search for approved SELT test centres on the IELTS or British Council website. Not all IELTS centres offer UKVI tests.

3

Book Online

Register on the official IELTS website or through British Council/IDP. Upload a clear passport copy.

4

Pay the Fee

IELTS for UKVI costs approximately £200-£220 in the UK. Fees vary by country. Payment is usually online.

5

Prepare Documents

Bring your original valid passport/national ID on test day. No other ID is accepted for UKVI tests.

6

Take the Test

Arrive early with required ID. Listen, Reading, Writing on same day. Speaking may be same day or within 7 days.

Test Results Timeline

Paper-based IELTS: Results available 13 days after test. Computer-delivered IELTS: Results available 3-5 days after test. Your Test Report Form (TRF) will include a unique UKVI number that can be verified by the Home Office. Results are valid for 2 years from the test date.

IELTS Test Format Breakdown

Understanding the test format helps you prepare effectively and manage your time on test day.

Listening (30 minutes + 10 min transfer)

Four sections with 40 questions total. Each section is heard once only.

  • Section 1: Everyday social conversation (e.g., booking inquiry)
  • Section 2: Everyday social monologue (e.g., local facility information)
  • Section 3: Educational conversation (e.g., students discussing assignment)
  • Section 4: Academic monologue (e.g., university lecture)
  • Question types: Multiple choice, matching, plan/map/diagram labeling, form/note/table/flow-chart completion, sentence completion
  • Tip: Practice listening to various accents — British, Australian, American, Canadian

Reading (60 minutes)

Three long passages with 40 questions. Academic texts from books, journals, magazines, and newspapers.

  • Passage 1: Factual/descriptive text (e.g., history, biology)
  • Passage 2: Analytical/discursive text (e.g., research findings)
  • Passage 3: Complex argumentative text (e.g., opinion pieces)
  • Question types: Multiple choice, True/False/Not Given, matching headings, sentence completion, summary/note/table/flow-chart completion, diagram labeling, short answer
  • Tip: The True/False/Not Given format is the most challenging — practice extensively

Writing (60 minutes)

Two tasks that must both be completed. Task 2 carries more weight in scoring.

  • Task 1 (20 min, 150 words): Describe visual information — graph, chart, table, diagram, or process. No opinion required.
  • Task 2 (40 min, 250 words): Write an essay responding to a point of view, argument, or problem. Must give and justify your opinion.
  • Both tasks assessed on: Task achievement/response, Coherence and cohesion, Lexical resource, Grammatical range and accuracy
  • Tip: Practice writing under timed conditions. Task 2 is worth twice as much — allocate time accordingly

Speaking (11-14 minutes)

Face-to-face interview with a certified examiner. Recorded for quality assurance.

  • Part 1 (4-5 min): Introduction and general questions about yourself, family, work, studies, interests
  • Part 2 (3-4 min): Individual long turn — speak for 1-2 minutes on a given topic with 1 minute preparation
  • Part 3 (4-5 min): Two-way discussion — abstract questions related to Part 2 topic
  • Assessed on: Fluency and coherence, Lexical resource, Grammatical range and accuracy, Pronunciation
  • Tip: Don't memorize scripted answers — examiners can tell and will score you lower

One Skill Retake (OSR)

IELTS One Skill Retake allows you to retake just one section (Listening, Reading, Writing, or Speaking) if you didn't achieve your target score on your first attempt. Available in many countries at participating test centres. You must book the retake within 60 days of your original test. This can save you time and money compared to retaking the full test. Check availability at your local test centre.

IELTS Preparation Tips & Strategies

Effective preparation is the key to achieving your target IELTS score. Here are proven strategies for each section.

Listening Tips

  • Practice with BBC podcasts, TED talks, and academic lectures
  • Get familiar with different English accents (British, Australian, American)
  • Practice writing answers while listening — you only hear each section once
  • Pay attention to word limits in instructions (e.g., "NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS")
  • Check spelling and grammar — incorrect spelling loses marks
  • Use the 10-minute transfer time at the end to check answers
  • Predict answers before listening using context clues

Reading Tips

  • Master the True/False/Not Given question type — most candidates lose marks here
  • Skim passages first for main ideas, then scan for specific information
  • Don't spend too long on one question — move on and return later
  • Pay attention to synonyms — answers rarely use the exact same words as the passage
  • Practice timing — 60 minutes for 3 passages and 40 questions is tight
  • Read academic journals and newspapers regularly to build speed
  • Underline key information as you read

Writing Tips

  • Task 2 is worth double Task 1 — allocate 40 minutes to Task 2
  • Practice all common essay types: opinion, discussion, problem/solution, advantage/disadvantage
  • Learn to analyze and describe graphs, charts, and processes for Task 1
  • Use a range of vocabulary — avoid repeating the same words
  • Use complex sentence structures alongside simple ones
  • Plan your essay for 2-3 minutes before writing
  • Leave 2-3 minutes at the end to check for errors
  • Write at least 150 words for Task 1 and 250 words for Task 2

Speaking Tips

  • Practice speaking English daily — with friends, in mirror, or record yourself
  • Don't memorize answers — examiners can tell and penalize you
  • Use the 1-minute preparation time in Part 2 to make notes
  • Extend your answers — don't just give one-word responses
  • Use discourse markers ("however," "furthermore," "on the other hand")
  • It's okay to ask the examiner to repeat a question
  • Speak naturally — the examiner is testing communication, not perfection
  • Dress comfortably and be polite — first impressions matter

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between IELTS and IELTS for UKVI?

The test content, format, timing, and scoring are identical. The only differences are: IELTS for UKVI is taken under enhanced security conditions (video recording, stricter ID verification), results include a unique UKVI reference number verifiable by the Home Office, and it costs slightly more (£200-£220 vs £175-£195). Both are accepted by all UK universities.

Do I need IELTS for UKVI for a Student Route visa?

For degree-level courses (bachelor's, master's, PhD) at licensed Student Route sponsors, standard IELTS Academic is usually sufficient. However, for below-degree-level courses (foundation, pathway, pre-sessional English), you MUST take a SELT such as IELTS for UKVI Academic. Many students choose to take UKVI IELTS regardless to ensure visa compliance.

How much does IELTS for UKVI cost?

In the UK, IELTS for UKVI costs approximately £200-£220. Prices vary by country — in India it's approximately ₹17,000, in Nigeria around ₦350,000, in Pakistan around PKR 55,000. Check the official IELTS website for current pricing in your country.

How long are IELTS for UKVI results valid?

IELTS results are valid for 2 years from the test date. However, some UK universities and the Home Office may have specific requirements about how recent your test must be. Always check with your university or visa category for any time limits.

Can I retake just one section of IELTS?

Yes, IELTS One Skill Retake (OSR) is available in many countries. If you didn't achieve your target score in one section, you can retake just that section (Listening, Reading, Writing, or Speaking) within 60 days of your original test. This is available for both standard IELTS and IELTS for UKVI at participating test centres.

What IELTS score do I need for a UK nursing visa?

For NMC registration and the Health and Care Worker visa, you need IELTS Academic score of 7.0 in all four bands (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking). Alternatively, you can take the OET Nursing test with Grade B in all sections. Some nurses find OET easier as it uses healthcare-specific scenarios.

Can I use IELTS for UKVI for university applications outside the UK?

Yes, IELTS for UKVI Academic is accepted by universities worldwide, not just in the UK. Since the test content is identical to standard IELTS Academic, institutions in Australia, Canada, New Zealand, the US, and elsewhere will accept it. Always verify with your target institution.
