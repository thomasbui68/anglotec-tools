NHS Pay Guide

NHS Banding System Explained for International Nurses

Understand the NHS Agenda for Change pay bands from Band 1 to Band 9. Learn which band you will enter as an overseas nurse, what your salary will be, and how to progress through the bands.

Calculate Your NHS Salary

Quick Answer

International nurses entering the NHS for the first time typically start at Band 5 with a starting salary of approximately GBP 30,000 - 32,000 per year (2025/26 rates). With experience and specialist skills, you can progress to Band 6 (GBP 37,000 - 40,000) within 2-3 years. Senior nurses and team leaders work at Band 7 (GBP 47,000 - 52,000), while advanced practitioners and nurse consultants can reach Bands 8 and 9.

What is the NHS Agenda for Change?

The Agenda for Change (AfC) is the NHS pay system that covers all staff except doctors, dentists, and senior managers. Introduced in 2004, it replaced many different pay structures with a single system of 9 pay bands. Each band has multiple pay points, and staff progress through these points based on years of service, subject to satisfactory performance.

NHS Pay Bands 1-9: Complete Breakdown (2025/26)

Here are the current NHS pay scales for each band. Figures shown are annual gross salaries for full-time positions (37.5 hours/week) in England. Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland have slightly different rates.

Band 1

Domestic / Support Services
Entry Salary: GBP 24,927
Top Salary: GBP 24,927
Pay Points: 1

Band 1 roles include domestic assistants, housekeeping, and basic support services. These roles require no formal qualifications. Rarely relevant for nurses.

Band 2

Healthcare Assistants / Support Workers
Entry Salary: GBP 24,927
Top Salary: GBP 26,351
Pay Points: 2

Band 2 includes healthcare assistants (HCAs), porters, catering staff, and administration. HCAs work under nurse supervision providing basic patient care. Some international nurses take HCA roles while completing their NMC registration.

Band 3

Senior Healthcare Assistants / Therapy Assistants
Entry Salary: GBP 26,351
Top Salary: GBP 28,407
Pay Points: 3

Band 3 roles include senior HCAs, therapy assistants, and clinical support workers. These roles require some vocational qualifications and involve more responsibility than Band 2.

Band 4

Assistant Practitioners / Pharmacy Technicians
Entry Salary: GBP 28,407
Top Salary: GBP 31,552
Pay Points: 3

Band 4 includes assistant practitioners, pharmacy technicians, and some nursing associate roles. These are skilled technical roles that do not require full professional registration. Nursing associates (a newer role) sit at this band.

Band 5 - Newly Qualified / Registered Nurses

Most Common Entry Point for International Nurses
Entry Salary: GBP 31,552
Top Salary: GBP 34,581
Pay Points: 2-3

This is where most international nurses start. Band 5 includes newly qualified nurses, staff nurses, and some specialist nurses. You will work as a staff nurse on a ward or unit, managing your own caseload under the supervision of Band 6 and 7 colleagues. Progression to the top of Band 5 takes approximately 2 years. Many NHS employers recruit international nurses directly into Band 5 with an OSCE training package.

Band 6 - Senior Nurses / Team Leaders

Progression Target Within 2-4 Years
Entry Salary: GBP 37,350
Top Salary: GBP 40,462
Pay Points: 2-3

Band 6 includes senior staff nurses, deputy ward managers, health visitors, school nurses, and some specialist nurses. At this level, you will have additional responsibilities such as mentoring junior staff, leading shifts, or managing a clinical area. International nurses with 2+ years of post-registration experience may be able to negotiate Band 6 entry.

Band 7 - Advanced Nurses / Ward Managers

Senior Clinical and Leadership Roles
Entry Salary: GBP 47,148
Top Salary: GBP 52,809
Pay Points: 2-3

Band 7 includes ward managers, senior specialist nurses, practice development nurses, and some advanced practitioner roles. These are leadership positions requiring significant experience, often a master's degree, and advanced clinical skills. International nurses with 5+ years of specialist experience (e.g., ICU, theatre, emergency) may qualify for Band 7.

Band 8a-8d - Consultant / Director Level

Advanced Practice and Senior Management
Band 8a: GBP 54,619 - 61,309
Band 8b: GBP 63,530 - 72,840
Band 8c: GBP 75,915 - 87,754
Band 8d: GBP 91,072 - 105,854

Band 8 roles include modern matrons, nurse consultants, clinical directors, and senior operational managers. These are highly experienced roles requiring advanced qualifications, significant leadership experience, and specialist clinical expertise. Nurse consultants at Band 8a-8c work at the highest level of clinical practice.

Band 9

Most Senior Clinical and Executive Roles
Entry Salary: GBP 111,221
Top Salary: GBP 129,834
Pay Points: 5

Band 9 is the highest pay band in the NHS. Roles include the most senior nurse consultants and directors of nursing. These are exceptional positions held by a small number of nursing leaders with decades of experience and significant strategic influence.

* Figures are approximate and based on 2025/26 England NHS Agenda for Change rates. London weighting (High Cost Area Supplement) adds 5-20% for staff in London and surrounding areas. Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland have separate pay scales.

Where Do International Nurses Enter the NHS?

Most international nurses enter at Band 5, but your entry band depends on your experience, qualifications, and the recruiting trust's policies:

Band 5 Entry (Most Common)

You will likely enter at Band 5 if:

  • - You are a newly qualified nurse (less than 2 years of post-registration experience)
  • - This is your first nursing role after passing the NMC OSCE
  • - Your overseas experience is not in a specialist area
  • - The employer has a standard international nurse recruitment program

Salary: GBP 31,552 - 34,581 per year. Most employers offer an OSCE training package, accommodation support, and flight reimbursement.

Band 6 Entry (Experienced Nurses)

You may negotiate Band 6 entry if:

  • - You have 2+ years of post-registration experience
  • - You have specialist experience (ICU, ED, theatre, oncology, paediatrics)
  • - You hold specialist certifications from your home country
  • - You have leadership or teaching experience

Salary: GBP 37,350 - 40,462 per year. Band 6 entry often requires a formal interview and evidence of your specialist skills.

Band 7 Entry (Highly Experienced)

Band 7 entry is possible if:

  • - You have 5+ years of specialist experience
  • - You hold a master's degree in nursing or a related field
  • - You have significant leadership experience (charge nurse, team leader)
  • - You are recruiting for a specific senior role

Salary: GBP 47,148 - 52,809 per year. These roles are competitive and typically recruited through specialist international recruitment campaigns.

Nursing Associate (Band 4)

If you do not yet have full NMC registration but want to start working in the NHS:

  • - Nursing associate roles are at Band 4
  • - You work under nurse supervision
  • - This can be a pathway to registered nurse status
  • - Some employers sponsor nursing associates to complete registered nurse training

Salary: GBP 28,407 - 31,552 per year. Not all trusts offer this pathway.

Additional NHS Benefits Beyond Salary

The NHS offers substantial benefits that significantly increase the total value of your compensation package:

🌟 Pension Scheme

The NHS Pension is one of the most generous in the UK. Both you and your employer contribute (employer contribution is approximately 20% of your salary). It provides a guaranteed income in retirement based on your career earnings.

🏖 Annual Leave

Start with 27 days paid leave plus 8 bank holidays (35 days total). This increases to 29 days after 5 years and 33 days after 10 years of service. Many trusts also offer flexible working and the option to buy additional leave.

🏥 Sick Pay

NHS sick pay is generous: full pay for 6 months and half pay for 6 months during any 12-month period (after 12 months of service). During your first year, you receive 1 month full pay and 2 months half pay.

💰 Unsocial Hours Pay

Working evenings, nights, weekends, and bank holidays attracts additional pay. The enhancement ranges from time + 30% (evenings) to time + 60% (Saturday nights) and time + 94% (Sunday nights), significantly boosting your earnings.

🎓 Training and Development

The NHS invests heavily in staff development. You will have access to mandatory training, specialist courses, mentorship programs, and funding for postgraduate qualifications. Many trusts pay for your NMC registration and OSCE costs.

🚗 Relocation Support

Most NHS trusts recruiting internationally offer: flight reimbursement (GBP 500-1,000), initial accommodation (2-8 weeks), OSCE training and exam fee coverage, NMC registration fee reimbursement, and baggage allowance.

How to Progress Through NHS Bands

Career progression in the NHS is structured and transparent. Here is how international nurses typically move up the bands:

Band 5 to 6 Timeline: 2-3 years

Complete your preceptorship year. Gain experience in your clinical area. Take on additional responsibilities (mentoring students, quality improvement projects). Apply for Band 6 roles when advertised internally. Some trusts have automatic progression to the top of Band 5 after 2 years of satisfactory performance.

Band 6 to 7 Timeline: 3-5 years

Develop specialist clinical skills. Consider postgraduate study (PGCert, PgDip, or Master's). Take on leadership roles (shift leader, deputy ward manager). Build a portfolio of evidence including audits, research, teaching, and service improvement. Band 7 roles often require a formal interview process.

Band 7 to 8a+ Timeline: 5+ years

Pursue advanced practice qualifications (e.g., Advanced Nurse Practitioner). Complete a master's degree or doctorate. Gain significant leadership and strategic experience. Publish research and present at conferences. Band 8 roles are competitive and often require a formal assessment centre process.
