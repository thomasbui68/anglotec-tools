Why Study English in the UK?

Learning English in the UK — the birthplace of the language — offers an immersive experience that accelerates your learning dramatically. With hundreds of accredited language schools offering courses at every price point, there's an affordable option for every budget.

Benefits of Learning English in UK

  • Total Immersion: Surrounded by native speakers in daily life — shops, transport, media, social situations
  • Accredited Schools: British Council accreditation ensures quality standards
  • Short Student Visa: Study up to 11 months on a Short-term Study visa (no CAS required for under 6 months)
  • Gateway to Further Study: Improve English before starting a university degree
  • Career Advantage: UK-based English certificates valued by employers globally
  • Cultural Experience: Explore British history, culture, and diversity
  • Networking: Meet students from around the world

Types of English Courses Available

  • General English: Everyday communication skills — speaking, listening, reading, writing
  • Intensive English: 25-30 hours/week for faster progress
  • IELTS Preparation: Targeted training for IELTS Academic or UKVI exam
  • Cambridge Exam Prep: FCE, CAE, CPE preparation courses
  • Business English: Professional communication in workplace contexts
  • Academic English: Pre-sessional courses for university preparation
  • English + Activities: Combine learning with cultural excursions
  • One-to-One Lessons: Private tuition at premium rates

Student Visa Options for English Courses

Short-term Study Visa: For courses 6-11 months, costs £200, no IHS required, cannot work. Standard Student Visa: For courses at licensed institutions, can work 10 hours/week (language centers) or 20 hours/week (degree-level), requires CAS from an accredited provider. Many students start on a Short-term visa and extend or switch if they progress to further study.

Tips to Save Money on English Courses

Book longer courses for better weekly rates — many schools offer 10-20% discounts for 12+ week bookings. Study outside London where accommodation and living costs are 30-40% lower. Look for schools offering free social programs to reduce entertainment costs. Consider homestay accommodation which often includes meals. Some schools offer "low season" discounts during winter months. Apply early bird discounts (typically 5-10% off when booking 3+ months in advance). Share accommodation with other international students to split costs.

Visa Requirements for English Language Students

Understanding your visa options helps you choose the right course duration and plan your stay legally.

Short-term Study Visa (6-11 months)

  • For English language courses only (6-11 months duration)
  • Visa fee: £200
  • No Immigration Health Surcharge
  • Cannot work (paid or voluntary)
  • Cannot switch to another visa category in UK
  • Must leave UK when course ends
  • Requires: CAS from accredited provider, proof of funds, valid passport
  • Must be 16 years or older

Standard Student Visa

  • For courses at licensed Student Route sponsor institutions
  • Visa fee: £490
  • IHS: £776/year
  • Can work 10 hrs/week (language centres) or 20 hrs (degree)
  • Can switch to Graduate Route or other visas from within UK
  • Can bring dependents for degree-level courses
  • Requires: CAS, proof of funds, English proof, TB test (if applicable)

Visitor Visa for Short Courses (Under 6 months)

If your English course is less than 6 months, you can study on a Standard Visitor visa. This is free for many nationalities, allows tourism alongside study, but prohibits work. You cannot extend this visa or switch to another category from within the UK. Ideal for short summer courses or taster programs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest English course in the UK?

The cheapest accredited English courses in the UK start from around £150 per week for part-time General English (15 hours/week) outside London. Schools in Sheffield, Newcastle, and Liverpool typically offer the lowest rates. For full-time intensive courses (25 hours/week), expect to pay from £190-£210 per week at budget schools.

Can I work while studying English in the UK?

On a Short-term Study visa (6-11 months), you cannot work at all. On a Standard Student visa studying at a language school, you can work up to 10 hours per week during term time. If your English course is part of a degree preparation pathway at a university, you may be able to work 20 hours per week.

Do I need a visa to study English in the UK?

If your course is under 6 months and you're from a visa-national country, you need a Standard Visitor visa. For courses 6-11 months, you need a Short-term Study visa. For longer courses at licensed institutions, you need a Standard Student visa. EU/EEA/Swiss nationals can study up to 6 months without a visa.

How much does accommodation cost for language students?

Outside London, shared student accommodation costs £120-£180 per week. Homestay (living with a British family) costs £140-£200 per week and often includes meals. In London, expect to pay £180-£280 per week for shared accommodation. University halls are rarely available for language-only students.

Can I get a student visa for an English language course?

Yes, the Short-term Study visa is specifically designed for English language courses lasting 6-11 months. For courses at licensed Student Route sponsors, you can obtain a Standard Student visa. The school must be accredited by the British Council or an approved body to issue the necessary documentation.

Are cheap English courses in UK good quality?

Yes, if the school is British Council accredited. Accreditation ensures the school meets strict standards for teaching, facilities, welfare, and administration. Cheap does not mean low quality — many affordable schools offer excellent teaching with smaller class sizes and more personal attention than expensive schools in prime locations.

How long does it take to improve my English level in the UK?

Most students improve by approximately half an IELTS band or one CEFR level per 10-12 weeks of intensive study. A beginner (A1) can reach intermediate (B1) in 20-30 weeks of intensive study. An intermediate student (B1) can reach upper-intermediate (B2) in 12-20 weeks. Individual progress varies based on starting level, intensity of study, and amount of practice outside class.
