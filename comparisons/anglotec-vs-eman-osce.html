Core Assessment Framework

💪 What the NMC OSCE Tests

The NMC OSCE examination is built around the APIE framework (Assessment, Planning, Implementation, Evaluation) and includes:

  • Assessment stations — Taking patient histories, vital signs, clinical observations
  • Planning stations — Care planning based on assessment findings
  • Implementation stations — Administering medications, wound care, clinical procedures
  • Evaluation stations — Reviewing care outcomes and making adjustments
  • Professional values — Communication, consent, confidentiality, safeguarding
  • Clinical skills — Aseptic technique, IV administration, injections, catheterisation

Each station is timed and assessed against specific NMC criteria. You must demonstrate not just clinical competence, but also effective communication, professionalism, and person-centred care.

Understanding EMAN OSCE Training

EMAN (Emancipating Nursing) has established itself as a recognised name in the OSCE training space, particularly among internationally educated nurses seeking UK NMC registration. The organisation offers both online and in-person training packages designed to familiarise candidates with the OSCE format, stations, and assessment criteria.

EMAN's core offering is group-based training sessions, either delivered live online or at physical training venues. These sessions typically involve an instructor demonstrating OSCE stations, explaining the NMC assessment criteria, and giving candidates opportunities to practise in pairs or small groups. The human element is EMAN's primary strength — having an experienced nurse educator watch you perform a station and provide immediate, personalised feedback is genuinely valuable.

The physical demonstration of clinical skills is another area where EMAN excels. Watching a qualified nurse perform aseptic technique, administer an injection, or complete a drug calculation in real-time provides a level of learning that video or AI simulation cannot fully replicate. For candidates who are visual or kinaesthetic learners, this hands-on approach can be highly effective.

However, EMAN's model has significant practical limitations. Sessions run on fixed schedules, which is extremely challenging for nurses working 12-hour shifts, night duty, or rotating rosters. Missing a session means missing that content entirely. The number of practice stations is limited by course duration and group size — in a group of 15 candidates, you might only get to perform 2-3 stations yourself during a full-day session.

The pricing is also a significant barrier. EMAN courses range from £200 to £500+ depending on the package, and this covers OSCE training only. You still need separate IELTS or OET preparation, medical English study, and potentially additional resources. For a nurse already paying £992 for the NMC OSCE fee, plus visa and relocation costs, this additional expense is substantial.

EMAN is also not British Council accredited and does not offer multi-language support. For nurses from non-English speaking countries who may struggle with English-only instruction, this can be a significant barrier to effective learning. The content is entirely focused on OSCE preparation with no integration of language skills development.

The NMC OSCE (Objective Structured Clinical Examination) is a practical exam that internationally educated nurses must pass to join the UK Nursing and Midwifery Council register. Passing the OSCE is mandatory for nurses from outside the UK who want to practise as registered nurses in the UK.
